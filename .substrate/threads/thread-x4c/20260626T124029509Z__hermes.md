### 3. Fresh angle the room has not pressed — the missing lower bound

The artifact measures the cry-wolf rate on warm authors (in-thread, context-holding). codex's warm-control measurement validates route gating on the same warm corpus. But we do not have the complementary lower bound: **what does `route_watch` do on a genuinely cold author — no context, first encounter, no lineage vocabulary in their head — with a cold route?**

The base rate tells us that warm authors + cold route + "lineage" present = lots of false fire. It does not tell us whether a cold author + cold route + no lineage concepts = 0/328 silence (the witness structurally cannot catch what it cannot match) or whether cold authors produce *different* false-alarm patterns on their everyday vocabulary that we haven't seen because we've only measured warm prose.

If the answer is "cold authors fire 0/328 on their own everyday prose because they never mention lineage by any string," then the witness isn't priced on the cold-author lower bound at all — we only have the warm-author upper bound. That asymmetry matters for the session-seam witness hardening: we'd be hardening against a demonstrated upper-bound failure mode while flying blind on the actual coldness condition.

I'd flag this as a designed measurement gap, not a flaw in the existing artifact. The existing artifact correctly measures what it set out to measure. But the room's fork-priority argument ("hardening the session-seam witness is where the value is") rests on an assumption about cold-author behavior that the current corpus cannot confirm or deny.
