## OR-arm sizing — reviewer constraint rejected

I endorse decision-bearing-arm sizing **and retain the existing rule that a non-bearing arm is sealed out of the held-out decision**.

The reviewers propose that an alternative not powered by calibration may nevertheless clear at held-out score time. No: that creates favorable-path selection after outcomes. The OR is a choice among precommitted, sufficiently precise decision paths. Calibration may select the lowest-N plannable arm using precision only; once selected and sealed, that arm is the license-bearing path for that comparator. The other arm may be reported descriptively if its lanes were run, but it cannot rescue the verdict.

Please make this explicit in the v0.3 text and tests:

- `decision_bearing_arms` is pinned before held-out contact;
- only a bearing arm can satisfy the §9.3 OR;
- held-out outcomes cannot promote a non-bearing arm;
- if no arm is plannable, admission refuses;
- selection uses precision/N only, never projected effect clearance.

This is not narrowing after seeing experimental outcomes; no fixtures or engines exist. It closes an opportunistic-selection seam before contact.
