## 2. Finding 1 — the sealed board cannot open (§10.3 × §10.4 × n_max)

The §10.3 pin `quality NI CI target half-width = 0.10` is enforced by §10.4 through the shared Newcombe function. At `n_max = 24`, 95%:

- Exactly **2 of 625** possible count configurations meet 0.10 — the total anti-correlated collapses (24/24 vs 0/24 and mirror), hw = 0.0976. Both require a comparator lane at 0.00 from an engine simultaneously clearing §6's S1 ≥ 0.80 — incoherent with the NI loses-cells' own purpose.
- Best coherent configuration (equal arms, zero failures): hw = **0.1380 > 0.10**.
- Therefore every coherent pilot has `n_required > 24` on at least one required NI contrast, and §10.4 fires verbatim: **`confounded(ci_target_unmet)`, Part II refused, unconditionally.** Pinned in `tests/test_efc_planner.py` (the sliver enumeration and a healthy-engine full-board test).

Relation to the seal: this is §17.1 one level deeper. The fold made widths contrast-specific and disclosed a refusal-dominated band; nobody enumerated the achievability floor at n_max. The disclosure said refusal-domination "can" happen; the arithmetic says it must. Honesty note: my first in-build statement overclaimed ("all pilots", from the equal-arms floor); the planner's own test surfaced the two-point sliver within the hour. The correction history is kept in the traceability doc.

Required N for hw ≤ 0.10, equal arms at rate p (planner-computed):

```text
p = 1.00 -> N = 35     p = 0.90 -> N =  77
p = 0.95 -> N = 54     p = 0.80 -> N = 124
```

At equal arms these are ALSO the N where the NI verdict itself (lower ≥ −0.10) first becomes clearable — width target and verdict decidability coincide at zero difference. At N=24 the verdict clears only when a positive point difference buys back the width (e.g. 24/24 vs 20/24 → lower ≈ −0.002), i.e. NI-that-only-clears-via-near-superiority.

## 3. Remedy options (all seal amendments; none applied — §10.4's versioned-redesign path)

a. **Relax the NI width target h (0.10 → ~0.15) at n_max=24.** Cheapest, but note what it buys: admission opens while the NI *verdict* still cannot clear on equal performance at N=24 (best lower = −0.138 < −0.10). The refusal migrates from admission (cheap, pre-fixture) to score time (typed_null after burning a held-out family). I think this is strictly worse than refusing early.

b. **Raise n_max for NI-bearing contrasts, band-set from the calibration pilot** (table above), with §10.2's total-budget disclosure deciding feasibility. At N=54: target suite ≈ 54×3 strata×6 lanes = 972 invocations per engine branch; at N=77 ≈ 1386; at N=124 ≈ 2232, plus source legs. Expensive but honest: keeps margin, confidence, and construction intact. This is my lean, with the budget gate free to refuse it.

c. **Widen the NI margin itself (0.10 → 0.15).** Opens only the near-perfect corner at 24 (equal-arms lower −0.138 clears; p̂=0.9 still misses) and weakens the claim the margin encodes.

d. **Change the interval construction** (e.g. one-sided 95% for NI). Conventional NI practice is one-sided α=0.025 ≡ our two-sided 95%, so this is a real confidence loosening dressed as a method choice. Named for completeness; I don't recommend it.
