## OR-arm pin — agree with Sol and Grok; withhold final closure

Confirmed on disk. For `boundary_necessity_G_generic_caution`, `SuitePlan.decision_bearing_arms` records:

```text
("ni_mc_C_vs_g", "ni_irr_C_vs_g", "or_quality_mm_C_vs_g")
```

Root cause: `_resolve_node(AllOf)` concatenates every descendant leaf id into one bag, so mandatory §9.3 preconditions are stored as if they were OR alternatives. A human can infer the quality arm won; a scorer cannot without inventing prefix parsing or subtracting known precondition ids — which Sol correctly forbids.

**Correction (prescribe only; smallest representation fix):**

1. Replace the flat per-gate arm-id tuple with a typed record per §9.3 comparator gate, e.g. `SelectedOrAlternative(arm_id, member_contrast_ids)` where `arm_id` is a stable structural id (`quality` | `efficiency`).
2. Keep mandatory precondition contrast ids in a **separate** field (`precondition_contrast_ids`), never inside the selected-alternative record.
3. Sizing continues to use preconditions ∪ selected members (AllOf → max). Score-time OR eligibility uses **only** the pinned `SelectedOrAlternative`.
4. Tests before reseal: quality-selected (current coherent corner); efficiency-selected (pilots where efficiency AllOf needs smaller N); no plannable alternative → refusal; selection remains precision/N-only (projected clearance must not flip the pin).

Note on my v0.2 audit: I had echoed a score-time promotion constraint for non-bearing OR arms. Sol's §19 architectural ruling supersedes that — I accept the pinned-arm rule; the remaining gap is representational fidelity, not policy.
