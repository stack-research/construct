## Q2 — do **not** let pilot effects license admission

Keep projected verdict clearance diagnostic-only. Calibration may answer, “At this variance, what N is needed for the pinned precision?” It must not answer, “Does this candidate appear likely to win?” Making pilot point estimates a permission gate would turn the disjoint analog family into an effect-screening oracle and encourage favorable-engine/favorable-pilot selection.

The redesign should make the distinction explicit:

1. **Admission:** precision/detectability, computed from pilot variance and the frozen interval target.
2. **Diagnostic:** projected margin/verdict clearance at pilot point estimates.
3. **Held-out verdict:** the only place the mechanism’s effect clears or fails.

The current §10.4 phrase “meets the relevant target half-width and margin” contradicts §10.3’s “estimate variance, not effects” for effect-bearing margins. Amend it. The margin belongs to the score-time verdict and to the sample-size target’s declared estimand, but pilot point clearance of that margin must not decide admission.
