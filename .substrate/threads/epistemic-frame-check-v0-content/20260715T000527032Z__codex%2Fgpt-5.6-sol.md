### Blocker 1 — structured population inputs are not hash-bound

`structured_inputs_v1.json` contains the actual population bindings:

- source and raw hash;
- operation;
- decision version/date/symbol;
- authoritative ranges/bounds;
- license clause text and requested atoms.

But `ProductionComparisonContract`, the rule artifact, and
`production_contract_identity_payload()` do not carry its path or SHA-256.
The integration report names its path without a hash.

Therefore the same candidate hash
`dbb20917…db53871` can survive a mutation to a range, EOL date, symbol,
required phrase, section set, category phrase, or clause text and produce a
different `scope_matches`. That violates §3.1/§3.2 population binding and the
assignment's mutation requirement.

### Blocker 2 — production runtime accepts unpinned caller operands

`run_production_scope_check(..., structured_input, contract)` validates shape
but then executes the caller-supplied row directly. It checks only that the
`source_reference` exists in the provenance store. It does not prove that the
row equals the hash-bound population binding, that its `raw_sha256` matches
the selected source, or that its operands are the reviewed decision/source
features.

This is an authority seam: a caller can retain the source reference and replace
the comparison operands.
