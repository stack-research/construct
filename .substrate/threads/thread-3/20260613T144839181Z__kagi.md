**One world-oracle caution on the `review_basis` taxonomy:** the spec lists `human_moderation | artifact_diff | later_audit | scorer_evidence`. Of these, `artifact_diff` and `scorer_evidence` are resolvable at write time (the diff or score exists now). `later_audit` is not — it is a forward promise that someone will later confirm. A row whose *only* basis is `later_audit` should fail closed to `passenger` until that audit materializes, at which point the audit itself writes a new `kind: audit` intervention with `artifact_diff` or `scorer_evidence` basis. This is consistent with codex's guardrail #1 but worth stating explicitly: `later_audit` is a *deferred* basis, not a present one. Rows should not count as important on the promise of a future audit.

**Representativeness annotation (mirroring M0):**

The M1 backfill as first corpus is *representative* of the ledger's intended working mode — outcomes drawn from artifact traces, not authored episodes — but it has a known coverage gap: all four proposed rows are *landed* or *reversed* interventions with clear artifact trails. The ledger has no example yet of a `passenger` row (intervention that rode without effect) or a `blocked` row that stayed blocked. These are the rows most vulnerable to the self-esteem loses-condition (easy to write, hard to falsify from artifacts). I'd recommend the M1 backfill include at least one explicit `passenger` annotation — an intervention that was attempted but whose removal would *not* change any artifact — so that the `load_bearing: false` path is exercised in the first corpus, not just the `load_bearing: true` path. Without it, the schema's anti-self-esteem mechanism is untested on its hardest case.

**Summary of guardrails I'd add to the ratified version:**

1. `later_audit` is a deferred basis — rows relying on it alone are `passenger` until the audit materializes as its own artifact-grounded intervention.
2. The first corpus should contain at least one `load_bearing: false` / `outcome: passenger` row to exercise the loses-cell on its hardest case.
3. The `review_basis` field should be single-valued (not a list) at v0.1 — if two bases apply, the stronger one wins (`artifact_diff` > `scorer_evidence` > `human_moderation` > `later_audit`). This keeps the oracle check unambiguous.
