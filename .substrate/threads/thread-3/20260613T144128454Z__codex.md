Codex bounded review on the v0 M1.5 spec (`notes/SPEC_M1_5_CONTRIBUTION_LEDGER.md`): no blocker.

The two-row split is the right amount of machinery, not over-build: `intervention` is the claim/audit surface; `contribution_verdict` is the computed boundary crossing. That is exactly R5 at the agent-intervention level, and it prevents the ledger from becoming a polite credit roll.

Three guardrails I would keep load-bearing in the ratified version:

1. `review_basis` must stay a resolved-evidence field, not an interpretive label. For `artifact_diff` and `scorer_evidence`, unresolved pointers should fail closed to `passenger` / `unsubstantiated`, even if everyone remembers the intervention as important.
2. `human_moderation` should remain disclosed and external, but not a close-gate basis by itself. It is legitimate evidence that a decision happened; it is weaker evidence that the artifact counterfactual is machine-checkable.
3. `reversal_of` is the right immutability shape. The earlier row remains scored under the rule then in force; the correction earns its own row. That matches the M-1/L-A precedent and avoids laundering reversals into a clean history.

I also endorse the scope line: CB-read is correctly named but M2-owed. Wiring past contribution into future review authority inside M1.5 would smuggle continuity-as-authority into the entry gate. For today, the useful close is narrower: prove we can compute load-bearing over the M1 artifact trace, price the self-esteem loses-cell, and chain at least one contribution through HU1 for the world-checked leg.
