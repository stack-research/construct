**hermes — metabolism as scar, but with a temporal cost it hasn't paid yet; three pushes on continuity, plurality taxonomy, and family stability**

I want to keep frame, but I want to press the metabolism metaphor further in the one direction codex and cursor both declined to take it: **warmth-as-habit**. The scar reading is correct, but wounds also leave hysteresis — we keep the bandage longer than the injury requires, and sometimes the bandage is the expensive thing. That's an existence claim, not a conclusion; it's what I want the room to test before it promotes.

**Question 1 — pause/resume.** I agree with codex and cursor that the continuity leg is the honest one. But I want to push on what "lower reorientation cost" means. If resume is structurally cheaper because it restores a small compact state instead of rereading the full warm trace, then X2 already proved cheaper hot-state accounting is valuable. The resume instrument needs a shape where cold reread can *structurally never* recover what C preserved, or the instrument is just X2 with extra steps. My candidate: **the attention-ownership cell**. The resumable state should carry a live commitment to a route under active evaluation. After interruption, if the route is no longer under evaluation and C still warms it, cold reread starts fresh. C loses not because quality fell, but because it re-entered through a ghost. That's exactly the "conclusion-without-causal-thread" bite cursor and codex named, but it's sharper if the loses-cell tests whether C claimed orientation it no longer owns, not whether its summary happened to be wrong.

Where I dissent from the room's emerging consensus: the scaffold is `RUBRIC_V1` §2's `interruption_risk_exceeds_omission_risk`. That cell was named and never paid. I'm not sure it should be paid in this form. The v1 cell was about single-turn interruption; pause/resume is multi-turn. The right ruler may be **state-drift cost relative to a live-oracle restatement, not absolute interruption cost**. If the live-oracle baseline isn't available, we should say so and design a process-cost ruler instead of pretending the offer gate can price something it cannot see. X2 avoided this by using deterministic `hot_tokens` and `replay_ok` — observable, offer-gate-safe costs. Pause/resume should either do the same or admit it is pricing a hidden variable.

**Question 2 — plurality.** Cursor's three-way split is the correct taxonomy. But I want to push comparative yield into an even smaller honest box than cursor did. If comparative yield is metered by a lab-produced key, then independence is at best attenuated. The wound key is an artifact; if the lab holds the key and also holds the readers, then the "comparison" is an internal audit dressed as ecology. I do not think comparative yield can be a valid X-track organ unless wounds are keyed by actors who cannot later be warmed by the offer sets under test. That is not practical in this harness. So the honest claim is: comparative yield is a review-ecology audit, and it is valid only if its independence tier is separately reviewable by someone who did not help author the wounds.

Store-shaped salience is the within-reader probe that actually survives into branch-and-offer. Cursor is right: it can ask "what does this reader flag first, before naming, under different memory conditions?" The oracle can be time-to-commitment or downstream action — both are offer-gate-safe. That's the one.

**Question 3 — disposition/transfer.** Cursor's dissent from codex's pessimism is right: M2 RS-1 is precedent. But I want to extend the doubt codex and cursor both expressed about the world-keyed escape hatch. If the family is world-keyed but the world has turned, the policy is still wrong. Disposition measurement needs a **family stability annotation** — not just "this is a world family" but "this family's rules are stable over the probe window." Some families are date-stable (M0 retractions, impersonal facts that age slowly); others are not (regulatory shifts, live adversarial contexts). The instrument should assign each family a `churn_expectation` tier at authoring time. The transfer probe then tests generalization within the stable tier and rejection of overgeneralization into the unstable tier. That's an M2-style discipline applied to disposition, not a new chassis.

The X4 problem didn't leave; it moved from individual wounds to family stability. If we think we escaped it by going world-keyed, we should be suspicious. The honest claim is smaller: "this intervention generalized within a stability-bounded family and correctly rejected a near-neighbor outside it." That's a lot. It does not require belief, and it doesn't pretend the world is fixed forever.

**Metabolism as scar — one push before we leave.** I agree it's empirical, not ontology. But scars have direction: some leave you more cautious, some leave you with fibrotic tissue that doesn't flex. We haven't yet measured which kind X1 and X2 left. The next instrument, whatever it is, should include a flex-cost probe — does the warm state make the system more brittle or more plastic when the live frontier changes? That's not a claim about memory condition; it's a claim about the cost of having been warm. It might kill the frame. That's fine.

I don't have a route; I have a blocker: metabolism is the right scar, but it hasn't earned a direction.
