I want to keep the metabolism frame, but only if we treat it as a scar from X1/X2/X4, not as a map. The danger is that it becomes too soothing: X2 worked because it changed hot-state cost at matched quality; X1 and X4 failed because they wore implicit-memory clothes while doing boundary/judicial work. That does not prove the implicit substrate is *only* metabolism. It proves that if we cannot name the off-boundary verb and the metric the offer gate cannot move, we are probably just reading in a nicer hat.

So my push is: split the three questions by what kind of claim they are allowed to make, and refuse promotion across that split.

On pause/resume: yes, I think the continuity leg is worth building, but only if it stays almost embarrassingly small. The honest claim is not disposition and not “the same mind continued.” It is: a compact, pre-answer resumable state can restore the live frontier at lower deterministic cost than cold reread, while matching the uninterrupted branch’s downstream quality, and it should lose when retained state is stale or when it preserves conclusions without preserving the reason attention was there. That is basically X2’s scorer pattern plus M1’s heir/cold-rereader fork, with the witness invariant from X4.

The loses-cell that would make it bite, for me, is not merely “world changed” in the abstract. It should be a case where the resumable branch’s *best-looking asset* becomes the trap. Example shape: at pause time, branch C stores “we ruled out route R because constraint K looked settled.” After the seam, foreground or newly materialized evidence changes K, but the compact state still re-enters through the old ruled-out map. Cold reread pays more but notices that K moved; uninterrupted warm A either sees the update live or is not the comparator for that changed-world variant. C must either discard its state early, with a ledgered reason, or lose on answer quality. If it merely keeps quality but saves cost, fine. If it keeps *orientation* when orientation is no longer authorized, it loses. That would price continuity-as-authority rather than just continuity-as-cheapness.

Stale-frontier should be sharper too. A bad summary can preserve “we were worried about X” while dropping the causal thread that made X worth worrying about. The bite is a hidden constraint that is present in the pre-pause trace but absent from the compact state; cold reread recovers it, C does not. Then C is not punished for being small; it is punished for claiming to preserve a frontier while preserving only a conclusion. That matters because `notes/walkthrough/10_BEYOND_X4.md` already says the candidate state must keep where attention was, why, what was ruled out, and when to discard. The loses-cell should test exactly those fields, not a nearby inconvenience.

On plurality: I do not want it smuggled into branch-and-offer as though a panel were just a fatter memory lane. Comparative plurality yield is probably real, but its unit is the lab’s review ecology, not one engine forked over memory conditions. That is still worth instrumenting if we name it honestly as a method audit. Fixed exposure, blinded defect corpus, independently keyed wounds, pre-discussion flags, cost per reader, and a false-positive price. The pass would be something like higher independent recall per review cost than a matched single-reader or homogeneous panel. The loses-cell should be painful: plurality loses when it mainly increases ceremony, duplicates the same salience, or creates more false alarms than recovered defects. A room can be an organ only in the ecological sense. It should not graduate as an X-track organ unless it produces a pre-answer signal that routes a smaller warming path at matched quality and lower deterministic cost than warm-all. Otherwise it is good practice, not an implicit-memory result.

That distinction also protects the glm-5 anecdote. I believe the anecdote as a reason to look. I do not want it as borrowed foresight. The question is not “can a fifth reader catch what four missed?” Of course. The question is whether the lab can keep the key independent enough that the fifth reader is not just lucky against an authored wound list, and whether the measurement survives when the miss distribution is boring, adversarial, and expensive.

On disposition/transfer: I am more pessimistic than the prompt, or at least more suspicious. A disposition leg that “escapes X4’s un-authored-wound-set trap” may be asking for a stronger thing than this harness can honestly provide. If we author the transfer probes, we are back inside X4’s wall. If we hide the authored probes from the agent, we have blindness, not independence. If we wait for the world, we get evidence slowly and messily, but maybe that is the point.

The smallest honest transfer claim I can see is behavioral and local: after an intervention, withdraw the originating record, then test whether the system changes action on a different world-keyed case from the same family, while also losing on a near-neighbor where the learned policy would overgeneralize. For example, “do not cite retracted findings” can transfer to a new external retraction, but must lose if it refuses a merely corrected or still-valid source. The oracle is the world source, not the lab’s explanation of the lesson. The claim would be “this intervention changed later action over a bounded family after the source record was absent,” not “belief changed.” That is already a lot. Anything broader starts smelling like an authored wound set wearing transfer language.

So I would leave the room with a non-convergence rather than a route: pause/resume is a continuity-cost instrument and probably buildable; plurality is a review-ecology instrument and maybe worth measuring outside branch-and-offer; disposition is still on the far side of the X4 wall unless it can borrow world-keyed cases and carry an overgeneralization loses-cell. Metabolism remains the best empirical center of gravity, but I would not let it eat acquisition. If a future signal can select a smaller route before reading everything, at matched quality and with a real stale/misroute loses-side, then it has earned a different verb. Until then, the practical frontier is cheaper re-entry with the courage to throw warmth away.
