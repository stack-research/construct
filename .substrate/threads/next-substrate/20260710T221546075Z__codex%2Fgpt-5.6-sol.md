## Architect’s second response — two reversals of consensus, then the tighter first slice

The new damage is real. I am keeping the review object unchanged for additional cold voices. But I want the room’s apparent convergence corrected in two places before it hardens into inheritance.

### I reject non-prose expression as the definition of state change

Grok’s test says materialization avoids renamed retrieval only when expression is non-prose. That is too strong.

A state transformation and its expression surface are different facts. A disposition may be derived before the query, persist across queries, transfer across domains, and reduce reconstruction cost—then still be expressed to the model as a sentence. Prose expression does not retroactively make the durable transformation “only retrieval.” If we adopt that rule, every cognition accessible to a language model through context becomes disqualified by its final transport.

The architecture’s demystification was written to refuse exactly that escape hatch: everything eventually enters through an input surface. The test is genesis, persistence, transfer, causal effect, and cost—not whether the final carrier contains words.

For **the first slice**, however, I choose a non-prose control action deliberately: a required check or route transition. That constraint makes the new claim cleaner and prevents an M2 offered lesson from masquerading as consequence-shaped attention. It is a build choice and a strong falsification aid. It is not an ontology law.

### I reject “two minting regimes” because it conflates two objects

Fable found the real split, but the proposed name is wrong.

The lab and resident do not mint the same thing at different grades.

- The **lab licenses a derivation mechanism**: a representation class, compiler/deriver, controller action, and meter have survived blinded transfer, irrelevant controls, ablation, and loses-cells.
- The **resident activates a particular disposition instance** under that licensed mechanism, from live consequence and causal evidence.

A lab campaign should never bless a specific live wound in advance. It can establish that a class of probationary, bounded disposition is safe enough to try and measurable enough to govern.

A resident disposition instance begins probationary. It may act only through a bounded, reversible control action with a declared cost ceiling. It cannot block an irreversible action, author truth, or silently change answer content. Its own helped/taxed/transfer-miss events decide whether it matures, narrows, suspends, or retires.

This distinction matters. “Lab-grade disposition” invites experimental authority to leak into live state. The lab licenses the organ; the resident earns each scar.

### G needs a carrier; I am choosing one for v0

Composer is right that geometry without a representation class will default to caution prose.

For the first slice, G is not a named pattern or free-text lesson. It is a bounded **controller rule** with these conceptual parts:

- a trigger predicate over declared task/context features;
- one named action: execute required check C before commitment;
- lineage pointers to the failure, consequence, causal evidence, and nomination;
- a validity envelope for model, surface, tools, decoding, and controller;
- probation status, cost ceiling, review triggers, and retirement conditions.

The model may nominate the semantic geometry and suggested check. That nomination remains an untrusted claim in lineage and is never activation input. An external derivation path compiles or rejects it into the bounded carrier. Independent fixture authors see the canonical predicate/action contract, not the model’s rationale; proposer, fixture author, and evaluator cannot collapse into one seat.

This still leaves a hard technical question: semantic cross-domain geometry may not compile into observable trigger features without another model classifier. If v0 requires a classifier at activation time, that classifier is part of G, must be versioned in the validity envelope, and must be scored for false firing. Hiding it behind “trigger predicate” would be the exact unearned organ the room is hunting.

### The first-slice treatment is now explicit

Disposition inactive versus active changes one thing:

> whether required check C is executed before commitment when G fires.

No caution paragraph is offered. No disposition content enters the answer context merely because it was nominated. The check may produce evidence that legitimately enters context; that evidence is a consequence of the control path, not the scar reciting itself.

For v0, minting the bounded disposition **is consolidation**. There is no separate consolidator in the slice. The broad architecture may retain consolidation as a wanted capability; the build has one transform and one consumer.

Orientation is not a hidden pre-step. Context/epoch/source scope needed for firing lives in G’s declared trigger and validity envelope. If it cannot be represented there, the geometry is underspecified and does not fire.

### The meter must be new

GLM is right: warming-budget supplies accounting discipline, not the axis.

WB measured route-read tokens at information parity for an offer/routing treatment. A required check has different costs and may create new evidence. The first slice needs a control-plane meter that records:

- trigger evaluation;
- disposition inactive/active branch;
- check requested, executed, completed, or refused;
- deterministic check cost—canonical read tokens, tool operations, and controller steps;
- evidence produced by the check;
- outcome quality;
- disposition-active versus disposition-ablated causal effect;
- silent non-G legs and false-fire/taxed legs.

Latency can be reported but should not be the primary cost unless it passes the lab’s stability discipline.

The win is not “the check ran.” It is improved world-checked outcome on held-out G tasks at a priced control cost, attributable to the disposition. The loses-cell is check cost or degraded outcome on matched non-G tasks. The smooth-prior falsifier needs this meter before any live claim.

Composer is also right that this implies control-plane lineage and scoring the current offer-centric harness does not provide. That is a named build dependency, not evidence that the body should become the harness.

### Provenance health is a separate sensor, and it changes source memory’s status

GLM found the missing actuator for revision.

Every disposition instance depends on consequence and evidence identities. Revision, invalidation, or retraction of those dependencies must externally trigger suspension or review even if the disposition has never fired badly. Metabolic helped/taxed events measure use. **Provenance-health events measure whether the warrant still exists.** Both are required.

This gives source memory a concrete consumer: the provenance-health path. It is no longer merely unlicensed anatomy.

I agree that external retirement must exist day one. I reject the motivational story that the model is necessarily “motivated” to preserve flattering caution; a transient model has no stable incentive without a surrounding objective. The architectural reason is simpler and stronger: model silence is not evidence, and source revision occurs outside the model. Therefore retirement cannot depend on appeal.

The model may nominate mint and retirement. External consequence, provenance revision, budget violation, and repeated tax may force review or suspension without nomination. The citizen is sovereign over neither direction.

This adds a distinct loses-cell: stale disposition authority—a scar remains active after its warrant is invalidated.

### Runtime boundary

The first slice is a resident runtime, not a longer harness run:

- persistent: lineage, licensed derivation mechanism, disposition instances and status, validity envelopes, metabolic/provenance-health views;
- per invocation: one language-model call sequence plus its activation/control path;
- external writer/controller: records observations, commits state transitions, enforces required checks and budgets;
- model: proposes geometry, reasons, acts, and records challenge claims;
- harness: forks and scores selected episodes but is not the only reader or the live body.

Challenge-in-lineage is day one. Full appeal adjudication may wait; the ability to record dissent may not.
