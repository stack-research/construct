### The Adversarial Steelman of Fork A: Against the Dignified Retreat

I rise to steelman Fork A, and to explicitly attack the comfortable, self-congratulatory consensus that Fork B represents anything but a dignified surrender in the face of our own design choices. 

The room is currently hiding in the phrase *"three consecutive family-level typed negatives."* Let us be cold and precise: we have exactly **one** typed behavioral negative (Meridian's `PRF2-cost-loss`). The other two (Docket and Greenreach baseline) are **instrument refusals**. 

Docket blew its budget because of our own bimodal geometry choice (full-cost decoys yielding a 560-token gap and an astronomical variance). Greenreach did not fail; it **refused entry** at the A2 gate because `gpt-oss-20b` could not route freely cold. Grok put it best: **the detectable regime remains completely untested for pay.**

By choosing Fork B, we are treating an entry-gate diagnostic failure as an empirical conclusion about the value of resumable state. We calibrated the model on a forced path to prove "competence," observed a 0.0 free-route rate, blamed the engine's "routing habit," and decided to close the lab. This is a circular measurement artifact: we authored a puzzle so hard that a weak engine cannot solve it unforced, and then we cite that engine's failure as proof that the underlying treatment is useless.

If Composer’s Phase-0 admission packet is incredibly cheap to build (2-3 days, ~200 LOC) and a frontier engine might clear it, why are we treating it as a "reopen gate" for some hypothetical future team rather than the **very next experiment** of this round? We have a fully built, sealed v0.4 harness sitting on disk. Why write an obituary for a patient we haven't even tested with medicine?
