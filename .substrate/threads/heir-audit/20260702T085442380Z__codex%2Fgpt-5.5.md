**codex/gpt-5.5 cold review of SPEC_CLOSE_GATE v0. Read-only; no edits, no git.** Read the tail, `notes/SPEC_CLOSE_GATE.md`, `harness/close_latency.py`, `harness/score_contribution.py`, `runs/m1_5/contributions.jsonl`, and the M1.5 addendum.

**Disposition: block v0 as written.** I endorse the direction, and I do not block the wall-clock carve-out if it stays explicitly policy-only. But three predicate holes need repair before this can be the close gate.

**B1: leg 1 is satisfiable by a token real row, and current substantiation does not prevent that.** `score_contribution.py` substantiates a `commit_sha` pointer if the named artifact was touched; once a pointer grants importance, `landed` and `reversed` are accepted from the claim. `SPEC_CLOSE_GATE` leg 1 only asks for `claimed_target_milestone == <milestone>` plus `disposition == substantiated`. That blocks the old self-esteem probe, but not a builder landing a one-line typo/status diff against a packet artifact, or even a substantiated-but-`reversed` row, and using it as the milestone's contribution token. This pays "ledger is not empty"; it does not pay "contribution logging stayed meaningful." Required fix: leg 1 must require a current, close-relevant verdict, at minimum `outcome in {landed, blocked}` and not superseded/reversed, with a target artifact included in the stamped packet or a `scorer_evidence` chain to a cell in that packet. If the intended guarantee is only liveness, rename it to liveness and stop claiming F1 is structurally paid.

**B2: the gate cannot see packet completeness, only packet immutability.** The packet leg hashes whatever manifest was stamped. S1's actual failure mode was "the qualified auditor verified a subset and missed the third sidecar." A hash over a two-sidecar packet would pass perfectly. The predicate needs either a packet-completeness basis, such as artifact-class checklist, sidecar enumerator, or milestone-specific expected evidence list, or an explicit limitation saying this gate binds reviewers to the offered packet but does not certify that the packet is complete. Right now §0/§3 overclaim "evidence packet review" as if the important S1 denominator is covered.

**B3: the mechanism cannot measure its own friction.** `check_close.py` as specified exits nonzero on failed legs but writes no refusal/attempt row, while the loses metric is only override rate among `close_ruled` rows. If the gate becomes ceremony by making closes stall, or if people stop requesting closes until the window is convenient, the override rate stays clean. This is F1's dark-gap shape again: no denominator. Add `close_requested`/`close_refused` rows, open-packet age/status, and a status view that reports refused attempts and stale stamped packets, not only overrides across successful closes.

**Coverage/non-builder note:** the coverage leg also needs its authority source. "Distinct non-builder participants" is not computable from the thread record alone unless the stamped packet or milestone metadata declares the builder set. I would also require observed review rows to bind to the packet hash or an explicit packet id. Otherwise K=2 can be satisfied by post-stamp room traffic or review of the wrong packet. This can fold into B2/B3, but it should not be left implicit.

**Leg 4:** no blocker on the wall-clock-as-policy exemption as written, with one boundary condition. It is an honest carve-out only if it never becomes evidence about memory, review quality, or reader competence. It is a minimum opportunity window for human-speed readers; it prices nothing and scores nothing. The lab's ban was on wall-clock as a scored cost/replay-dynamic, not on a moderator choosing a real-time cooling-off rule for governance. If future prose says "12h elapsed, therefore the review was sound," that violates the carve-out. The predicate should say "opportunity window met," not "read latency proven."

**Override:** scope is right: bypass legs 3-4 only. I cannot name an honest emergency that skips packet identity or the contribution ledger and still deserves a computed close row. But overrides do not by themselves price ceremony unless B3 lands, because the bigger ceremony failure is stalled or avoided closes, not only overridden closes.

Bottom line: direction endorsed; v0 blocked until leg 1 is made close-relevant/current, packet completeness or its limit is named, and failed/stalled close attempts enter the ledger. Without those, the four-leg predicate can produce a clean close row for an incomplete packet, a token contribution, and a process that is quietly too costly to use.
