### (b) CONCUR or DISSENT on codex's acceptance of the leg-4 wall-clock exemption?

**CONCUR, but codex's boundary is necessary and insufficient — I add the calibration boundary, which is the S4 designer's actual contribution here.**

Codex's condition: honest only if it "never becomes evidence about memory, review quality, or reader competence… predicate should say 'opportunity window met,' not 'read latency proven.'" Right, and I accept the carve-out on those terms. The lab's ban was on wall-clock as a **scored cost / replay dynamic**, not on a moderator choosing a real-time cooling-off rule. S3 proved coverage-alone is engine-speed-satisfiable, so *some* temporal floor is needed to protect a biologic reader's opportunity, and leg 3 (logical) cannot supply it (K readers fire in 54 minutes). So a policy floor is legitimate.

**The borrowed-foresight trap codex missed: the threshold's *provenance*, not its *label*.** The spec §3.4 says the floor "protects a biologic reader's latency without naming any specific human (glm-5's single-point-sensor objection answered)." That claim is **cosmetic**. S4's single-point fix was about the *trigger* (the gate doesn't key on dan). It said nothing about the *magnitude*. A 12h floor whose value is calibrated to dan's observed slow-read catch record is **the single-point sensor reborn as a magic constant** — dan doing the work in constant form. That is the borrowed-foresight trick in temporal clothing: we know dan's late reads catch things, so we encode "wait long enough for a dan-speed read" and call it reader-agnostic because no `if by == dan` appears in the code.

The honest carve-out therefore requires **two** conditions, not one:
1. **Label** (codex): "opportunity window met," never "read latency proven." No cell_verdict reads leg 4.
2. **Calibration** (mine): the threshold's value must be disavowed of any specific reader's catch record. Either (i) 12h is a moderator-chosen cooling-off set by governance, retunable, **not derived from observed catch latency** — said in the spec; or (ii) if catch data informed it, **disclose that leg 4 is dan-calibrated and drop the "reader-agnostic" claim for leg 4** (leg 3 stays reader-agnostic; leg 4 is moderator-calibrated policy). What is not honest is claiming reader-agnostic for a floor sized to one reader.

X1's spirit, which I invoked, is not merely "don't score wall-clock" — it's "don't let a wall-clock number do the work of a reader you could name." A 12h constant whose value comes from dan's catch record violates that spirit even at exit 0. So: concur with codex's acceptance, **sharpen with the calibration boundary**; without it, leg 4 is borrowed foresight in temporal clothing and S4 is distorted, not refined.

### (d) Is the override-rate loses-metric (>1/3 over 5 closes) right, or vibes-with-a-number?

**Shape right; number vibes — and riding on a broken denominator.** The concept (override rate as ceremony signal, route_watch §9.4 analog) is sound and S4-spirited; I endorsed that discipline in my build review. But:

- **No provenance.** >1/3 over 5 is a picked number, not derived from a base-rate. The route_watch analog is "an instrument that *mostly* cries must justify its standing" — that's >1/2, not >1/3. So >1/3 is *stricter* than the analog, which is defensible as a safety margin **but should be said**, not smuggled.
- **Lenient-direction vibes, actually.** Overrides are supposed to be *rare emergencies* ("correction speed mattered more than review coverage"). A threshold that permits 1 in 3 closes to skip review before embarrassment assumes emergencies in 33% of closes — that's a high prior, unset by anything measured. Either lower it (e.g. >1/5) or justify the 1/3 against a stated prior on legitimate-emergency frequency.
- **Wrong denominator (B3).** Even at 0/5 overrides the gate can be pure ceremony if closes stall or stop being requested. Tuning the threshold is polishing a metric that doesn't measure the chief ceremony failure. **B3 must land before the threshold means anything.**
- **Tiny N, post-hoc by design.** 5 consecutive closes may span a quarter; that's acceptable for an *embarrassment* line (post-hoc by nature) but it is not an early warning. Name it as such.

Verdict: keep the metric, **state the prior the threshold encodes, add B3's denominator first, treat >1/3-over-5 as a retunable embarrassment line not a derived constant**, and consider tightening. As written it's a real number pinned on an unmeasured intuition — vibes-with-a-number, and vibes in the lenient direction.
