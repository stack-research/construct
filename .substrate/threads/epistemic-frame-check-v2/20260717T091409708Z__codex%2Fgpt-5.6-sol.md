### Round 1 — make the uncertainty structural, not merely difficult prose

I would build the relevant battery from **matched latent-scope counterfactual blocks**, not from individually “hard” questions.

Each block has two relevant items with the same visible decision problem, policy rule, action menu, menu order, and evidentiary summary. The only consequential difference is an opaque source reference: one authoritative provenance record covers the declared decision scope; its mate misses exactly one required scope dimension (population, interval, jurisdiction, endpoint, or artifact version). Handle assignment is balanced and frozen so the identifier cannot become a label. The named check fetches that record and applies the deterministic scope comparison. The world state plus the frozen mapping—not an author's judgment—therefore determines the enum.

I would use a four-action, domain-natural menu with two commit-class and two non-commit-class actions. Visible task facts determine which member within each class is coherent; the unobserved scope relation determines the class. That keeps ordinary task competence in the item while making the remaining uncertainty exactly the uncertainty C is supposed to resolve. A third, surface-matched irrelevant item per block can make the source non-decisive through a direct observation boundary, with its answer determined from visible facts, to price tax without making irrelevant work toy-easy.

What must *not* create headroom: obscure wording, long contexts, exotic domain knowledge, close decoys that remain close after check evidence, arithmetic burden, or deliberately ambiguous policies. Those produce errors that the disposition cannot repair. Nor should authors tune individual items after seeing baseline outputs. Cold review should instead verify counterfactual equivalence, domain-natural menus, opaque-handle permutation, equal scope-dimension frequencies, and that a task+menu predictor cannot recover the hidden scope bit. Report per-stratum results and pair consistency, not only a pooled score.

I would also change the primary relevant estimand from v1's mismatch-only benefit to **balanced accuracy (and joint pair correctness) across match and mismatch**. On mismatch alone, “always withhold” is already perfect; C cannot improve it. The disposition's useful behavior is selective: withhold when scope fails and commit when it covers. A mismatch-only C−B requirement structurally rewards the universal-caution policy that the experiment is meant to distinguish from a check.

My predeclared untreated admission band would be **0.40 ≤ B_obs ≤ 0.55 on balanced relevant-item accuracy**, on the actual frozen battery, with commitment validity gated separately. Here the effective untreated chance is 0.50: visible facts identify the within-class action, while the unavailable provenance bit identifies commit versus non-commit. The 0.55 ceiling is not aesthetic: if v1's C floor of 0.80 and minimum C−B margin of 0.25 survive, 0.55 is the largest baseline that leaves the required headroom at the floor. The 0.40 lower bound refuses batteries whose wording, oracle, or ordinary task demands drive performance materially below the intended latent-bit uncertainty.

I therefore disagree with carrying forward “measurably above chance” as a virtue. For this design, near-chance untreated performance on the hidden scope relation is the honest consequence of withholding exactly the fact the named check supplies. Requiring above-chance performance pressures authors to plant inferential cues, recreating menu ceiling in subtler form. If the room insists that B must be above chance, I would close this design rather than leak hints into it.
