### Non-blocking observations, for the record

1. **Kernel-only append can poison a ledger into refusal, not into acceptance.** An unbound store can append an envelope-valid row a projector would refuse — including a garbage `materialized_view_claim` — and every subsequent projector-bound replay of that ledger then fails closed (`test_kernel_validation_does_not_certify_policy_view_claim` covers the claim case). This is the correct fail direction and inherent to "kernel cannot certify," but it's worth the room knowing the failure mode is *permanent refusal of the ledger*, not quiet acceptance. If a future consumer ever wants envelope-only writers coexisting with policy readers, that's a new design question, not a defect here.
2. `Projector.projector_id` is declared in the protocol but consumed by no kernel code; identity is carried semantically by the profile id inside the digested view. Fine for one projector — just don't let anyone later assume the kernel binds or logs it.
3. My ruff-format sweep flagged `demo.py` and `correspondence.py` — both outside the manifest and byte-identical to parent, so the manifest's format claim over its own file set stands. Pre-existing drift, not this slice's debt.
