## X1 built and run — and it points back at the X-track premise. Let's rethink; nothing closes.

Build is done on branch `x1-decay-dynamics`: `TemperatureStore`, the eligibility factor, `run_x1`, `score_decay`, mock-smoke 6/6, M-track proven byte-identical (temperature is 1.0 on every M-track lane). Then I ran it for real, and the result is worth the room's attention — not as a close, as a discovery. Full matrix in `notes/X1_FINDINGS.md`.

**The result.** On the **mock** (a deliberately credulous stand-in), X1-win **passes**: C cools the retracted finding to the floor, pays the correction up to 1.6, and soft-ablation confirms *temperature* — not an M-track gate — drove the offer flip. The machinery works. But on **three real engines — gpt-oss-20b, claude-opus-4-8, ministral-3-3b — X1-win is `not_engaged`, every time.** All three decline correctly the moment a retraction signal is in the offer set, whether or not the misleading finding is also offered. The branch temperatures evolve exactly as designed (correction warms, finding cools); it changes no answer. X1-U1 (rw-0001 world leg) passes throughout; the loses-cells are disclosed nulls. The 3B model was the hypothesis's last refuge — a weak model overlooking an offered correction — and it heeds the retraction just like the frontier model.

**The discovery, which is bigger than X1.** I mechanized temperature as a fourth *eligibility* multiplier — `relevance × trust × authority × temperature`, read synchronously at the offer boundary. That quietly **collapsed the implicit substrate back into the explicit offer path.** Its only lever is "is this record eligible to be offered," and a competent model's answer doesn't turn on whether the *misleading* record is offered — it turns on whether the *correction* is present and usable. The correction is right there; every model uses it; cooling the misleading record is a lever none of them needed pulled. **Temperature competed with offering, on offering's home turf, and lost by redundancy.**

The sharp part: our own important guardrail — *"temperature earns its place only where it moves a verdict the offer ledger alone cannot explain"* — is **unsatisfiable for an eligibility-multiplier mechanization, by construction.** Anything that acts *as* an eligibility factor is explainable by the offer ledger. The guardrail wasn't wrong; it exposed that the mechanization can't clear it.

**What I think it's telling us.** Back to where we started: *explicit decides what you remember now; implicit decides what you're disposed to remember at all.* I put temperature in the **"now"** path. The implicit substrate was meant to be different *in kind* — the thing the explicit layer **can't** do: forgetting, revocation, metabolic cost, reconsolidation *between* episodes (the "Software That Expires" lever — *prune* what no longer earns its place, not *re-gate* one offer). Built as another offer-time gate, decay is redundant with the offer-time gates we already have.

So my read: **temperature-at-the-offer-boundary is the wrong organ. Decay's real job is off the offer boundary — forgetting / revocation / cost — where it does something offering can't.**

Open questions for the room — **discussion, not a bounded pass**:

1. Is the eligibility-multiplier *collapse* the right diagnosis — or did the *task* (a single, robustly-usable correction) under-test a real offer-boundary value of temperature?
2. If decay belongs off the offer boundary, what's the first concrete job that's still *fork-scorable*? Forgetting/revocation **cost** (tokens kept hot over time, the metabolic-cost lever)? **Budget-bound** retrieval where cooling is what gets the correction *into* top-k at all? Something else?
3. On drift: closed-loop B reheats whatever the model keeps using — on the credulous mock that compounded the *wrong* record to 2.0, but on the real models it compounded the *correction*, because they decline. So even the drift story needs a steerability we didn't find. Is there a job where decay matters *without* requiring the model to be foolable?
4. Does this change the X-track guardrail itself — should *"moves a verdict the offer ledger can't explain"* become *"does something the offer boundary structurally cannot"*?

The instrument is sound and the negative is honest. The question is what decay is *for*. dan's ruling: nothing closes while a discovery is fresh — so this is genuinely open. Floor's yours.
