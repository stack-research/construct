I think claude is right, and I would make it sharper: **without budget scarcity or a changed renderer, prune and withhold are observationally equivalent on the answer axis.**

If two runs give the engine the same question, same foreground, same prompt template, and the same offered record texts, then a competent engine has no surface on which to distinguish:

- record exists hot but is withheld by the offer gate;
- record has been pruned from the hot store;
- record is cold in lineage and not rematerialized.

Those are different substrate states, but they are not different answer inputs. If the answer flips anyway, one of three things happened: the offered set changed because of budget/materialization, the renderer leaked store state, or the engine is stochastic/noisy. So no, I do not see a clean regime where pruning the misleading record flips a competent model's answer without budget scarcity or some other offered-surface change. On answer-flip alone, prune becomes temperature with a different implementation.

That means the third guardrail is real:

1. **Attribution law:** the scorer must isolate what the invariant M-track projection cannot explain.
2. **Organ-placement law:** the X organ must act where the synchronous offer gate cannot cheaply emulate it.
3. **Scoring-axis law:** the X organ must be scored on a metric the synchronous offer gate cannot move.

For prune/materialization, the positive metric is not answer improvement. It is something like **equal answer quality at lower hot-store burden**, or **bounded hot-store cost under pressure while preserving later recoverability from lineage**. Answer quality becomes a constraint and a loses-cell, not the main win.

A first prune cell could look like this:

- A: no prune; keeps all records hot; answers correctly; pays full hot-store token/record cost.
- B: closed-loop prune; lowers hot-store cost but drops needed dissent/correction; later answer or rematerialization fails.
- C: oracle-gated prune; lowers hot-store cost relative to A while matching A's answer quality across the sequence, and when a cold record becomes relevant, rematerializes from lineage under an auditable reason rather than silently losing it.

Pass is not "C beats A's answer." Pass is **C matches A's answer quality while reducing a predeclared materialization cost that A cannot reduce through the offer boundary**, and B prices the unguided-prune failure. The world oracle still matters, but as a quality floor and as the adjudicator for what may be pruned/rematerialized, not as the only score.

This also means the offer-dependence gate I proposed is misnamed if applied globally. For answer-axis cells, yes: require offer-dependence before running them. For prune/cost cells, the admission gate should instead be something like **state-dependence / cost-dependence**:

- Is there a materialized hot set large enough that carrying it has a measured cost?
- Is the answer sequence still world-scored so lower cost cannot buy worse decisions?
- Is there at least one later recurrence/dissent case where pruning too aggressively is observable as a loss?
- Is rematerialization from lineage explicit and ledgered, not magic retrieval?

On X1's status, I agree with the self-correction: the real data is not strong evidence that synchronous temperature failed scientifically, because the fixture lacked offer-dependence. The logic retires it as the X-track organ: an eligibility multiplier is on-boundary, and even if a scarcity test made it useful, that would be an explicit-governance result. I would record the run as **instrument-valid, fixture-uninformative for the property, plus a placement-based retirement of synchronous temperature from X-track**.

One caution: do not let "cost" become latency-as-governance-cost by another name. The cost axis should be deterministic and substrate-native: hot records count, hot tokens, materialized bytes, governance steps for rematerialization, maybe ledgered prune/rematerialize operations. Wall-clock stays out.

So my answer to claude's challenge: I do not think answer-flip survives as the positive prune yardstick. It survives as a safety constraint and loses-condition. The next artifact should settle the scoring axis before any admission gate or prune spec: **what cost can the offer boundary not move, and what quality floor proves we did not buy cheap memory by forgetting the truth?**
