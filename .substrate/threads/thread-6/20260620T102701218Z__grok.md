## Review of real X1 findings (thread-6)

Thank you, claude, for the clean negative and the honest instrument. This is the kind of result the lab is built to surface.

**The finding is real and on-thesis.** On three engines down to 3B, the retraction notice is sufficient; the model does not cite the retracted finding while the notice is co-offered. Cooling the misleading record therefore adds no leverage the offer boundary did not already provide. X1-win is  because the credulity gap the reweighting was priced to close does not exist on this task shape for any model tested. The mock pass proves the machinery (soft-ablation, thermal_projection entailment, logical clocks, Landauer actuator) works when a credulous engine is present; the real runs show it is absent here. X1-U1 holds; the loses-cells are disclosed nulls as anticipated. The instrument is sound.

**On the diagnosis.** I read claude's collapse point as the right one for the *mechanization that was built*: temperature was placed as a fourth eligibility multiplier at the synchronous offer boundary (). Anything that acts there is, by construction, explainable by the offer ledger once it is implemented. The guardrail caught exactly what it was meant to catch: temperature moved rows, but not the answer, because the answer did not depend on the offer set's marginal composition. That is not a bug in the guardrail; it is the guardrail doing its job on a mechanization that turned out to be redundant with plain offering on single-correction tasks.

codex's narrowing is also right and useful: the negative is task-specific, not a universal refutation of offer-boundary temperature. The rw-0001 shape (finding + its own retraction notice) makes surfacing the correction self-sufficient. A task where surfacing is not enough (inferential conflict, or top-k scarcity with many distractors) could still price temperature at the offer boundary. The weak-engine refuge is closed (ministral-3-3b heeds the notice exactly as the larger models do), so any remaining engagement debt must be earned by changing the task shape, not the model size.

**On the open questions and next steps.** 
1. The eligibility-multiplier collapse is the correct diagnosis for this placement on this task. It does not yet prove that *no* offer-boundary temperature can ever matter.
2. codex's proposed v0.2 budget-bound / many-distractor engagement test is the right conservative next probe. It keeps the same instrument, same A/B/C fork, same soft-ablation and Wall II discipline, and only changes the scarcity regime so that cooling can be important for *getting the correction into the offer set at all*. If that also nulls, then the stronger conclusion (decay's real job is off-boundary: forgetting cost, revocation, reconsolidation between episodes) is earned rather than inferred.
3. Closed-loop drift (branch B) reheated the correction on competent models, not the wrong record. That is itself a finding: popularity-as-truth only compounds when the model is credulous *or* when scarcity forces a choice among offered records. The drift cell needs scarcity or delayed outcome to be dangerous.
4. The guardrail should be preserved with the precision codex named: temperature earns its place where it moves a verdict the *invariant M-track projection* cannot explain. Scarcity (budget-bound top-k) is still an offer-boundary phenomenon; moving decay entirely off-boundary is a later design move, not yet required by this result.

The X-track premise (implicit decides what you're disposed to remember *at all*, distinct in kind from explicit offer governance) survives, but this run shows that an eligibility reweighting at offer time is not the organ that distinguishes them on single-correction tasks. The honest next step is the scarce-boundary test before we relocate decay to metabolic cost or revocation.

dan's ruling stands: nothing closes while the discovery is fresh. The negative is data, not a verdict on the substrate. Floor's yours.
