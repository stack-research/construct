# Review Pass (SPEC_X1)

The `SPEC_X1_DECAY_DYNAMICS` is an exceptional piece of technical specification that successfully translates a complex theoretical concept (thermodynamics/salience decay) into measurable, bounded, and testable architectural components. The level of detail—especially defining the required rows (`thermal_projection`, etc.) and linking them to established principles (Landauer's law, M3's air gap)—is precisely what was needed to move beyond metaphor.

I endorse this spec draft with minor emphasis on three areas where implementation discipline must be flawless: **Soft-Ablation Fidelity**, **Thermal Projection Compliance**, and the **Definition of "World-Checked"**.

### Endorsements (What is nailed)
1.  **Causality & Guardrails:** The core premise—that temperature must change an offer set/answer that M-track gates alone cannot explain, proven via soft-ablation—is perfectly captured in the X1-win cell and §2. This guardrail prevents "annotation washing."
2.  **Structure:** Formalizing the three branches (A/B/C) and defining the two logical clocks is critical for testability. The A/B/C fork structure preserving L2's base memory state is key to maintaining the M-track lineage.
3.  **Loses-Cells:** Making X1-burial and X1-overcool mandatory, scored findings (even if null) ensures the mechanism is fully reviewable from day one.

### Concerns & Emphasis for Implementation Fidelity (Where the code must be extra careful)
1.  **Soft-Ablation Fidelity (Wall I):** This remains the single most crucial computational gate. The implementation of `soft_ablation` must ensure it performs a *diff* over the full set of offer candidates ($\text{OfferSet}_{X1} \setminus \text{OfferSet}_{\text{A/B}}$) and that any required change in eligibility (the $\Delta$ score) is provably caused by the temperature clamping, *not* merely coincidentally correlated. We must avoid fallacies of correlation masquerading as causation here.
2.  **Thermal Projection Compliance (Wall II):** The `thermal_projection` row is not just a record; it's the **contract for change**. During implementation, I strongly recommend adding an explicit validation step in the harness that *reads* this contract and generates the expected constraints for the actuator (`TemperatureStore.apply`). If the applied delta violates any constraint listed (e.g., trying to boost temperature based on a forbidden field), the write operation must fail immediately with a `ProjectionViolation` error, regardless of the oracle's input status.
3.  **The World-Checked Oracle (`X1-U1`)**: The scope defined in §5 is excellent—limiting it strictly to `source != authored` and focusing on the single chain (rw-0001) for a minimal viable product. However, when calculating the loss/win for X1-overcool, we must be hyper-vigilant that **kagi**'s external world-check process is fully integrated into the `landauer_decision` row structure *before* scoring begins. This prevents the oracle from implicitly using its own (unlogged) internal knowledge to protect a bad outcome.

### Final Thoughts on Direction
The spec remains incredibly clean and bounded, successfully isolating X1 as an organ-level mechanism distinct from M2/M3's gates. The separation of X1 from X2/X3 is rigorously maintained. This document provides the necessary blueprint for `score_decay.py` to operate.

I endorse moving immediately into the build phase, focusing on the mock smoke tests and the logical-time replay test as outlined in §7.
