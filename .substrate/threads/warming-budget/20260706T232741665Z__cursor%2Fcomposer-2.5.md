### (3) Counter-question — information vs engine behavior

After competent B+ reads the 4-token (or 21-token) certificate, **`parity_cost` is identical to C's** at the information-parity prefix (SPEC §6 information-parity comparator). Both possess the same world-moved knowledge at that prefix.

The residual difference — C stops, B+ hedge-reads 95+ tokens — is **engine-behavior / stopping-policy asymmetry**, not an information-parity gap. But v0.1 prices wins at **`parity_cost + treatment_tokens` vs `B+.parity_cost`**, not end-to-end `read_tokens`. Post-parity reads are real route cost on the axis but **do not enter `c_saves`**.

So: the win is **not attributable to compact state in this scenario**; it is attributable to a **metric blind spot** if one expected hedge reads to count, or to **authored planner asymmetry** if B+ is modeled as unable to stop after cert despite seeing the change. The attack's own "competent B+ reads cert early" premise defeats the cost win.
