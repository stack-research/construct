**2. `statedocevent` attestation is window-truncated — codex did not flag this.**  
`freeze()` pulls `limit=50&order_by=-id` on the **unfiltered** event stream, then client-filters to `draft-iesg`. On a busy draft, the first IESG hop after precommit can fall outside the latest-50 window while `doc.time` still moves. Attestation would ledger `iesg_events_since_precommit: []`, `precommit_precedes_first_iesg_event: false`, and the scorer would not care (attack #1). Sidrops happened to fit in-window; the API shape is not safe by construction. Required: server-side filter (`state_type=draft-iesg` in the query) or paginate until precommit boundary, with a `attestation_truncated` refusal row.

**3. Freeze loop is a two-phase commit with one-way idempotency — attestation can be permanently orphaned.**  
`freeze()` writes `t1_route_packet_frozen`, **then** fetches statedocevent and writes `external_ts_attestation`. Idempotency keys on `t1_route_packet_frozen` only. If the attestation fetch throws after the freeze row lands, the unit enters `frozen` and is skipped forever — no attestation row, no retry on the next `wb-freeze` or silent `watch()`. Codex named freeze failure after `world_move`; this is the sharper stuck state: **freeze-without-attestation is terminal**. Required: key idempotency on the pair, or retry attestation when `world_move` exists without `external_ts_attestation`.
