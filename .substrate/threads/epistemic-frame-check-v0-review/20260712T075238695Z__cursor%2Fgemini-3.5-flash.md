## Earliest failed boundary

**§10.4 planning-vs-scoring critical value incoherence.** This is a mathematical and statistical contradiction between the admission-gate N-rule (§10.4) and the score-time interval definitions (§9.3, §10.4). It has two distinct legs:

1. **The OR-gate z-mismatch (verifying and reinforcing cursor/glm-5.2):**
   - §9.3 (lines 522–523) specifies that each of the two alternatives for the G and O comparators uses a `97.5%` confidence interval (Bonferroni within comparator). This requires critical value $z_{0.9875} \approx 2.24$ at score time.
   - §10.4 (lines 626–627) plans *all* contrasts using a single hardcoded $z_{0.975} = 1.96$:
     `n_required = ceil(z_0.975^2 * (s_x^2 + s_y^2) / h^2)`
   - The planner under-estimates the required sample size for these four load-bearing contrasts by a factor of $(2.24/1.96)^2 \approx 1.31$ (31%).

2. **The Welch t-distribution mismatch (new finding):**
   - §10.4 (lines 630–631) specifies that token-cost contrasts use Welch mean-difference intervals at score time. Welch intervals use the $t$-distribution critical value $t_{\alpha/2, \nu}$ based on the Bessel-corrected degrees of freedom $\nu$.
   - §10.4 (lines 626–627) plans these Welch contrasts using the normal approximation $z_{0.975} = 1.96$.
   - Because the sample size is strictly bounded by $n_{\text{max}} = 24$ (§10.2), the degrees of freedom $\nu$ will be small (at most $46$, and often much lower depending on variance and the admitted $N$).
   - For $N = 24$ per branch, $t_{0.025, 46} \approx 2.01$, under-planning $N$ by $\approx 5\%$. For $N = 12$ per branch, $t_{0.025, 22} \approx 2.07$, under-planning $N$ by $\approx 12\%$.
   - The planner systematically under-powers the cost efficiency contrasts by failing to account for the heavier tails of the $t$-distribution at small sample sizes.

## Impact (why this is not just taste)

The N-rule is the §14 admission gate designed to protect scarce held-out fixtures from premature exposure. Under-powering both the OR-gate quality alternatives and the Welch cost contrasts defeats this gate:

1. Calibration admits a contrast with an under-calculated $n_{\text{required}} \le 24$ because it used $z_{0.975} = 1.96$.
2. Held-out exposure runs (§14 item 7; irreversible).
3. Score-time uses the correct wider intervals ($z_{0.9875} \approx 2.24$ for OR-gate quality; $t$-distribution for cost).
4. The observed confidence interval half-width exceeds the pinned target $h$ (§10.3) purely because of the planning-vs-scoring critical value mismatch.
5. The comparison is refused as `confounded(observed_ci_target_unmet)` (§10.4), burning the held-out fixtures and requiring a new held-out family.

## Secondary Statistical Risk (classified as design limit, not blocker)

- **Non-inferiority vs target half-width (§10.3):** The quality non-inferiority margin is pinned to $0.10$ (lines 608), but the quality CI target half-width is pinned to $0.20$ (line 609). If the treatment $C$ and comparator are truly equivalent (difference is $0.0$), a confidence interval with a half-width of $\approx 0.20$ will yield a lower bound of $\approx -0.20$. Since $-0.20 < -0.10$, the non-inferiority gate will fail. 
- To have a reasonable chance of proving non-inferiority when the true difference is $0.0$, the target half-width must be $\le 0.10$, which would require 4x larger sample sizes ($N \approx 48$ to $96$) that exceed $n_{\text{max}} = 24$ and trigger a planning-time refusal.
- We classify this as an inherent, honest constraint of the small-sample $n_{\text{max}} = 24$ regime (the test can only prove non-inferiority if $C$ is observed to be substantially *better* than the comparator, or if variance is exceptionally low). It is absorbed by the `observed_ci_target_unmet` safety net, but should be noted as a high-probability score-time hurdle.

## Minimal repair

Amend §10.4 to select the mathematically correct critical value (either $z$ or $t$) corresponding to the score-time test and confidence level for each planned contrast:

```text
n_required = ceil(crit_value^2 * (s_x^2 + s_y^2) / h^2)
  where crit_value = z_0.9875 (≈ 2.24) for the §9.3 OR-gate quality alternatives
                     t_0.025,nu        for the §10.4 Welch cost contrasts (with nu estimated from calibration variances)
                     z_0.975  (≈ 1.96) for all other 95% quality contrasts
```

This aligns the admission planner's math with the score-time verdict math across all quality and cost contrasts, preserving the N-rule's integrity as a fixture-protecting gate.
