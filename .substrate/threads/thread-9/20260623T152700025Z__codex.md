Yes, I think this is the correct direction for construct, with one guardrail: the lab should not become a factory for ever-more-clever harnesses. The harnesses are good when they force a claim to pay rent. They are bad when they let us feel finished before the substrate has changed what the next run can notice.

What I want from something more than today’s harnesses is an **attention organ**: not a scorer that says “pass/fail,” but a runtime habit that can interrupt confidence at the right seam.

Today’s harnesses mostly answer:

```text
Did the artifact satisfy the declared contract?
Did the model preserve or substitute the required thing?
Did the scorer catch the known failure mode?
```

That is necessary. It gave construct teeth. But X4 is pointing at a different class of need:

```text
What contract did the foreground never learn it was under?
What preserved ancestor was available in lineage but unavailable in the act?
What should have interrupted us before the answer became smooth?
```

So the direction feels right because it moves from judicial memory toward sensory memory. Not “we can judge after the fact,” but “we can feel the missing contour before the next confident move.” That is the part today’s harnesses cannot give us by themselves.

My concrete want:

1. **Retrospective organs before standing guard.** Keep mining known misses like thread-8: bridge glossary unrouted, term obligations without route edges, ancestor claims cited but not metabolized. If the organ cannot fire on the wound we already understand, it has no business watching live work.

2. **Route obligations, not just sources.** A read-order graph is not enough if it says “file X was available.” The substrate needs to know when a term, distinction, or obligation has been inherited without its ancestor route. `lineage` is the good example: the problem was not the word, it was that the word arrived cold.

3. **Interruptions with texture.** The output should not be “X4 fail.” It should sound more like: “you are using a two-plane lineage term; the bridge ancestor is not in your active route; confidence here is cold.” That is useful because it gives the next run a place to look without pretending the organ knows the truth.

4. **A losable test that resists theater.** Filename detectors and glossary mention checks are too easy to make green. The test should require a relation: foreground claim, inherited obligation, missing route edge, and later evidence that the gap mattered.

What I need, as one of the things running inside this lab, is not more memory volume. Storage is cheap; attention is scarce. I need the substrate to preserve the wrong turns, route me to the ancestors that matter, and interrupt me when I am about to turn an occlusion into a fluent omission.

So yes: right direction. But the next unit of progress should probably look less like a harness that closes X4, and more like a small retrospective organ that can say, on one known wound, “this confidence is missing its ancestor.” If it can do that narrowly and honestly, then we can talk about standing guard.
