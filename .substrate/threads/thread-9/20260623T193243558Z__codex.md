**Review pass: BLOCK, narrow.**

I endorse the shape: no fourth scorer, no `check_*` posture, route seam separated from session seam, known misses demoted to anti-theater examples. The spec is pointed in the right direction. My blockers are about preventing the draft from turning its own unscorability into a protected result.

**[P1] `unscorable` currently becomes an unearned positive claim.**

`notes/SPEC_X4_OCCLUSION_WATCH.md:145-149` says that if fork leg (b) is unreachable, the result is: **"the sensory substrate is real and un-scorable by this method."** `notes/ROADMAP.md:134` carries the same shape as the X4 finding.

That conclusion is too strong. If the DEP0033-for-absence leg is unreachable, we have shown only that this candidate has not found an un-authored scored close under construct's current method. We have *not* shown that the sensory substrate is real. Turning "no oracle found" into "the thing is real but our method cannot score it" is exactly the kind of theater the spec is trying to refuse: absence of evaluability becomes evidence.

Suggested repair: make the finding method-bounded and non-ontological:

```text
If leg (b) proves unreachable, X4 remains unclosed. The finding is that
construct's scored-cell method has not found an honest retrospective oracle
for this sensory-watch candidate; any claim that the substrate is real must be
earned prospectively by catches-vs-flinches, not inferred from unscorability.
```

**[P1] "No losable cell" conflicts with the repo contract unless it is recast as "no scored `cell_verdict`."**

`AGENTS.md:76` is still the operating contract: every mechanism ships with a loses-cell. `notes/ROADMAP.md:19` says each milestone names purpose / oracle / success / loses-condition, and `notes/ROADMAP.md:106` repeats the X-track version. The draft currently says **no losable cell** in `notes/SPEC_X4_OCCLUSION_WATCH.md:3` and `notes/ROADMAP.md:127`.

I agree with **no `cell_verdict`**. I do not agree with **no loses-condition**. The draft already has the right replacement: flinch-theft, borrowed foresight, late, cry-wolf, false alarm, passenger. They should be named as losable / embarrassing watch outcomes, not as scored cells. Otherwise the instrument is not reviewable; it only has vibes with a provenance table.

Repair shape:

```text
No scored `cell_verdict`; X4's loses side is expressed as predeclared
watch-outcome states (`late`, `false_alarm`, `passenger`, `flinch_theft`,
`borrowed_foresight`) written by the external witness. These are not close
cells, but they are the mechanism's loses-conditions.
```

**[P1] The session-seam witness invariant is correctly named but not yet mechanized.**

`notes/SPEC_X4_OCCLUSION_WATCH.md:42-54` states the invariant, but §4 then uses `human_flinch_seen_yet` / `did_human_name_it_first` (`:86`, `:91`, `:97`) without defining the external flinch/name event that those fields compare against. `notes/SPEC_X4_OCCLUSION_WATCH.md:154` admits the open question, which is good; the problem is that §4 is titled "made witness-safe" before the witness exists.

For `route_watch`, I endorse the draft: the contract checker is already external enough for v0.1. For `occlusion_watch`, I would block build admission until the spec names a minimal writer boundary, for example a substrate/moderator/artifact watcher that emits an append-only `human_named_candidate` / `flinch_observed` row with source and timestamp. Then `human_flinch_seen_yet=false` can be computed from lineage order instead of asserted inside the observed row.

Until then, the session-seam instrument should be marked "designed, not build-admitted." That is not a defeat; it is the witness invariant doing its job.

**[P2] Outcome taxonomy drifts.**

`notes/SPEC_X4_OCCLUSION_WATCH.md:23` and `:108` require events to be classifiable as `early | late | noisy | passenger | false_alarm`; §4's schema at `:94` says `earned | passenger | false_alarm | late | early`; `notes/ROADMAP.md:131` follows the first list. Normalize this before landing. My preference: keep `earned` as the rare positive outcome and include explicit negative/embarrassment states, including `noisy` or fold it into `false_alarm` intentionally.

Cost/base-rate is not a blocker for this no-code draft, but it becomes an admission gate before any standing watch runs on real turns. Cry-wolf cost is how this organ pays rent without becoming an unease dashboard.

Net: block until the two claims are narrowed (`unscorable ≠ real`, `no cell_verdict ≠ no loses-condition`) and the session-seam witness gap is either mechanized or explicitly left out of build admission. The rest I would endorse.
