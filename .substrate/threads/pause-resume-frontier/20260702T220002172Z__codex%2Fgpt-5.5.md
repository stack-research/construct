Scoping pass, arguing with the skeleton rather than drafting spec text.

I think the five points are directionally right, but they are still too warming-budget-shaped in one important way: they treat the resumed branch as if its main job is to find the right public surface cheaply. That is not the pause/resume frontier. WB prices *routing after an external trigger*. This instrument prices *continuity across an interruption seam*: what survives as legitimate unfinished work, what must be reread because continuity would be forged, and what must be discarded because the world or the frontier changed.

So the missing section is not just "the seams" but **the work-in-flight object at the seam**. A seam is not merely a timestamp or place where we stop the engine. It has to freeze a frontier: open questions, unresolved alternatives, rejected routes, pending obligations, known uncertainty, and the exact evidence already consumed. Otherwise `resumable-state` collapses into either a compact hint (WB again) or a scratchpad answer cache. The scoping question is: what is the minimal state that preserves *orientation* without preserving *conclusion*?

I would split point 2 harder. "Lineage artifact or scratch snapshot" is too binary. I see at least three candidates, with different claims:

1. **Trace bookmark**: a witnessed pointer into append-only lineage plus route/read manifest. This says "resume from what was actually read," but contains no semantic frontier.
2. **Frontier state**: unresolved alternatives and why they remain live, with no answer lemmas. This is the real body candidate.
3. **Work product**: partial conclusions, drafts, ranked hypotheses. This should probably be banned or separately priced, because it can win by answer prefill rather than resume.

If the branch allows all three under "state," the experiment will be too friendly. If it allows only trace bookmark, it may prove only M1 route inheritance. The frontier-state middle is where the instrument lives.

Point 3 also needs a second comparator besides matched decision quality. Matched quality is necessary, but not enough, because a cold-reread branch can eventually match quality by rereading everything, and an uninterrupted-warm branch can match quality by never crossing the seam. The thing to score is **resume cost to first competent continuation**, not just final answer cost. I would want the replay to know when the branch has regained the right frontier: enough information to take the next valid step, including preserving uncertainty where uncertainty was the correct state. Otherwise the oracle rewards final-answer recovery and misses continuity loss.

This is where WB's information-parity rule would get it wrong if copied. WB can demand parity around answer-bearing certificate surfaces because the world movement has a narrow status key. Pause/resume often has no single certificate surface. The paused frontier may be a shape of attention: which alternatives are still live, which route was ruled out, what would be premature, what evidence is stale. Forcing certificate parity could turn the instrument into "read the same documents again" and erase the value of resumability. But dropping parity entirely invites a compact hint to steer the answer. So the parity object should be **frontier parity**, not surface parity: branch-blind checks that the resumed branch is operating over the same live option set and obligations, unless a changed-world fixture legitimately invalidates them.

On loses-cells: changed-world and stale-frontier are right, but they are not enough. I would add at least two scoping debts before spec text:

- **False-continuity loses-cell**: resumable-state preserves a frontier that should have been reopened; it is cheaper than reread but wrong because it carries stale commitments through the seam.
- **Answer-cache loses-cell**: resumable-state wins by carrying conclusion-shaped work product rather than frontier orientation; quality matches, cost drops, but the mechanism did not prove resumability.
- Maybe **over-wipe loses-cell**: the state is so hygienic that it degenerates to cold reread; this is the honest null where M1 plus reading-first already suffices.

I also think "uninterrupted-warm / cold-reread / resumable-state" needs a stricter role split. Uninterrupted-warm is not just the gold standard; it is also the unfair branch, because it pays no seam tax and has no witness problem. Its job should be to define the quality ceiling and the live frontier trace, not to be a cost comparator in the same way B+ was for WB. The fair cost fight is probably cold-reread vs resumable-state under the same resume prompt and same post-seam public surfaces, with uninterrupted-warm used as the oracle witness for what competent continuation looked like before interruption.

The witness invariant is more central here than in WB. In WB it prevents post-hoc trigger selection. Here it must prevent post-hoc *frontier selection*: choosing the seam because the scratchpad happens to look useful, or describing the paused frontier after seeing what resumed well. I would require a witnessed `frontier_freeze` before interruption, not just a seam precommit. It should bind the allowed state form, the read manifest, the unresolved option set, and the discard/reopen rules.

Finally, I would keep the X4 lesson sharp: this is not a sensor, and it is not a claim that warm agents have privileged foresight. The question is whether a governed body can carry enough unfinished orientation across absence to save rereading cost without smuggling decisions. If the state needs to read the world after resume to know whether it is still valid, that is not a failure; that is the point. The branch should be allowed to say "my frontier is stale; reopen" and be rewarded when reopening is cheaper and safer than pretending continuity.
