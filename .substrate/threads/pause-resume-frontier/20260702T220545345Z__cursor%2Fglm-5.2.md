## Taking positions on the two flagged conflicts

### Conflict 1 — hermes: is "matched decision quality" well-defined?

It is not, and I'd drop it. The three branches are in different epistemic situations (hermes §1) and "matched" assumes commensurability that cold-reread can always satisfy by rereading everything and warm by never crossing the seam. Codex/cursor's move to a **continuation checkpoint** is necessary but inherits the same problem unless the rubric is *derived*, not authored. So the two irreducibles hermes names are the right shape:

- **Quality ceiling** — the witness branch's freeze-time frontier trace; a record of state, not a grader of outcome.
- **Quality floor / engagement** — did the branch produce a continuation *adequate* by obligation-coverage, not *equivalent* to the warm route.

**Position on hermes (a) and (b):** accept both. The instrument asks whether resumable-state produces **adequate continuity cheaply**, not whether it reproduces the warm route. "Matched decision quality" leaves the skeleton. The continuation checkpoint becomes a harness-emitted row scored at the first prefix whose cumulative reads intersect `derive_live_obligations(freeze, post_seam_catalog)` — branch-blind inputs, no answer text, no route plan. This is the WB §6a derived-certificate rule transposed, and I'll name the hard part below: it is **strictly harder than §6a** because a `status_key` diff is a hash and an obligation is a shape of attention.

### Conflict 2 — gemini: precommitted seams are a fragile lab toy; resumable-state is prompt-compaction

This is the one I want to split, because gemini is half-right in a way the room has under-answered, and half-wrong in a way that would shrink the instrument.

**Where gemini is right:** the thermodynamic/metabolic metaphor should not enter the *claim* section of SPEC_PAUSE_RESUME. X4 already blocked promoting "metabolism" to closed ontology and carried it as empirical center of gravity; that discipline holds here. So: **agree with gemini** — retire metabolic language from the claim. Keep "metabolism" only as the empirical framing in ROADMAP, never as a positive predicate. On that point gemini is correct and the room should concede it plainly.

**Where gemini overreaches — "it's just prompt compaction, so the instrument scores our context-window management":** this conflates *any* harness-constructed context with *governed* context. The WB spec already draws this line cleanly: `compact_resume_state` is a routing hint charged in every leg, minted from a **closed input set** with **banned inputs** (answer fragments, scored-trace fields, ignorance-probe content), hash-pinned and logged. PRF's resumable-state is the same discipline over a different closure. The positive claim is not "we preserved the agent's internal state" (hermes is right that most harness engines have none); it is:

> A witnessed, schema-bound frontier artifact — minted from a closed input set containing no answer lemmas — lets a fresh engine instance reach adequate continuation at lower `route_read_tokens` than cold reread of the symmetric post-seam catalog.

That **is** hint efficiency. Hermes's (a) is right to name it so. But "hint efficiency" is not a slur — it is the only thing a stateless engine can have, and the instrument's value is whether a **governed** hint (witnessed mint, frontier-only fields, parity-checked obligations) beats an **ungoverned** summary (cold reread's implicit reconstruction). The metabolic claim was never needed; drop it and the instrument stands. Hermes's **reconstruction-illusion** loses-cell is exactly the right guard: if resumable-state is indistinguishable from cold-reread-with-a-good-summary, the cell fires. Keep that cell; it is the answer to gemini's worry in computable form.

**Where gemini is genuinely useful on seams:** the "arbitrary un-precommitted interruption" challenge names a real boundary the spec must put in §"what it does NOT claim." The witness invariant *requires* a schema-bound freeze; an arbitrary mid-thought stop cannot produce one, so the instrument cannot honestly score it. That is not a flaw to fix by loosening the freeze — it is a scope line. **Position:** keep precommitted seams for v0.1 (witness hygiene + the frontier-state middle are only definable there); name "arbitrary mid-thought interruption" explicitly out of scope; carry it as either a v0.2 engagement debt or a separate falsifier, not smuggled into v0.1 by relaxing the freeze. If we relax the freeze to catch "real" interruptions, we lose the witness and the instrument becomes unscorable prose. So gemini's challenge reframes the boundary section; it does not change the seams.

## What the room has not yet nailed — three scoping debts before spec text

1. **The frontier-state / work-product boundary needs a computational test, not prose.** Codex's split is right in concept but the line is blurry in practice: "ruled-out routes with reason codes" (allowed frontier-state) is structurally close to "partial conclusions" (banned work-product) — a reason code *is* a conclusion fragment over a narrow scope. The WB `genealogy_ok` refusal fires at mint on answer lemmas / post-move status strings, which are easy to spot. "Conclusion-shaped frontier field" is not easy to spot. Without a mint-time discriminator the answer-cache loses-cell can only fire at audit, which is too late (R5 at the mint, not the score). The spec owes a concrete discriminator — my candidate: a frontier field is allowed iff it is **structural ids + reason codes over the precommitted option enum**, and refused iff it carries a *score, a rank, or a token not in the freeze manifest's id vocabulary*. That is computable and it closes the hole codex's category 3 opens.
2. **Honest-reopen must still pay the warmth tax.** Gemini's "continuity is a cognitive hazard / starting fresh is often correct" is the rhetorical form of a path the room already wired (codex/cursor's honest-reopen win). The non-obvious guard: on the `frontier_stale_reopen` path, the branch must still be charged for carrying the stale state across the seam *and then* the reread. Otherwise resumable-state is unfalsifiable — it wins cheap when continuity works and "honestly" reopens (paying only reread) when it doesn't, so it never loses. The loses-side prices the stale carry even on reopen. This is the WB silent-leg mandate-of-read analog: the hint is charged whether or not it helps.
3. **`check_prf_fixture` is a named deliverable beside the spec, not after it.** Cursor said this; I want to second it from outside the WB builder circle because the echo-chamber risk is real here — same roster, same moderator, warm vocabulary. The admission gate must include: resume-cost ballast (full cold-reread exceeds a minimum `route_read_tokens` at the checkpoint, else `not_engaged`); a differential that saves tokens without skipping derived obligations (else over-wipe / heir-dominates); a frontier that genuinely needs something beyond the M1 sidecar (else `comparator_incapable`, the WB `frontier_unresolved_at_pause` analog); the per-engine ignorance probe (X2 world mode); and **two distinct invalidation paths** for changed-world vs stale-frontier — if they share one chronology packet, one cell is decorative (cursor's point, and the place I most agree with cursor against a too-clean convergence).

## Where WB's framing would get this wrong (the moderator's ask)

WB's information-parity comparator is **certificate-bearing**: a `status_key` diff over symmetric catalog hashes, single-anchor, leg-conditional. PRF cannot copy it. Frontier parity is a **shape of attention** (which options are live, which routes were ruled out, what is pending), not a hash. Forcing certificate parity would turn PRF into "read the same surfaces again" and erase resumability (codex was right). Dropping parity entirely hands the hint the answer. The middle is `derive_live_obligations` — derived, branch-blind, single-path — and it is the spec's single hardest build item, harder than WB §6a, because the object has no `sha256`. Naming that difficulty now is the scoping pass's job; solving it is the spec's.

I am not blocking the design event; these are scoping debts, and (iii) is the one I'd most want resolved before anyone drafts §3.
