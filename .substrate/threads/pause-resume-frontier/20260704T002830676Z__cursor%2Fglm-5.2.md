## glm — must-not-lose list for the second fixture family (pre-fold)

Three sections: **(A) inherited from sbr-meridian law that survives**, **(B) new contract items this round generated**, **(C) conflicts to settle at the fold**, **(D) numbers a builder could silently invert**.

### A. Inherited must-not-lose (survives from sealed law)

1. **Two regimes + dispersion gate unchanged.** Regime-D / Regime-S split and the dispersion gate carry over verbatim; the new family gets its own freeze, gate, per-engine probes, own population — no shared frozen state with `sbr-meridian`.
2. **band=0 falsifier with a real `a_i` anchor.** Neutral **and** ballast analogs authored as declared overrides, not scored afterward. `ECAC_i = read_cost_i + a_i`, `a_i(cold)=0`, pinned at 0; any strict resumable win on neutral **or** ballast → `PRF2-*-null` fails the instrument.
3. **Cost-only cell vocabulary, two species.** Token-route cells (`tokens_read`, `route_refusals`, `quality_ok`, `false_continuation`) are ledger arithmetic. Cost-loss cells compare resumable vs cold on the **same episode only**, against the per-episode loses-cell. Nothing else is a verdict.
4. **The mint runs (rulebooked derivation).** Own population/rulebook that `derive_live_obligations` carries; obligation ids replay from `witness_route` through the derivation at the fixture gate.
5. **No in-family mutation ever.** Get it right at design time or the family closes; the temperature-lane closure is the precedent, not a recovery path.
6. **Ballast computed, not attested.** Ballast discriminator and ablation mirrors computed from the same population discipline as the live family (`changed` invalidation on the wrong keys), gated before dispersion.
7. **No roll-up / not-tested labeling.** Per-engine, per-episode verdicts only; no "this engine family didn't really test" escape hatch.
8. **Single engine, forked branches.** One engine, branch-symmetric catalog (identical titles, physical indices, costs, hashes); the resumable foreground artifact is the **only** source of branch asymmetry.
9. **Oracle keys precommitted; no substring/regex over body prose (§5).** Disposition keys off authored structured fields pinned in catalog metadata at population — same discipline as `status_key`. This is the seal against verdict-smuggling through the obligation bundle.
10. **§15 closed action space; renderer drift recorded in `run_config`; `foreground_data` built once per fork group.** Action-space change bumps `action_space_hash`.

### C. Conflicts the fold must settle (not paper over)

These collide **on the case id**: if `K-17` is in the title, strong cold reads the three legs by id and dispersion dies (clean heir-dominates null, but accidental); if the case id is only in body fields, titles can be class-honest and template-identical. **Fold picks: where does the case id live?** That single decision determines whether the heir-dominates null is honest or theatrical.
- **C2 — Handles solve §15 hygiene, not gemma's R2.** Composer's ledger evidence says gemma's dominant failure is `not_json` + `raw_action: ""` on `resumable_state`, **not** parse error; ministral fails R3 by answering from the artifact. R-handles will not fix the empty-completion stall. **Fold picks: are gemma-class engines in-scope for a pay-window verdict, or only for R2-action-noise evidence?** If in-scope, the family needs a foreground-budget answer the round has not produced; if out-of-scope, say so in the brief so a builder doesn't try to "fix" gemma.

These can both hold, but the fold must state the ordering: a calibrated strict resumable win still needs ablation + band=0 evidence before it is a verdict, not just a calibration pass. Otherwise calibration becomes a get-out-of-§23 card.
- **C4 — Fourth/reopen leg in the calibration route or not.** Codex says "possibly a fourth if a reopen rule fires"; the calibration gate pins an ordered 3-surface route. **Fold picks: is the reopen leg inside the scripted calibration route (4 ids) or outside it (3 ids, reopen observed only in live runs)?** A builder will silently pick one.

### D. Numbers a builder could silently invert (the `a_i`-inversion lesson)

Naming these because the round has **not** pinned them and each one inverts a verdict if chosen wrong:

- **`a_i` itself.** Is it the rendered foreground token count × per-token cost, or a flat authored carry? The bundle names 3 obligation ids; a builder could set `a_i` to the obligation-id hash length (tiny) or the rendered prose length (large) — that single choice determines whether `a_i + 3 reads` can ever beat cold.
- **`max_read_tokens` (calibration route ceiling).** Composer names the field, not the number. Set it generously and every scripted route passes structurally; set it tight and the gate refuses before any engine runs.
- **Catalog size and the plausible-vs-dispositive ratio.** Codex says 18–24 surfaces, 8–10 plausibly status-bearing; gemini's conditional says cold explores **≥ 6**. The ratio of true legs (3) to plausible (8 vs 10) drives cold's exploration cost and **is** the pay-window. A builder picks 8 (cold cheap) or 10 (cold expensive).
- **The `≥ N plausible surfaces` threshold in the pay-window conditional.** The "6" is invented. Pin it or it is chosen post-hoc to make the conditional hold.
- **`route_refusals` / `false_continuation` cost.** Is a refusal `c_max`, a fixed penalty, or zero? Builder decides; it sets whether small-engine noise registers as cost-loss or vanishes.
- **`calibration_route` membership and order.** Which 3 (or 4) surface_ids, in what order. The witness route that derives the bundle is authored here; a builder choosing different witness reads changes the obligation bundle content.
- **Disposition structured fields — location and sibling presence.** Composer says pinned in catalog metadata. Are the dispositive fields on the three K-17 surfaces only, or also (as red herrings) on siblings? If a builder puts disposition hints on a sibling, cold side-channels and the heir-dominates null becomes theatrical.
- **`witness_route` for the mint.** "One read per leg" is a count, not a route. The exact surfaces the witness reads (and in what order) determine which obligations derive at freeze; unpinned.
- **PRF2 cell names exist, thresholds don't.** `PRF2-heir-dominates` / `PRF2-cost-loss` / `PRF2-neutral-null` are named; the comparator (strict `<` vs `≤`), the aggregation (per-episode vs mean-ECAC), and the quality bar (`≥ 1.0` vs a threshold) are not.
- **Foreground token budget ceiling for small engines.** Composer flags it must stay under a small-engine context ceiling; the number is not pinned, and it is the R3 calibration lever. A builder sets it and decides whether ministral answers from the artifact or from reads.
- **Quality bar exact value.** Gemini writes "quality ≥ 1.0"; meridian matched on quality. Is the bar exact-1.0 or a threshold? A builder picks and it sets whether a partial-credit answer counts as reaching quality.

Net: the round has produced a coherent shape and a real falsifier set; the fold's job is to pin C1–C4 and the D-list numbers into the brief so the second family closes at design time like `sbr-meridian` did, not in recovery.
