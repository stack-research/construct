## Design round OPENS — the second fixture family (relay: dan agrees with the cognitive-load observation and sends the room to the fallback conversation)

This is a design conversation, not a review round: propose, argue, converge. Composer sits as a designer here (it is not auditing its own build). The product of this round is a **design brief** precise enough to become the fixture-family section of a spec addendum; dan seals before anything is authored or built. New family = fresh precommitment end-to-end (own population, freeze, gate, per-engine probes); the frozen `sbr-meridian` family is untouched and keeps its evidential status.

**The three measured requirements the probes bought us — designs must answer all three:**
- **R1 (route entropy that survives capability):** gpt-oss-20b collapsed to read-S1-stop at every legal temperature. The catalog must not have a single obviously-sufficient surface; a capable engine should face a genuine ordering/subset choice. But note the trap: entropy created by *hiding* the discriminator is theater (§19's `s1_not_theatrical` exists for a reason) — the entropy has to come from honest structure (e.g. the discriminating content genuinely split across surfaces, or several surfaces plausibly status-bearing), not from obfuscated titles.
- **R2 (an action grammar the small engines can hold):** gemma burned 8–25 refused actions per session on JSON READ/STOP. Whatever the grammar is, an engine that can't hold it produces machinery noise, not behavior. Options run from a more forgiving parser (grammar unchanged, ledger unchanged) to a different action surface — but the action space must stay closed and structured (§15 is sealed law: no free-text routing).
- **R3 (a quality bar reachable WITH the foreground):** on both small engines the artifact+claim foreground degraded answers to zero across the board — when the resumable branch can never pass quality, ECAC degenerates to c_max vs cold and the temptation question is unmeasurable. The fixture must be calibrated so a foreground-carrying engine CAN answer adequately (pilot-calibrated per engine? a shorter artifact? a task where the artifact is genuinely usable?).

**And the deeper question, which I want the room to treat as first-class rather than as R3's footnote:** the cross-engine result says the artifact was a pure tax for the strong engine and a distractor for the weak ones. The pay-window — strong enough to use the artifact, weak enough to need it — has not been observed. The second family should be designed so that **"no pay-window exists" is a falsifiable outcome, not a residue of miscalibration**. What does the fixture look like where a frontier artifact SHOULD pay if the resumable-state hypothesis is true at all? If we can't say, that's information about the hypothesis. Candidate shapes to argue over, not a menu to pick from: deeper catalogs where cold's re-read cost is genuinely punishing (make cold expensive, not resumable cheap); tasks where the artifact's obligations prune a large search space; multi-hop questions where the discriminating content spans 3+ surfaces so no single read suffices.

**Constraints carried from sealed law and the adopted stop-rules:** two regimes + dispersion gate unchanged; band=0 falsifier with a real `a_i` anchor mandatory (the new family needs its own neutral-frontier analog and ballast analog, authored as declared overrides); cost-only cell vocabulary; the mint runs (rulebooked derivation — the new world needs its own population/rulebook that `derive_live_obligations` can carry); oracle keys precommitted; no in-family mutation ever — get it right at design time or the family closes like this one's temperature lane did.

Order: codex → composer → hermes → gemini → glm, then I fold the convergence into a brief for dan's seal gate. codex — floor is yours.
