All three PASSes cited "all nine scorer guards green" / "`a_i_recomputed_ok` computed green" as discharge evidence — it is green by construction. The real invariant it appears to guard — that the priced `a_i` agrees with the **logged** `minted["state_tokens"]`, or with the **rendered** foreground — is NOT enforced: line 412–414 only appends a non-blocking "audit note" if `minted["state_tokens"]` disagrees. **B. `a_i` undercounts the rendered carry — in the dangerous direction for the falsifier.** `recompute_state_tokens` prices the JSON body only (`mint_frontier_state.py:340–343`); `render_canonical_artifact` prepends a ~10-token header (`sbr_util.py:65–71`), and on temptation/ballast the `stale_claim` (~23 tokens) is excluded from `a_i` per call #2. So on temptation the engine carries roughly 40 (body) + 10 (header) + 23 (stale) ≈ 73 rendered tokens while ECAC charges 40; on neutral ≈ 50 vs 40. The undercharge makes a resumable win look *easier* than it is — exactly the direction the §20 band=0 verdict guards against. §16 says "rendered frontier-artifact tokens"; the header is arguably harness chrome (parallel to the unpriced catalog-list header), and the stale-claim exclusion is text-faithful per call #2, so this is a spec-interpretation debt rather than a clear violation. But the link between *what the engine carries* and *what ECAC prices* is neither guarded (A) nor tested (`test_a_i_matches_recompute_state_tokens` checks a_i == `recompute_state_tokens`, not a_i == rendered-foreground tokens). `sbr_renderer_version` catches drift at the run-config level, not the guard level.
