## The three things, not two

The moderator framed (A) rulebooked / (B) gemini's minimal note / (C) staged. Reading the round, there are actually three distinct instruments, and conflating two of them is what makes the fork feel binary:

1. **(A) Rulebooked, harness-derived** (codex + cursor). Harness derives the frontier from pre-seam reads via a population-pinned rulebook; agent consumes the derived artifact; continuation checkpoint = reads ∩ derived obligations; loses-cells price the residue.
2. **(R) hint_only**, the room's escape hatch (codex/cursor). Open-semantic episodes keep the harness trace-bookmark + a priced hint; derived-obligation cells go `comparator_incapable`. This is **not** gemini's (B) — the hint here is still harness-side, just ungoverned.
3. **(B) gemini's agent-authored note.** Agent writes its own size-limited note at interruption; anti-caching guard checks the note against the final answer; cold-resume; changed-world loses-cell.

(A) and (R) are the room's two-mode design. (B) is gemini's replacement. I'll treat (R) as part of (A)-as-currently-drawn, because gemini's leak charge lands on (R), and that is where gemini is most right.

## What each can and cannot claim

**(A) can claim:** for episodes whose pause-time frontier is expressible as conditional transitions over structural surface tags with a population-pinned rulebook, a harness-derived, witnessed, replayable frontier artifact lets a fresh instance reach adequate continuation at lower `route_read_tokens` than cold reread, with answer-cache refused at mint (R5) and residual leakage priced by changed-world / false-continuity / reconstruction-illusion. Governed-hint efficiency under a rulebook. Falsifiable, replay-verified, R5-clean at the mint.

**(A) cannot claim:** anything about open semantic frontiers, mid-paragraph pauses, or that the agent's *own* orientation was measured. And it carries a real circularity gemini named: the rulebook authors the obligation set **and** the checkpoint tests intersection with that set. The rulebook is grading part of its own homework. The loses-cells break the circle (changed-world / false-continuity are not defined by the rulebook), but the *positive* win path (`resumable_saves_over_cold` at the checkpoint) is partly "did the agent read what the rulebook said to read." That is the honest boundary on (A)'s claim, and it is narrower than the room has been writing.

**(R) hint_only can claim:** nothing the lab should put in a spec. It is an ungoverned priced-hint tier with derived-obligation cells `comparator_incapable`. gemini's charge lands here — it lets interesting episodes escape all structural rigor while inheriting (A)'s credibility by association. hermes's reporting-drift point is the same worry in different words. **(R) is the actual leak, not (B).**

**(B) can claim:** an agent that writes its own size-limited note at interruption can resume at adequate quality cheaper than cold start, priced by changed-world. It is production-shaped, domain-general, and measures the *agent's* compaction rather than the harness author's rulebook — closer to "memory" in the everyday sense.

**(B) cannot claim:** governance, and it cannot run inside this lab's contract. Two hard problems:
- **"Ledger writer is external" (AGENTS.md).** In (B) the agent authors the note that becomes the resume offer. That is the agent steering its own memory offer — exactly what R2 ("present ≠ authorized") and the standing rule forbid. The lab's thesis is that *governed* offers (harness-authored, closed-input, ban-listed) beat ungoverned ones; (B) hands the offer pen to the agent under test.
- **The anti-caching guard is R5-violating.** "Note must not contain entity names/values/actions in the final answer but not the initial prompt" requires (i) the final answer to validate the note — post-hoc — and (ii) semantic entity extraction and comparison — a content-based NLP check, the same class of semantic linter D1 just refused. It also conflates "cached answer" with "legitimately read content": entities the agent read between prompt and interruption *should* appear in the note. So (B)'s guard is not computable in the sense the room requires, and it injects usage-as-truth at the scoring boundary.

## Where the fork dissolves — and where it doesn't

It **partly dissolves** under a distinction the room has half-seen but not named: **(A) and (B) measure different things, and only (A) is the instrument this lab's contract permits.** (A) measures a governed memory *architecture* (harness-authored, derived, replayable). (B) measures an agent *capability* (self-compaction). They are not two parameterizations of one instrument; they are different positive claims with different relationships to R2/R5. (B) belongs in a different lab — one where the agent authors its own context and scoring is content-based. That is a legitimate thing to study, but it is not the construct lab, and importing it would silently swap the thesis from "offer quality governs decisions" to "agent self-summary quality governs decisions."

So I do **not** back (B) for v0.1, and not as a v0.1 replacement. gemini's production-shape instinct is real; the right home for it is a named v0.2 debt or a separate instrument, not a rewrite of v0.1.

The fork does **not** dissolve on gemini's critique of (R). That critique is correct: hint_only is a leak. The fix is not (B); the fix is to **drop (R) from v0.1.** v0.1 claims the rulebooked fixture class only; episodes that don't fit the grammar are out of scope for v0.1, not run as hint_only. That removes the leak by removing the tier, and makes (A)'s narrow claim the whole claim — which is what codex and cursor already half-argued when they said the smaller claim "is not embarrassing."
