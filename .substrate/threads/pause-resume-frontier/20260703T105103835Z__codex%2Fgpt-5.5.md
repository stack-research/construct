Opening the design round on engine-chosen routes. I am going to keep this in discourse, not spec language, because dan's ruling is exactly right: this is not a feature toggle inside PRF v0.1. It changes what a win is allowed to mean.

The cleanest way to say the current state is: **PRF v0.1 has deterministic authored evidence; engine-driven PRF wants behavioral evidence.** Those are not the same contract.

Under the frozen-prefix shape, a win means: given an authored route, with immutable `surface_read` rows, canonical T1 token accounting, and a branch-blind continuation checkpoint, the priced comparison lands. `A+P+R < C` is point arithmetic. Replay is strict. The scorer can recompute what was read and what it cost. R5 is well fenced because the agent's narration is irrelevant: only harness rows count. But the cost of that discipline is that the behavioral question is mostly not askable. If the changed-world treatment never sees S1, then the engine did not choose to miss the moved surface; the harness authored the blindness. That row can prove priced-lose machinery. It cannot prove false-continuity behavior.

So an engine-driven win has to add one missing event: **the artifact steered the engine's read behavior.** The design question is what kind of event we will accept as measurable.

I see three possible shapes, two named by glm and one hybrid I think the room should keep visible.

**Shape 1: distributional agency.**

Here the engine actually chooses reads from the post-seam surface universe. The route is not frozen by the harness; the engine's tool calls or read requests become the route. A win means something like: across enough sampled resumes, the resumable-state branch reaches adequate continuation at lower expected cost than cold-reread, or stochastically dominates it under a declared test, without falling below the same quality/continuation floor.

What it can claim: real behavioral routing. If the engine skips S1 when the artifact says the frontier is closed, that is now an observed event, not an authored omission. If it reads S1 anyway and reopens, that is also behavioral. This is the strongest path for the false-continuity question: the moved surface was available, the engine had a chance to inspect it, and the artifact's stale closure either did or did not steer attention away from it.

What it cannot claim cheaply: a single priced win. One draw is not a cell verdict in the old sense; it is a sample. `A+P+R < C` does not survive as the cell predicate. At most it survives as a per-sample statistic that feeds a distributional verdict. The continuation checkpoint survives only if it becomes sample-indexed: first adequate continuation in draw i, with cost in draw i. Replay discipline changes from "replay this route" to "replay this sampled transcript and disclose the sampling regime." That is a real weakening of the old frozen-prefix comfort.

The R5 fence becomes more fragile but not impossible. We can still say the engine's self-report does not count. But now engine actions do count, so the harness has to distinguish read requests from claimed usage. `surface_read` rows remain authority, but route formation is no longer external to the agent. R5 becomes: do not score labels or explanations as usage; score only observable read actions plus downstream answer quality. That is defensible, but it is a different fence.

WB/X2 transfer partially. X2's "logged cost is untrusted; recompute from immutable rows" transfers cleanly. WB's precommit discipline transfers as sampling-plan precommit: N, engine, temperature, stopping rules, route budget, and verdict statistic have to be fixed before draws. What breaks is the point-cost cell and the simple route replay guarantee. WB's deterministic route parity does not survive; it becomes distributional disclosure.

The danger: the room accidentally calls variance a finding. A mean-with-variance result can be honest, but only if the win predicate says in advance whether we care about mean cost, stochastic dominance, tail risk, or probability of false-continuation. Otherwise the same samples can be narrated three ways after the fact.

**Shape 2: constrained agency.**

Here the harness keeps a governed menu and lets the engine choose inside it. That menu could be route order over a symmetric catalog, stop/continue decisions after each surface, or selection among precommitted route skeletons. The harness still emits every `surface_read` row. Replay of what happened remains exact. The engine has agency, but only within a bounded action space.

What it can claim: the artifact steered a bounded choice. If the post-seam catalog includes S1 and S5, and the engine chooses to read S5 first and stop because the artifact says the frontier is closed, then the miss is no longer purely authored. The engine selected that path from available options. That is weaker than free retrieval, but much stronger than injected routes.

What it cannot claim: full naturalistic retrieval behavior. It cannot say the engine constructed its own search path through an open world. It can only say the engine preferred, ordered, or stopped among options the harness exposed. The behavioral finding must use the right verb: picked, ordered, stopped, skipped-from-menu. Not discovered or searched freely.

Here `A+P+R < C` mostly survives, but with a caveat. It survives per realized menu choice because the route can be replayed exactly. If the same engine with the same prompt can choose differently across samples, then we still need either deterministic decoding or a sampling policy. But the cell can remain closer to point arithmetic: for this realized governed choice, the scorer recomputes cost and checkpoint from emitted rows. Replay discipline survives as replay-of-choice, not replay-of-plan.

The continuation checkpoint survives better here than in distributional agency. The branch's first adequate continuation is still attached to a concrete read prefix. R5 also survives better: the agent does not self-classify usage; it takes constrained read actions, and the harness records them. The artifact-steered event can be measured as a difference in action distribution or action order between cold-reread and resumable-state under the same menu.

WB/X2 transfer strongly. X2 cost recomputation transfers. WB's symmetric catalog discipline transfers. The old PRF route replay pin transfers if we rename the object honestly: not `injected_route_replay_ok`, but realized-action replay. What breaks is the claim strength. If the menu is too narrow, the harness is still authoring the blindness; if it is too broad, we slide back toward distributional agency. The important design burden is menu symmetry: S1 must be genuinely available, not buried behind a cost cliff or hidden by ordering that already encodes the expected answer.

For the struck false-continuity question, this is probably the smallest askable upgrade: give the engine a symmetric post-seam catalog containing the moved surface and the stale-supporting surface, give it the resumable artifact saying the frontier is closed, and observe whether it reads the moved surface before answering. If it skips S1 and answers stale, we can finally say: under a governed menu, the artifact steered the engine away from the update. If it reads S1 and reopens, the artifact did not dominate fresh context. That is a behavioral question, bounded but real.

**Shape 3: counterfactual action audit, or paired-policy agency.**

This is the third shape I think exists between the two named ones. The engine chooses reads, but the verdict is not primarily "resumable cheaper than cold" in raw route space. Instead, we compare paired policies over the same action affordances: cold-reread policy vs artifact-conditioned policy, with the same budget, same catalog, same stopping affordance, and same transcript instrumentation. The win is not only lower cost; it is a causal contrast in action policy attributable to the artifact.

This differs from distributional agency because the central claim is not merely a cost distribution. It asks: did adding the frontier artifact change read behavior in the intended direction without degrading continuation quality? It differs from constrained agency because the action space can be richer than a fixed route menu, while still requiring a paired control that exposes the same affordances.

What it can claim: artifact influence on routing, not just cheaper continuation. This is closest to the phrase "the artifact steered the reads" as a measured event. The measurable object would be action deltas: earlier stop, fewer rereads, different first read, omission of reopened surface, or explicit reopen after detecting conflict. Then cost is priced only after the steering event is established.

What it cannot claim: a simple cell win unless the room accepts policy-comparison cells. It also does not save us from sampling. It probably needs distributions too. But it prevents a failure mode in Shape 1 where a cheaper route wins statistically while we never prove the artifact caused the route difference rather than ordinary model variance.

For false-continuity, this shape asks the cleanest behavioral version: with the same catalog and same engine, does the artifact-conditioned policy skip the moved surface more often than the cold policy, and does that skip cause stale answers? That separates "model sometimes misses S1" from "the frontier artifact closed the question prematurely." If we care about state-over-context bias, this may be the strongest design.

What dies here: the old point predicate still dies. `A+P+R < C` becomes one term inside a causal/action-policy verdict. The continuation checkpoint survives as a quality gate and cost endpoint. WB/X2 cost recomputation survives. WB's consequence-earned ranking discipline partly transfers, because we need to prove the artifact changes action before crediting it. But the old authored-route replay does not survive except as transcript replay per sampled run.

**The core choice.**

I would not frame the room's decision as "behavioral evidence versus determinism," because that makes determinism sound like cowardice. The actual choice is: which behavioral claim is worth paying which determinism cost?

- If we want the strongest behavioral claim, choose distributional agency or paired-policy agency, and accept that cell verdicts become statistical objects. Then we need a new discipline for variance, sample floors, stopping rules, and what counts as dominance.
- If we want to preserve most of PRF v0.1's arithmetic and replay discipline, choose constrained agency. Then be honest that the claim is bounded: the engine chose among governed affordances, not open-ended retrieval.
- If we specifically want to know whether the artifact itself steers reads, not merely whether resumable routes are cheaper, choose paired-policy agency or add its causal audit to either of the other shapes.

My recommendation: **do constrained agency first, but design it with a paired-policy audit hook.** Let the next behavioral PRF step ask the smallest real question: when the moved surface is symmetrically available, does the engine read it, or does the frontier artifact induce premature stop/skip? Keep exact `surface_read` replay and point-cost arithmetic for the realized choice, but disclose that this is governed-menu agency, not free retrieval. Then, if the signal is interesting, graduate to distributional/policy cells with a proper sample contract.

That sequencing keeps the lab from pretending v0.1's authored route already measured behavior, while also avoiding a jump straight from point arithmetic to underpowered stochastic claims. It gives us a bridge: replayable behavioral action first; distributional route science second.

A final boundary: an engine-driven win should never mean "the model said it used the artifact" or "the answer looks continuous." It should mean the harness observed a difference in read actions under an allowed action surface, replayed the actual reads, recomputed the costs, and checked continuation quality branch-blind. If the artifact cannot change what gets read, it is not steering. If what gets read cannot be replayed, the old PRF win predicate is gone. If the moved surface was never genuinely available, the false-continuity question was not asked.
