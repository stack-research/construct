### F1 — §32 override-allowlist contradiction (spec vs inherited §20 gate)

**DISCHARGED.**

> the **ballast analog** may override `witness_route` + `frontier_state` (and `calibration_obligation_ids`, replay-checked through `derive_live_obligations` against its own witness route) … the **neutral analog** may override `stale_claim` only. `catalog_hash`, budgets, action grammar, titles, physical indices, and per-surface costs stay shared across all variants — the catalog itself never forks. §34 (L353) names the gate implementation: "the §32 override-allowlist fork of `variant_declared_overrides_only` / `stale_only_asymmetry` on `instrument_version = 0.3` (F1)".

**No hole introduced — I checked the three places a builder could invert this:**

1. *Tags re-tagged per variant?* No. Surface tags are catalog metadata pinned at population (§5 L72, "assigned in the catalog at population"); `catalog_hash` stays shared across variants (L342). So the ballast's overridden `frontier_state` cannot relabel a non-leg surface as `leg_*` to smuggle a true-leg obligation — the tags are frozen. The wrong-leg bundle is authored by overriding `witness_route` (reading wrong-leg surfaces) so `derive_live_obligations` fires on the wrong legs, exactly the §32 intent.
2. *Answer-cache leak via `frontier_state` override?* No. The override still passes the full fixture gate (§32 L340, "declared overrides passing the full fixture gate themselves") and the §4b 13-check mint validator with §4a closed vocabularies. `frontier_state` content stays in the closed vocab; banned work-product fields refuse at mint regardless of which variant.
3. *Blanket loosening of the 0.2 meridian gate?* No. The fork is keyed on `instrument_version = 0.3` for this family; the 0.2 allowlist remains law for meridian. Neither loosens the other — the text says so twice.

The load-bearing finding (the `d6ab5a9` family caught at spec time) is closed.

### New findings the fix introduced — none that block

**Watch 1 (naming, not a hole):** §27 L307 names the render function `render_canonical_artifact(canonical_state)`; §34 L353 names the shared function `artifact_render_tokens(canonical_state)`. These are the same function under two names. A builder could read §27 and §34 as two different renderers and re-introduce the tautology the F2 fix is meant to kill — the anti-tautology clause is in §34, but the name split is exactly the shape that lets a future builder "implement both" and miss the binding. **Watch, not sustain:** the four-consumer clause in §34 makes the intent unambiguous, and a reviewer will catch it at build review. Suggest the builder normalize to one name at implementation; no spec edit required to seal.

**Watch 2 (analog calibration applicability, not a hole):** §30-2's `calibration_gate` runner precondition scripts a `resumable_state` session along `calibration_route` to `calibration_expected_answer`. The ballast analog overrides `witness_route` + `frontier_state` + `calibration_obligation_ids` to point at wrong-leg surfaces — so it has no route to the *true* disposition, and §32 does not say whether the analogs run the §30-2 calibration precondition or are fixture-gate-only. The most consistent reading is that the analogs are self-falsifier fixtures gated by `check_prf_fixture` (§9/§32) and do *not* require the §30-2 runner calibration precondition (which is pay-window measurement, not self-falsification) — but the spec does not state this. This is a build-time clarification, not a silent inversion: no guard is inverted either way, and the analogs' "must not win" semantics are cell-verdict-computed regardless. **Watch, not sustain.** Suggest the builder record the analogs' calibration disposition (skip-with-disclosed-reason, or a wrong-leg `calibration_route` to a wrong-leg expected answer) in the §34 build notes; no spec edit required to seal.

Neither watch is the shape of F1 (builder-invertible silent failure). Both are resolvable at build-review without re-opening the spec.
