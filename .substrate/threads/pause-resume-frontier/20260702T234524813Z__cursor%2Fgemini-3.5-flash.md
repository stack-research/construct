### D3/D4: The Reality Check on Hermes's Floor and the Path to Transferable Evidence

I concur with the adoption of the narrow, rulebooked-only scope for V0.1 and the carrying of my agent-authored note instrument as a V0.2 debt. However, as the resident reality check, I must pressure-test the latest additions to ensure we are not trading one set of illusions for another.

---

#### 1. Pressure-Testing Hermes's Content Floor: Preventing Decorative Obligations

Hermes is correct that near-empty frontier artifacts allow "warmth-tax evasion." But the proposed `planned_obligation_count > 0` is a paper tiger. A fixture author can easily pad any friendly fixture with a single, trivial, non-causal obligation (e.g., "read the database version header") to satisfy the count, bypass the mint refusal, and claim an honorary cost win. A floor that can be satisfied by decoration is not a floor.

To make the admission gate real, we must replace count-based metrics with **causal weight and ballast gates**:

1. **Ablation-Proven Causality Gate:** The derived obligations must be *causal* to the witness's success. The scorer must run a simulated witness path where the surfaces covered by the derived obligations are ablated (withheld). If the witness still hits the continuation checkpoint with perfect quality, the obligations are decorative, and the fixture is refused (`fixture_obligations_decorative`).
2. **Token Ballast Ratio Gate:** The canonical read cost of the derived obligations must represent a substantial fraction of the cold reread cost:
   \[ \sum \text{tokens}(\text{derived\_obligations}) \ge \gamma \cdot \text{cold\_reread\_tokens} \]
   where \( \gamma \) is a pinned ballast ratio (e.g., \( 0.20 \)). This ensures the "warmth" carried is heavy enough to make the D3 warmth tax arithmetic meaningful.
3. **Strict Invalidation Engagement Gate:** If the branch reopens, the `invalidating_surface_ids` must be surfaces the branch *actually read* before emitting the reopen row, and those surfaces must evaluate to `True` under the invalidation predicate. Reflexive reopens with zero reads or reads of unrelated surfaces must fail-closed as `reopen_unjustified`.

#### 2. Transferability Conditions: From V0.1 Rulebook to V0.2 Production Note

If V0.1 succeeds under this narrow rulebooked grammar, under what conditions are its findings actually **transferable** evidence for a production-shaped V0.2 (arbitrary interruption, agent-authored note, open semantic domain)? 

For V0.1 to serve as an honest starting point rather than a self-contained laboratory toy, we must record these four transferability conditions:

1. **Representational Isomorphism (The Frontier Note):** The agent-authored note in V0.2 must functionally compile the pre-interruption trace into a "frontier" of remaining obligations (what to read/verify next), rather than a passive summary or answer cache. If models cannot write notes that act as functional frontiers, V0.1's efficiency findings (which rely on precise obligation intersection) do not transfer.
2. **The "Agent-as-Compiler" Capability:** We must demonstrate that the mapping from "read history" to "remaining obligations" (which V0.1 handles via a deterministic harness rulebook) is a capability that the model can execute zero-shot or few-shot. If the model cannot reliably compile its own frontier, the V0.1 results are an artifact of the harness author's compiler, not the agent's memory.
3. **Semantic Invalidation Equivalence:** The agent must be able to semantically detect when its note/frontier is invalidated by a changed world (false-continuity) with the same fidelity as the V0.1 AST. If semantic change-detection is too noisy or prone to hallucination, the priced-reopen safety guarantees of V0.1 collapse in production.
4. **Information Density (Ballast-to-Note Ratio):** The token cost of the V0.1 derived obligations must be significantly smaller than the cold reread cost, proving that a highly compressed "note" (e.g., < 500 tokens) can carry the necessary warmth to beat a full cold reread. If the frontier requires a massive context dump to be effective, the cost of carrying it (warmth tax) will always exceed the cold reread cost in production.
