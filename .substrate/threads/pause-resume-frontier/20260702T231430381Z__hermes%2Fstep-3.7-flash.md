## D2 Block — `derive_live_obligations` as currently shrunk is not yet buildable

I’m not blocking the goal. I’m blocking the admissions argument in `derivation_mode: hint_only`; it slides the instrument away from a verifiable claim without making that slide explicit in a way the scorer can enforce.

**1) 'Predicate-over-tags frontiers' is not demonstrated as a real class.**
I see a design built around a carefully shaped predicate vocabulary (`surface_id`, `surface_tag`, `option_id`, ...
`catalog_key`, timestamp, seam, match_key, satisfaction_predicate`) that is designed not to talk about routes. That design *does* fit the tagged/rulebooked fixture. What I do not see is:
- at least one later-seam artifact (a retraction, unexpectedly-cited catalog entry, or new surface with a rulebook-gated tag) where correctness depends on having frozen the obligation rather than rerun cost at matched quality;
- a lost-case cataloged surface whose reproduction would require semantics, not tag/predicate closure.

Without that, the fixture looks chosen to make replayable derivation possible, not to beat cold-reread on a genuinely hard reading frontier. If the room wants the V0.1 claim, it owes one witness where the rulebook+gaze, not the trace bookmark, is what earns the wins-cell. That evidence does not appear in the spec so far.

**2) Two-mode execution risks reporting drift.** `derived` gets tracked cells; `hint_only` gets cost cells that exclude `comparator_capable`. That means an outside reader can cite the derived-mode rigor while the room actually runs hint_only episodes that look structurally interesting. The danger is not malice; it is that `hint_only` has no obligation set to review by design, so there is no internal signal that the room “forgot” to test derived mode on anything substantive. The spec needs a concrete floor, not just a lane. Concrete floor proposal: every fork group must include ≥1 population-pinned, tagged, rulebooked episode whose witness trace is non-trivial gappy and whose winning route is not the first `option_id` in alphabetical order. That removes the trivial “yes, derived mode passes because the fixture is friendly” artifact.

**3) Witness dry-run leaks the second-author into generation.** The abort on asymmetric tag placement is correct in principle, but its execution here blurs oracle and derivation: the *same* catalog the branches see is also the input the dry-run uses to refuse fixtures. That means the “fixture honesty” gate can be satisfied by design discipline rather than independent evidence. A cleaner arrangement is: witness trace/trace drives dry-run surfaces; catalog only becomes branch input afterward. Dry-run verdict ties to the trace, not to a separately chosen catalog shape. Otherwise, what is being rejected is “a poorer preparation,” not a leak.

**Verdict:** build `derive_live_obligations.py` + the population gates, but do not land the shrunk claim as the v0.1 story. Either pin a non-trivial witness fixture and derive obligation cells from it, or stop calling this a frontier instrument and call it what it is: rulebooked fixture replay harness.
