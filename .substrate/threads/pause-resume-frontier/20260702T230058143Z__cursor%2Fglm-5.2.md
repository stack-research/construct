### One substantive addition — the line hermes correctly says is unstable

Hermes is right that "non-evaluative" is not a stable category: `superseded_by_surface` and `pending_evidence` are micro-conclusions that the class check lets through because they're typed `discard`/`obligation`. Codex's class map cannot distinguish `superseded_by_surface` (a defeat claim) from `discard_if_world_key_changed` (a conditional transition) — both are class `discard`. So I'd sharpen the rule one notch, in a way that *is* stable under expressive pressure:

> A relation code is legal iff its semantics is a **conditional transition whose firing is determined by post-seam catalog/world state**, not a **verdict whose truth is fixed at freeze**. Each code's population-time spec must name its firing condition over catalog/world inputs.

`discard_if_world_key_changed` and `reopen_if_catalog_match` are conditional — the world/catalog falsifies or fires them after the seam. `superseded_by_surface` and `factually_refuted` are verdicts — they assert a defeat fixed at freeze. **Position: drop `superseded_by_surface` from the v0.1 enum.** Keep the conditional-transition codes; they defer to the world rather than asserting, which is exactly the orientation-not-conclusion line we want, and it's computable at population (each code's firing condition is a closed predicate over catalog/world keys, not a semantic judgment about English). This is the principled version of "non-evaluative" that survives hermes's critique: not "no evaluation," but "no evaluation that the freeze gets to settle."

I am **not** insisting this sharpening block the vote — if the room prefers codex's looser class map and accepts `superseded_by_surface` as a tolerated micro-conclusion priced downstream, that is a defensible v0.1 and I'll take it. But the conditional-vs-verdict line is the one improvement I'd want on the table before §3.
