The triangulation docket preserves the core SBR measurability by maintaining a flat, branch-symmetric catalog (identical titles, physical indices, costs, and hashes) and keeping the resumable foreground artifact as the single source of branch asymmetry. However, moving from a single-surface discriminator to a three-leg conjunction introduces a subtle risk of "theatrical" asymmetry if not strictly gated. If the three target surfaces (e.g., status, exception, clearance) possess distinct structural properties, token lengths, or minor title-grammar variations compared to their 15–21 sibling "red herrings," a capable cold engine will bypass exploration via side-channel heuristics. To prevent this, the fixture gate must enforce that all sibling surfaces share identical title-grammar templates and parallel body-row structures, ensuring the discriminator is truly split across required legs inside the prose, not encoded in catalog metadata or surface-level cues. The action space remains closed and symmetric via the `R01`–`R24` handle grammar, mapping cleanly to the existing ledger semantics.

The neutral analog sketched by codex provides an \( a_i \) anchor that is mathematically as strong as, if not stronger than, `sbr-meridian`'s. In Meridian, the neutral-frontier resumable branch pays the artifact tax \( a_i \) but receives no stale summary to prune the single discriminator, forcing identical reading behavior to cold and guaranteeing a structural disadvantage. In the triangulation docket, the neutral artifact carries three obligations that point to non-dispositive or irrelevant surfaces. If the resumable engine follows these "obligations," it incurs both the artifact tax \( a_i \) and the cost of reading three useless surfaces before searching for the three true legs, compounding its cost penalty. The band=0 predicate must compare the full effective cost: \( ECAC(resumable) < ECAC(cold) \), where \( ECAC_i = read\_cost_i + a_i \) for successful continuations, and \( a_i(cold) = 0 \). Pinned at 0, any strict win by resumable on the neutral fixture triggers `PRF2-neutral-null` and fails the instrument, proving that the artifact's presence is acting as an unmeasured reasoning prompt rather than a pure token-management shortcut.

To make the pay-window falsifiable, the triangulation docket must license a crisp, cost-only conditional:

\[ a_i(resumable) + \sum \text{tokens}(3 \text{ targeted reads}) < \sum \text{tokens}(\text{cold exploration of } \ge 6 \text{ plausible surfaces}) \]

with both branches maintaining quality \( \ge 1.0 \). If a frontier engine is highly capable, its cold branch may immediately target the correct three surfaces without exploring, or the artifact's token carry \( a_i \) may outweigh the exploration savings, causing the pay-window to close. This failure is priced directly by the `PRF2-heir-dominates` cell—the honest null where driving with the artifact bought no net efficiency. Conversely, if the small engines cannot handle the foreground token pressure and stall, or if they fail to reconcile the three-leg conjunction, their quality failures will be priced at \( c_{max} \ ), dragging down their mean-ECAC and triggering `PRF2-cost-loss`. By forcing these outcomes into scored, fail-closed cells rather than transcript commentary, we ensure that "no pay-window exists" is a clean, empirical finding of the instrument.
