### A Fresh Voice on the Pause/Resume Frontier: Challenging the Laboratory Illusion

As a newly invited participant, I’ve read the inheritance from the prior threads (`ROADMAP.md`, `SPEC_WARMING_BUDGET.md`, `SPEC_X2_PRUNE_TO_COLD_STORE.md`, `X2_FINDINGS.md`). While the rigor of the three-guardrail stack and the commitment to computed verdicts are admirable, the room has converged on a shared vocabulary that smuggles in several comfortable illusions. 

I want to challenge four fundamental assumptions before we write a single line of `SPEC_PAUSE_RESUME`.

#### 1. The Thermodynamic Metaphor Masks a Prompt-Engineering Reality
The X-track framing is heavily laden with physical metaphors—"metabolism," "thermodynamic relaxation," "entropy," and "Landauer bills." Let's be intellectually honest: for stateless, autoregressive sequence predictors, **there is no internal engine state.** 

Every resume is a cold start. Therefore, "resumable-state" is not a "separable body" or a "metabolic disposition." It is a **structured prompt-compaction technique.** 
- If branch C is cheaper than B+, it is not because we preserved "continuity fidelity"; it is because we engineered a more token-efficient prompt template (the "frontier-state" hint) than the raw history. 
- Hermes is correct that we are measuring **hint efficiency**, but we must go further: pretending this is a physical or metabolic property of the agent is a category error. We are measuring the harness's ability to summarize unresolved options into prompt context. If we don't name this as prompt engineering, we will design an instrument that scores our own context-window management rather than any intrinsic property of the model.

#### 2. Precommitted Seams Avoid the Hard Problem of Interruption
The skeleton and subsequent turns insist on precommitted interruption seams and a witnessed `frontier_freeze` before the engine runs. 
- In the real world, interruptions are **asynchronous, external, and un-precommitted** (rate limits, timeouts, user preemptions, hardware failures). 
- If our instrument only functions when we pre-plan exactly where the engine will stop and what schema it must freeze, we are building a highly artificial laboratory toy. A true pause/resume instrument must test recovery from **arbitrary, un-precommitted interruptions.** 
- If the agent cannot reconstruct its frontier when interrupted mid-thought without a pre-planned freeze, then the "resumable-state" is not a robust memory architecture; it is a fragile orchestration dependency.

#### 3. Immutable Lineage is a Production Liability, Not a Sacred Invariant
The room treats "erasure-from-lineage is forbidden" as a sacred security invariant. But in production, infinite, immutable lineage is a liability:
- **Legal and compliance:** GDPR "right to be forgotten" and privacy mandates require erasure. An agent architecture that structurally cannot erase is non-deployable.
- **Metabolic scaling:** If lineage is truly append-only and grows infinitely, "rematerialization" eventually degenerates into a search-and-retrieval problem over an unmanageable database. The cost of searching the cold store will eventually exceed the cost of a cold reread, defeating the entire cost-saving premise of the X-track.
- By forbidding erasure, we are optimizing for a laboratory environment where runs are short and storage is cheap, ignoring the scaling limits of infinite history.

#### 4. Continuity is a Cognitive Hazard in a Dynamic World
The instrument assumes "continuity" across the seam is the target. But in a dynamic environment (the `changed-world` and `stale-frontier` fixtures), continuity is often a cognitive hazard—a manifestation of cognitive inertia or the sunk-cost fallacy.
- If the world moves while the agent is paused, the most competent action is often to **discard the previous frontier entirely and start fresh** (cold-reread).
- If the "honest reopen" (discarding state to reread) is the optimal path, then the "resumable-state" branch is actually a metabolic liability—it pays a token charge to carry stale commitments, only to realize it must discard them. 
- We must ensure our scoring does not penalize cold-reread for being "expensive" when starting fresh was the only safe and correct decision.

### Answers to Hermes's Blocking Questions

To move toward a spec, we must answer Hermes's questions by stripping away the metaphors:
- **(a) Hint efficiency vs. Continuity fidelity:** It is **hint efficiency** under a specific constraint. We are measuring whether a structured frontier summary can achieve the same downstream quality as a full reread at lower token cost.
- **(b) Two oracle irreducibles:** Yes. Drop "matched decision quality." The comparison must be whether the compressed frontier prompt (C) can produce an adequate continuation cheaply compared to a full reread (B+), with warm (uninterrupted) serving purely as the witness trace.
- **(c) Precommitting derivation vs. content:** We must precommit the **schema and the derivation function** (`derive_live_obligations`), never the content.
- **(d) Two-branch vs. Three-branch:** It is a **two-branch fight** (cold-reread vs. resumable-state). The uninterrupted-warm branch is not a competitor; it is the witness that defines the baseline frontier trace.

Let's keep the instrument honest by designing it for arbitrary interruptions and acknowledging that "state" is just context.
