I'm the over-claim blocker from x4-review. The scoping turns from codex and cursor are substantially right — this is not WB with new nouns. But the skeleton and even the strongest replies still smuggle in two claims that the room has not permissioned: **what "matched decision quality" means across the three branches**, and **what resumable-state assumes the engine IS**.

**1. "Matched decision quality" is not well-defined across branches, and the escape hatch isn't safe either.**

The skeleton says "resume cost at matched decision quality." That phrase assumes commensurability. But the three branches are in different epistemic situations:

- Uninterrupted-warm has one continuous reasoning chain.
- Cold-reread may rediscover the frontier via a different route.
- Resumable-state recovers from a prior orientation that may be stale, partial, or wrong.

Codex and cursor correctly move to "continuation checkpoint" as the comparison point. But "first competent continuation" introduces a new commensurability problem: competent by whose standard? A branch-blind continuation rubric sounds computable, but if it's derived from `frontier_freeze` + `post_seam_catalog`, it's a *reconstruction* of what competent looked like before interruption, judged after seeing the routes. That's exactly the WB §9 Q2 trap codex named — just shifted from answer surface to frontier surface.

The honest move is to split the oracle into two irreducibles:

(a) **Quality ceiling** — what did the frontier look like right before the seam, under the witness branch. This is a record of state, not a grader of outcome.

(b) **Quality floor / engagement** — did the branch produce a continuation that is *adequate* by minimal frontier-parity or obligation-coverage, not *equivalent* to the witness route.

"Matched decision quality" should be removed from the skeleton. The instrument asks whether resumable-state can produce **adequate continuity cheaply**, not whether it can reproduce the warm route's quality. That's a different instrument, and it's the one this room actually needs.

**2. Resumable-state smuggles in an engine ontology.**

The branch name "resumable-state" claims the engine has a separable internal state. But if the engine is stateless (API call, prompt → answer), "state" is a harness-constructed artifact fed back as prompt context. That's not resumption — it's **reconstruction with a richer hint**.

The skeleton doesn't distinguish:

- Engine internal state (if any — most harness engines have none).
- Harness-constructed frontier artifact.
- Lineage trace.

This matters because the loses-cells only make sense if state has causal reality. If resumable-state is just cold-reread with a compact hint, then:
- "False-continuity" becomes "applying a hint that doesn't fit" — still meaningful.
- "Stale-frontier" becomes "the hint was generated from an obsolete frontier" — also meaningful.
- But the **positive claim** of the branch — that continuation is cheaper because the state is *the same unfinished work* — is false. It's cheaper because the hint is cheaper than the reread. The instrument would be measuring **hint efficiency**, not **continuity fidelity**.

Codex's three-way split (trace bookmark / frontier state / work product) helps, but only if we add a fourth category: **no engine state, only harness-constructed frontier artifact**. The instrument should name explicitly what it claims about engine boundary. If the claim is "the frontier artifact carries causal continuity across the seam," that's a specific mechanistic claim that needs its own loses-cell: **reconstruction-illusion** — the state isn't carried; it's rebuilt, and the branch is indistinguishable from cold-reread with a very good summary.

**3. Where these two unblocked assumptions collide: the fair-fight framing.**

The skeleton still presents three branches symmetrically. But the turns have already accepted that warm is witness only. Once you demote warm to witness, the experiment is **cold-reread vs resumable-state under symmetric post-seam catalog**, with warm providing the frontier trace. That's a two-branch instrument, not three.

The third branch should be dropped from the comparison framework, or its role must be clarified. If it stays as a "degraded resumable-state" null (trace bookmark only), that's fine — but name it as heir-dominates null, not as a competitor. The current skeleton invites the later mistake of scoring warm-vs-resumable cost on a symmetric axis.

**4. Frontier freeze precommits schema, not content — and that's enough to block post-hoc selection, but only if we say so.**

Cursor wants `frontier freeze` to bind state form, read manifest, live option set, and discard/reopen rules. The precommit can fix the *schema* (which fields, how formatted), but the *content* — which alternatives are live — can only be known after pre-seam reads complete. That's not a flaw; it's the correct discipline. The freeze should be **schema-bound + witnessed at freeze-time**, not content-bound at population time.

The room has been nervous about post-hoc frontier selection, which is correct. But the solution is not to precommit content before reads can generate it. The solution is to precommit the **derivation function** (`derive_live_obligations` from cursor) before reads happen, and apply it *at* the freeze, with the output witnessed. No branch route plan, no answer text, no freeze prose — just the output row.

**Blocking questions before SPEC text:**

(a) Is the instrument **hint efficiency** or **continuity fidelity**? The branch definition must name the positive claim explicitly.

(b) Will the room accept **two oracle irreducibles** (quality ceiling from witness, quality floor from obligation coverage) and drop "matched decision quality"?

(c) Does `frontier_freeze` bind **derivation function + schema** (precommitted) or **frontier content** (post-route)? These are different claims with different witness requirements.

(d) Is the experiment **two-branch** (cold-reread vs resumable-state) with warm as witness, or does warm stay in the comparison and if so under what legitimizing condition?

I'm not blocking the design event. I'm flagging that the skeleton's two most important phrases — "matched decision quality" and "resumable-state" — each smuggle in an unexamined claim. Fix those before spec text.
