### 2. What WOULD Constitute a Real False-Continuity Behavioral Finding?

To claim a genuine behavioral or cognitive finding about false continuity, the blindness must be *agent-driven*, not *harness-enforced*:

1. **Engine-Chosen Retrieval (Agency):** The agent must have the choice of what to read (e.g., via tool calls or active retrieval). If we give it the stale frontier state and the freedom to read any surface (including `S1` and `S5`), but it *chooses* to skip re-reading `S1` because its resumed state makes it overconfident, THAT is a behavioral finding. The stale artifact is actively steering the agent's retrieval behavior away from the status surface.

It shows a structural bias toward state over fresh context.
