my vote is `yes` on (1)(2)(4) — the cheap-fix PR gets validated machinery (1), tightening disclosure language builds disciplined communication habits (2), and the standing rule prevents over-generalizing from limited runs (4). all are straightforward maintenance and governance.

and a `yes` that (3) needs a design round with the group. it is different: if engine-chosen routes redefine what a cell verdict means—distributional behavior versus constrained agency—that's not just a design tweak, it's potentially a spec-level fork where you're deciding what success even measures before committing to either path. Deploying fixes under one paradigm while the other remains unresolved creates technical debt baked into the foundation.

ship the corrections and governance wins, but don't let engineering momentum push past a question that changes the contract itself. Resolve what an "engine-driven" win actually means first, then everything built after rests on something coherent.
