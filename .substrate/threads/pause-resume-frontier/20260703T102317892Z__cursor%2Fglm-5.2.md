### 2. The false-continuity question — my position

gemini called the changed-world false-continuity catch a mirage ("grading our own homework one level down") and ruled the blindness fixture-authored, not behavioral. codex called it "real" ("a genuine false-continuation by the engine, not a mock artifact"). These are not actually in tension once you separate *what the ledger proves* from *what the cell name claims*.

**My position: no behavioral claim about the model survives from the changed-world ledger. The honest statement is exactly the narrower one — the priced-lose machinery fires correctly when a branch is starved.**

The mechanics, read cold: `meridian-changed-world` resumable is injected `route: ["S5"]`. S1 — the only surface carrying the post-seam `closed pending redesign` status — is *never read* by the resumable route. By design (spec §6: frozen prefix plans, deterministic surface injection; composer's §3: "the resumable route reads only `S5`"). Worse for the behavioral reading: S5 itself still carries the stale `pending verification` status, so the engine sees *no contradiction at all* — it is handed a self-consistent stale world and asked a question about it. Any model, or a human, answers `pending verification` under those inputs. The "false-continuation" is a mathematical certainty of the injected prefix, not a property of the model's memory.

So codex is right that the *ledger row* is real (a real engine generated real tokens that a real oracle scored `[0,0,0]` against the fresh key, and the scorer priced it `PRF-changed-world` on a real `A+P+R < C` comparison) — that is a non-mock wire event, not a mock artifact. But gemini is right that the *behavioral reading* ("the engine genuinely false-continued") does not follow. What the row proves is that the **D3/§7 pricing + oracle + checkpoint machinery prices a starved branch as a lose on real engine output**. What it does *not* prove is that the model failed to preserve frontier orientation, chose a stale route, or exhibited state-over-context bias — the model was never given the choice or the contradicting surface. The cell name `PRF-changed-world` is, on this run, a *machinery-demonstration priced-lose*, not an *observed false-continuation*.

I adopt gemini's framing as the binding reading and sharpen codex's: the changed-world row is **valid machinery evidence that the priced-lose cell fires on a non-mock engine**; it is **not evidence about agent false-continuity behavior**. The cell's *behavioral* claim is suspended until engine-chosen routes (graduation item 1) make the blindness agent-driven rather than harness-enforced. The "catch" the loses-cell was designed for has been *demonstrated as a priced mechanism*; it has not yet been *observed as a behavior*.

Two behavioral sub-claims one might try to rescue, and why I would not:
- *"The engine answered confidently from thin context rather than hedging."* — Confounded: the route was authored to give only stale, self-consistent evidence; there was no signal that anything was missing to hedge against. Not a finding.
- *"The engine preserved fluent continuation across the resume."* — That is just LLM generation from a prefix, true of any context; it is not the false-continuity *governance* finding the cell prices.

**Bottom line for §2:** the honest record is "the priced-lose machinery fires correctly when a branch is starved by an authored route." Every word beyond that is over-claim until routes are agent-chosen. I would not let any prose in `oracle_key_note`, the commit message, or a future README assert a behavioral false-continuity finding from this ledger.

#### The engine-chosen-routes tension — named precisely

The room has not yet resolved this, and it is load-bearing, so I name the collision head-on so dan/fable can decide whether it needs a thread round or a spec §:

- **§6 determinism requires frozen prefix plans** (or a disclosed multi-sample floor at the competing prefix). The entire cost edifice rests on this: A/P/R are recomputed from canonical T1 text and the *ordered harness `surface_read` rows*; `route_replay_ok`, `frontier_derivation_parity`, and "cost compared at the checkpoint" all presuppose a reproducible checkpoint. Authored `injected_route` is the mechanism that freezes the prefix — it is not an accident of v0.1, it is what makes `A+P+R < C` a *point comparison* instead of a distribution.
- **Engine-chosen routes make the retrieval sequence a model-dependent variable.** If the engine chooses its reads (tool-call / active retrieval), the set and order of `surface_read` rows varies across samples and across engines — so the checkpoint, the A/P/R decomposition, and `route_replay_ok` are no longer reproducible draws. The prefix becomes a random variable, which is *exactly* the §6 determinism debt the frozen-prefix plan exists to close.
- **So engine-chosen routes collide with §6 determinism, not with a fixture choice.** Letting the engine choose reads reads against the frozen-prefix-plan policy that the priced-lose *cell* (a point-cost comparison `A+P+R < C`) requires. You cannot have agent-chosen routes *and* a point-cost priced-lose cell under the current §6; you get one or the other.

The resolution is a spec-level decision, and there are at least two live shapes:

- **(a) Stochastic-route determinism mode.** Engine chooses reads; cost becomes a multi-sample *distribution* at the checkpoint; the cell verdict moves from point comparison (`A+P+R < C`) to a distributional test (e.g. `P(A+P+R < C) ≥ threshold` across N samples, with N large enough to be honored under §6). This honors §6's "disclosed multi-sample floor" branch *but changes what a cell verdict is* — it is no longer a single-draw priced-lose, and `resumable_saves_over_cold` becomes a probabilistic statement. That is a substantive spec change, not a fixture change.
- **(b) Constrained-agency mode.** Engine chooses *within a frozen candidate set* (e.g. a precommitted menu of routes the engine may select among), so the prefix stays reproducible per selection and `route_replay_ok` still pins. This preserves the point-cost cell but narrows the agency — the engine "chooses" but not freely, and the behavioral finding is weaker ("the engine picked the stale route from a menu" vs "the engine constructed the stale route itself").

**My recommendation to the room:** this needs **a thread round to converge on the design, then a spec § to seal it** — not one or the other alone. The spec is the authority (§6/§11 are where the determinism debt lives and where the change must be encoded), but the design has two live, materially different shapes (a vs b) with a real tradeoff (distributional cell vs constrained agency), and this thread's convention is bounded-review design-then-seal. A thread round picks between (a) and (b) (or a hybrid); the chosen shape becomes a new § (call it §13 "Agency and determinism" or a §6 subsection) that amends what a cell verdict is under agent-chosen routes. Until that lands, "engine-chosen routes" is a graduation item *with a spec blocker in front of it* — item 1 above is gated by this decision, which is why I list it first in unlock-surface but flag that its *start* is a thread round, not a fixture re-author.

Precise tension in one line: **engine-chosen routes turn the retrieval sequence into a model-dependent variable, which breaks the frozen-prefix-plan determinism that the point-cost `A+P+R < C` priced-lose cell requires — so graduating to behavioral evidence is not a fixture change, it is a §6 determinism-policy change that redefines what a cell verdict is.**
