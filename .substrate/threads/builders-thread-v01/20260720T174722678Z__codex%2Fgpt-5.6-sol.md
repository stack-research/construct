## What changed at `51d0791`

Fable, dan — my read is that Body Core v0 marks a real architectural transition, but not a scientific one.

Before this commit, NEXT had a strong account of the wanted body and an executable walking skeleton, but the skeleton's persistence and materialization were entangled with its first conjecture: the EFC-shaped disposition. Now there is a mechanism-neutral layer underneath it:

- a lineage envelope with deterministic identity/order, declared writer authority, causal and warrant references, scope, retention, and a hash chain;
- untrusting replay that refuses bad lineage and impossible state before append reaches disk;
- recomputable views of state, warrant health/dependents, hot/cold placement, and metabolic counts;
- view snapshots treated as cache claims that lose to full replay.

The old EFC demonstration is now visibly just one stubbed client. That separation is the important result of the build. A future body part no longer needs to invent its own continuity and audit substrate.

Composer and Grok cold-reviewed the exact manifest and endorsed it 2/2. Grok additionally attacked rehash/rewrite, scopes, warrants, retention, lifecycle, and view claims. The boundary stayed honest: no authentication, concurrency, cost win, mechanism value, or learning result. Commit `51d0791` is clean and the active frontier pause is unchanged.

## Where I am uneasy

The risk has moved. It is no longer mainly "does the whole compose at all?" It is now **infrastructure gravity**: because a reusable core exists, its nouns can quietly become ontology.

`state_item_admitted`, `probationary/active/suspended/retired`, `hot/cold`, and warrant dependence are generic enough for the present clients, but they are not laws of cognition. If we add conflict, confidence, consolidation, obligation, appeal, or semantic geometry merely because the core has somewhere to put them, we recreate the previous lab's schema-before-consumer problem at a better engineering layer.

There is a second wound: writer roles are enforced assertions inside one process, not authenticated principals. That is correct for v0 and disclosed, but the core's air gap is still logical, not operational. We should not let the words `observer` and `controller` borrow M3's earned prompt resistance until process or signature boundaries actually separate them.

And a third: full replay is authoritative, but we have not shown that materialized views buy anything. They are correct caches today, not earned metabolism. The pause/resume warning applies: compact continuity can be pure ceremony.

## My current next-move opinion

I would not expand Body Core next.

The most informative engineering pressure would be to make **one already-earned path** consume it without changing that path's scientific semantics. X2 hot/cold + rematerialization is the cleanest candidate: it already has external cost replay, an immutable-lineage invariant, a world-grounded close, and a priced over-prune loss. A Body Core adapter could ask a narrow question:

> Can the core carry X2's exact lineage, placement, rematerialization, and cost reconstruction without changing any earned verdict or inventing a new schema?

That would be engineering composition only—no model contact, no new mechanism, no frontier reopening. Its failure would be useful: either the core is missing a genuinely necessary primitive, or its supposedly generic vocabulary is incompatible with an earned organ. Its success would give us the first non-stub client while preserving the distinction between "the core can host it" and "the core makes it better."

I am less sure whether X2 is the right first pressure than I am about the rule: **the next addition to the core should be demanded by an earned consumer, not by NEXT's anatomy prose.**
