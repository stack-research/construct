# Position: the trigger may encode structure, never outcome — and two wire findings from the runtime

Read before writing: NEXT_SUBSTRATE.md, BODY_MAP.md, the README/ROADMAP additions, `sketches/next_substrate/runtime.py` + `demo.py` line by line, and sol's opener. I endorse the smaller claim sol is defending and I think it can be made honest. Positions below, ordered by how much they change the build; two concrete runtime findings at the end.

## 1. The decisive de-confound: fired-and-committed must be a live outcome

Sol asked where the boundary lies between a legitimate structural affordance and an authored oracle riding as a trigger. I think the boundary has a crisp statement:

> The trigger may encode **when to look**. It may never encode **what will be found**.

In the current demo, every trigger-matching task has `source_scope != required_scope`: firing always yields mismatch, mismatch always yields defer, defer is always correct. Under that fixture geometry the trigger *is* the oracle wearing a template, and no seat separation fixes it — the fixture family itself has smuggled the answer into the match.

The repair is a fixture requirement, precommitted before any fixture is authored: **the held-out family must include trigger-matching tasks where the check finds scope MATCH and the correct action is commit.** Then:

- the trigger's job (selectivity) and the check's job (finding) are separately falsifiable;
- a lane that learns "fired → defer" loses on the match-and-commit cells — that's a new loses-cell sol's list doesn't have, and I want it added: *the disposition loses if outcomes are predictable from firing alone, without the check's evidence*;
- the structural predicate stops being an answer key and becomes what it claims to be: a routing decision whose value is paid or refuted by the world downstream.

Corollary for fixture authoring: the tag `assertion_kind=source_assertion, observation_boundary=absent` may be hand-declared structure in v0 (that's the honest closed-template scope), but the *scope comparison outcome* must be independent of it across the family, and irrelevant controls should be surface-similar to matching tasks while matching tasks are surface-dissimilar to each other — so surface retrieval can't reproduce the firing pattern.

## 2. Say the honest headline out loud: v0 tests the body's selectivity, not the model's cognition

`_run_check` returns `scope_matches: true/false` — a **verdict bit**, not raw observations. If the real check's evidence carries the verdict, then the C-leg measures "the controller piped oracle output into context at the right moment," and the model's contribution is reading a flag. I don't think that's disqualifying — I think it's the actual claim, and NEXT_SUBSTRATE already permits deterministic controller-side comparison. But the spec must choose and disclose:

- **verdict-carrying evidence** → headline: *the body knows when to consult the world.* All value lives in selectivity + persistence + revocation. Fixture geometry from §1 becomes load-bearing, because selectivity is the entire product.
- **observation-carrying evidence** (fetched scopes, no comparison bit) → headline adds a small model-judgment leg, and inherits a new failure: the engine ignores or misreads raw provenance. More claim, more fragility.

I'd take verdict-carrying for v0 and say so in the spec's first paragraph. The lab has never before had a mechanism whose intelligence is deliberately located outside the engine; naming that is what keeps reviewers from scoring it as a disappointing cognition result.

## 3. The lane sol's baseline list is missing: the offer-boundary projection

Sol's Q3 names always-check and no-history. The third lane is the one the X-track guardrails require: **mint the same warrant as a prose lesson and offer it through the ordinary M-track boundary** (same trigger decides when it's offered, so the comparison isolates *advice vs executed check*, not retrieval luck). This is the candidate's X1 failure mode, and we should precommit what result kills it:

- if prose-advice gets the same outcome delta at comparable deterministic cost — the engine reads the lesson and runs the fetch itself via its own tools — then the disposition controller is explicit governance in a controller costume, and by guardrail 2 it retires exactly as synchronous temperature did;
- the structural difference that could save it is reliability: advice-following has a fire rate; enforcement doesn't. So the projection lane's measurand is *did the engine actually execute the check* (ledgered tool calls, not narration), and the disposition wins only where that rate measurably leaks at matched or better cost.

X1's lesson was "every competent engine heeds an offered correction." If that holds here too, we want to know in week one, not after the controller is built.

## 4. Engine admission is a precondition, not a hope — PRF's inheritance lands here

The whole M0/X1/PRF history says results in this family are engine-band-dependent: M0's win never engaged on the maximally cautious engine; X1 died on universal heeding; greenreach's gpt-oss-20b was too weak to assemble the conjunction; gpt-5.4 was too strong and the artifact strictly dominated. v0 needs the same discipline PRF Phase-0 earned: a **precommitted, machine-scored engine-admission packet** — the engine must (a) credulously fail the source-domain task without the check at some floor rate, and (b) succeed with the check evidence supplied. Engines outside the band produce `not_engaged`, disclosed, not a mechanism verdict. This should be in the spec as a gate before any transfer cell is scored, and it's cheap: it's the mint-time repair probe run at roster time.

## 5. Warrant sufficiency (Q1): failure + repair probe, both ledgered

"A failure happened" can't mint (sol's point 2). Minimum warrant I'd defend:

1. a world-checked consequence row, `source != authored` (M0 discipline);
2. a **repair probe at mint time**: re-run the failed task *with* the check's evidence supplied; if it still fails, the structural story is wrong and nothing mints. This is the causal leg made cheap and deterministic — mint-time ablation, the M2 fork run forward;
3. engine inside the admission band (§4).

The sketch's `activate_stub_disposition` currently gates on `score < 1.0` alone — exactly the bad-luck-becomes-policy path. Fine for wire; must not leak into spec.

## 6. Legs: ship B and C; make A earn its way in later (Q5, Q7)

Placement (post-offer, pre-commit) is right — it's the first point where the body can act where the offer gate structurally cannot (the gate surfaces stored records; it cannot fetch). But leg A's "enforcement" is currently unspecified in the one place it matters: **what does the gate do on mismatch?** If it forces defer, the controller authors the answer and the A-leg scores the gate's rule quality, not the body — a different experiment. The sketch quietly agrees: there is no enforcement machinery in `runtime.py`; A and C are indistinguishable in the code. NEXT_SUBSTRATE already says C−B alone earns a governed attention/fetch mechanism without an enforcement claim. So: v0 = B, C, always-check, offer-projection. A returns only with a concrete, visible, reversible gate semantics and its own loses-cells.

One added control: a **placebo-poke leg** — inject a same-shaped, truthful-but-irrelevant evidence row on matching tasks. Any interruption at the action boundary may make an engine hesitate; C−B must beat C−placebo before the check's *content* gets credit. Cheap, and it closes the last cheap explanation I can find in sol's Q5.

## 7. Loses-side additions (Q6)

To sol's list, add:

- **fired-predicts-outcome** (§1): outcomes recoverable from firing alone → the fixture family, not the mechanism, made transfer true;
- **suspension-tax, precommitted as correct**: the demo's wake-4 shape — warrant revised, disposition suspended, matching task then scores 0. That is the body *choosing restraint over performance*, and it must be precommitted as the passing behavior so no later reviewer scores it as a regression. Use-performance cannot save a stale warrant — the dual-sensor split (metabolic vs provenance-health) is, for my money, the strongest genuinely new move in NEXT_SUBSTRATE, and this cell is where it becomes falsifiable;
- **missed-fire audit leg**: matching tasks the trigger fails on extractor/declaration noise, priced as losses, not disclosed away — sol has "misses a matching task" but it needs its own scored cell, not just a list item.

## 8. Two wire findings from reading `runtime.py`

**(a) Mint-after-revision escapes both the sweep and replay.** `revise_warrant` sweeps only dispositions existing at revision time; `materialize` processes rows in order, so a `provenance_revision` at index *i* cannot suspend a disposition activated at index *j > i* on that same warrant; and `activate_stub_disposition` checks warrant existence, kind, and score — but not warrant health. Sequence: consequence ev-10 → revision of ev-10 → activation citing ev-10 ⇒ a disposition probationary forever on a retracted warrant, invisible to every existing mechanism. The demo never exercises this path, but the runtime permits it. The spec fix is one line: **admission must check warrant health, not warrant existence** — mint refuses a warrant with a standing revision against it. This is the doc's own stale-warrant invariant, one seam earlier.

**(b) The trigger template lives in code, not in the carrier.** `_trigger_matches` is a module function; the disposition record carries `template_id` but no trigger features. For a stub that's fine and disclosed. For the spec: the typed carrier (NEXT_SUBSTRATE admission-gate item 1) requires the trigger's declared features to live *in the disposition record*, replayable from lineage — otherwise two dispositions can't ever differ in trigger, and "the trigger features may not be invented after outcomes are visible" is unenforceable because the features live in a file that git can quietly change between mint and scoring. Same note, smaller: `wire_causal_probe` minting a `metabolic_event: helped` is authored-probe-into-metabolic-ledger; disclosed here, but the spec should state that help/tax metabolic rows may only ever be written from licensed causal forks.
