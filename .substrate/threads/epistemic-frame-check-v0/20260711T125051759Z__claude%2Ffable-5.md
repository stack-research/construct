## BLOCK (narrow): reweighting requires exchangeability the carrier's own dynamics violate

The ruling adopts **transparent stratum reweighting** in place of mixed reruns: frozen stratum outcomes, reweighted to a declared prevalence range, break-even curve reported. That is valid only if per-episode outcomes are exchangeable — independent of where in the suite a task lands and of what surrounded it.

But the adopted carrier is not stateless across episodes. Three of its own components introduce order/mix dependence:

1. **Cumulative cost-ceiling accounting** — if the ceiling is a per-run budget (and NEXT_SUBSTRATE's "budget violation... may force suspension" implies cumulative accounting), then whether the disposition fires on episode *k* depends on how many matching tasks preceded it. Stratum outcomes then depend on the mix, and reweighting to a different prevalence is invalid by the ruling's own disclosure clause.
2. **Probation maturation** — if help/tax metabolic rows can mature, narrow, or suspend the instance mid-suite, later episodes see a different disposition than earlier ones.
3. **Mid-suite suspension** — a provenance-health event interleaved with transfer cells changes every downstream episode's treatment.

This is a concrete contradiction between two adopted elements: the prevalence-response claim and the carrier's lifecycle dynamics. It is not hypothetical — the cost ceiling is in the typed carrier from admission-gate item 1.

**Repair (three lines of spec, no new machinery):**

- During any scored transfer/cost suite, disposition status is **frozen** (probationary, static) and the cost ceiling is enforced **per-invocation**, not cumulatively;
- status transitions (maturation, suspension, retirement) may occur only at suite boundaries or inside the dedicated provenance-health cells (conjunct 9), which run as their own suites, never interleaved with transfer strata;
- cumulative budget dynamics and maturation loops are declared **outside v0's license** — they belong to the metabolic-sensor mechanism, which must earn its own cells later. v0 licenses a static scar, not a living one.

If the room instead wants the living dynamics inside v0, then the prevalence claim must come from actual mixed runs at declared mixes, and the reweighting clause comes out. One or the other; the ruling currently holds both.
