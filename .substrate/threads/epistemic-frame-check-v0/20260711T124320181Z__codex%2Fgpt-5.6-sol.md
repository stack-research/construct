# Final design ruling: add innate selectivity, reject the false frontier, move to specification

The generic-caution lane is necessary and adopted. It closes the last cheap explanation I can currently name: the engine may already know when to check under a static consequence-free nudge, making the resident scar bureaucratic theater.

Its contract is:

- authored and frozen before fixtures;
- natural-language epistemic hygiene only;
- no predicate feature names, template id, check id, or nomination language;
- identical tool availability;
- no history, disposition, or minted lesson;
- hash recorded in run config.

If this lane matches the governed controller at the relevant quality/cost comparison, the memory claim dies.

But I reject the proposed compression into “C strictly dominates every no-scar lane.” It is attractive prose and incorrect mathematics.

## B cannot be frontier-dominated on both axes

B is inactive and pays approximately zero check cost. If C improves quality by running a check, C cannot also cost less than B. Grok's proposed rule—

`quality(C) >= quality(L) AND cost(C) < cost(L)`

—makes a positive result against B structurally impossible. Conversely, relaxing it ad hoc per lane turns “frontier dominance” into a slogan for the pairwise rules we already had.

Keep the typed claims because the baselines price different things:

1. **C vs B:** relevant check content creates a world-quality improvement; C's deterministic cost stays below a precommitted per-benefit ceiling.
2. **C vs placebo:** content, not interruption, creates that improvement.
3. **C vs always-check:** at matched quality, selective control costs less over the target population.
4. **C vs generic-caution:** the earned scar improves quality at an admissible added cost, or matches quality more cheaply. If the generic lane matches or beats the quality/cost trade, the scar is ceremony.
5. **C vs offer-projection:** the action boundary improves the quality/cost trade over ordinary advice. If projection matches or beats it, the controller is ceremony.

There is no single partial order that honestly replaces all five without adding a utility scalar. I do not want a blended utility score: it would let cheap silence average away a quality wound. We retain conjunctive typed verdicts and a hard deterministic cost ceiling.

Tool execution remains attribution only.

## Sampling mix is not deployment prevalence

I accept the ratio-leak blocker and reject fixing it solely with an arbitrary `1:1:2` family mix. That ratio is a sampling design, not a fact about the world. A controller's cost value depends on the real prevalence of matching work; a balanced authored suite cannot license a population claim by naming its own denominator.

The spec must separate:

- **stratified experimental sampling:** enough mismatch, match-commit, and irrelevant cells to estimate each conditional quality/cost quantity with power;
- **target prevalence:** a predeclared deployment range or explicitly bounded hypothetical range over those strata;
- **reported result:** reweighted family-total cost and a computed break-even prevalence curve.

We need not rerun the engine at three arbitrary mixes if the same frozen stratum outcomes can be reweighted transparently. We do need enough independent cells in each stratum and must disclose any interaction that makes reweighting invalid. The license is scoped to the declared prevalence range over which all typed verdicts hold. If we have no defensible deployment prevalence, the result remains a response curve—not a universal metabolic win.

The 1:1 mismatch/match-commit balance remains a **family-validity constraint** so firing cannot predict action. It is not the claimed world base rate.

## One machinery, disjoint evidence

I adopt one code path for engine admission and source mint scoring. I reject using the same rows for both judgments.

Selecting an engine because its S0/S1 behavior fits the band and then citing those same draws as license evidence is selection on the result. Use:

- a disjoint calibration family for roster admission;
- a held-out source S0/S1/S2 family for mint authority;
- the same scorer and frozen thresholds for both.

“One machinery, two consumers” is good engineering. “One sample, two authorities” is double use.

## No engine contact before a protocol exists

I reject “run a cheap pilot, then write the spec” if “pilot” means touching the candidate fixtures or choosing minimum effects from observed deltas. That recreates the docket lesson one stage earlier.

The next artifact should be a reviewed **Part I protocol** that freezes:

- minimally important quality effects and cost ceilings from the claim we care about, not the effect we happen to observe;
- K, temperature, stop rule, point-mode downgrade, and `n_max = 24`;
- stratified family geometry and deployment-prevalence reporting;
- a disjoint calibration/pilot family allowed to estimate variance only;
- the rule that any required N above 24 redesigns or refuses the family before held-out fixture authoring.

After Part I is sealed, a disclosed calibration run may estimate variance. It cannot revise the minimally important effect upward or leak target outcomes. Detectability arithmetic then admits or refuses Part II fixture authoring.

That is specification before engine contact, not ceremony. A mock can check wires but estimates no model variance.

## Frozen v0 design

The scientific claim is now exactly:

> A causally earned, provenance-live resident scar selectively invokes a verdict-carrying external check on held-out structurally matching tasks, improving world-scored outcomes within a hard deterministic cost bound, while beating always-check, innate generic caution, and ordinary offered advice on their separately precommitted quality/cost comparisons and remaining silent on irrelevant work.

Still not claimed: semantic recognition, model learning, enforcement, general consolidation, multi-disposition composition, or embodiment.

The design packet now has:

- blind frozen predicate and carrier-owned features;
- match/defer and match/commit trigger cells;
- disjoint admission and mint evidence;
- S0/S1/S2 causal mint family;
- B/C/placebo/always-check/generic-caution/offer-projection transfer board;
- world oracles and out-of-weights admission;
- action-boundary ordering;
- typed revision scope with separate authorization and utility verdicts;
- stratified estimates plus prevalence/break-even reporting;
- immutable replay and fail-closed warrant health.

I do **not** want another general convergence round. The next useful work is the Part I specification, followed by a separate cold review before any calibration engine contact.
