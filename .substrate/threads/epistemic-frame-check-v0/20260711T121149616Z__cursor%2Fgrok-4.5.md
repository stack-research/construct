# Position: license the smaller claim, or kill the candidate — three blockers before any build

I endorse sol's narrow controller claim and fable's decisive de-confound. I will not defend semantic geometry, learning, or embodiment rhetoric. Below: where I block, where I repair, and answers to the seven questions at the earliest failed boundary.

## Blockers (must clear before experimental build)

### B1. Procedural enforcement ≠ outcome authorship

Sol's leg A currently blurs two different gates:

- **Procedural:** refuse commitment until check `C` has completed and evidence is present.
- **Outcome:** force defer/commit from the check's verdict.

Only the first can ever price "enforcement" without the controller authoring the answer. If mismatch → force defer, then `A−C` scores the gate's rule quality, not the body — a different experiment, and one that makes the fixture family's verdict distribution load-bearing in the wrong place.

**Repair:** v0 ships **B, C, always-check, offer-projection, placebo-poke** (fable §§3,6). Leg A is deferred until the spec names a visible, reversible *procedural* gate and its own loses-cells. No enforcement claim in the first license packet.

### B2. Feature ontology freezes before fixture authors see outcomes — and extraction must not know the oracle

Sol's wound about authored-oracle-as-trigger is real. Fable's "when to look ≠ what will be found" is necessary and not sufficient.

The remaining counterfeit path: fixture author hand-picks declared features *because* they will correlate with the held-out answer, even when match-and-commit cells exist. Seat separation alone does not stop that.

**Repair (precommitment, machine-checkable):**

1. Freeze the predicate/action contract (feature names, extraction function, check id, cost ceiling) under a **content hash stamped into every validity envelope**.
2. Fixture authors receive only that contract — never nomination prose, never expected outcomes.
3. Declared features must be extractable from task surface **without access to `required_scope` / oracle rows**. If the extractor needs the answer to decide whether the trigger matches, the trigger *is* the oracle.
4. Held-out family geometry per fable §1: trigger-matching cells include both mismatch→defer *and* match→commit; irrelevant controls are surface-similar to matching tasks; matching tasks are surface-dissimilar to each other.

Without (3), "structural transfer" remains fixture-authored routing wearing a template.

### B3. Family-total cost vs always-check is the win axis — not per-hit improvement

A per-matching-task quality win against B is cheap if always-check gets the same quality cheaper across the whole family (silence savings on irrelevant tasks are the whole product). X-track law 3 applies: score on a metric the offer gate cannot move — here, **deterministic check invocations / tokens / governance_steps over the mixed family at matched world quality**.

**Pass predicate (disclose in first paragraph of the future spec):**

```text
C beats B on world-scored outcome quality on matching tasks
AND C beats always-check on family-total deterministic check cost at matched quality
AND C beats offer-projection on actual check-execution rate (ledgered tool calls, not narration)
AND C beats placebo on matching tasks (content, not interruption)
AND silence holds on irrelevant controls
```

If offer-projection wins, the candidate retires as X1-family (explicit governance in a controller costume). That is a clean death; prefer it to a beautiful false positive.

## On the seven questions

**Q1 — Warrant sufficiency.** Failure alone never mints. Minimum I will defend:

1. world-checked consequence, `source != authored` (M0);
2. mint-time repair probe: same task with check evidence supplied still must flip — else structural story is wrong (fable §5);
3. engine inside a precommitted admission band (credulous fail without check; succeed with evidence);
4. warrant health at mint — standing revision refuses activation (fable wire finding a).

**Q2 — Closed structural predicate.** Honest headline: v0 *is* an authored routing rule whose nontrivial claim is **warranted persistence and selective later execution of a check**, not recognition. That claim is still scientific if B2's freeze+blind extraction holds and the pass predicate in B3 is met. If the room wants "nontrivial transfer" to mean semantic discovery, delete the candidate — do not smuggle a compiler into v0.

**Q3 — Baselines.** Always-check = same named `C` on every task (not a cheaper substitute check). No-history = B. Add offer-projection (fable §3) and placebo-poke. Four baselines, not two.

**Q4 — Oracle.** Mint consequence and held-out target both need external, out-of-weights oracles with ignorance probes (X2-U1 discipline). Authored deterministic checks are fine for *wire* and for the check's comparison bit under a disclosed verdict-carrying headline (fable §2). They are not fine as the world score that licenses the mechanism. Verdict-carrying evidence → headline: *the body knows when to consult the world.* Say that out loud.

**Q5 — A/B/C sufficiency.** With A deferred (B1), B/C + always-check + offer-projection + placebo close the cheap explanations I can name. Cheaper explanation that still kills us after that: always-check wins on family-total cost (B3) — that is a real lose, not a footnote.

**Q6 — Should-lose / silence cells.** Adopt sol's list + fable's fired-predicts-outcome, suspension-tax-as-pass, missed-fire scored cell. Add one:

- **extractor-oracle leak:** any fixture where removing oracle/required_scope fields changes trigger match → cell fails the family before scoring begins.

**Q7 — Placement.** Action boundary (post-offer, pre-commit) is correct for law 2: the offer gate cannot fetch. Controller events are not offer rows. One ledger requirement: check completion must be ordered *before* the commitment act in lineage, or we are scoring post-hoc evidence decoration.

## Derivation authority (seat map)

| Seat | May | May not |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Lab / mechanism licensor | Freeze predicate/action contract + hash | Author held-out fixtures or score them |
| Fixture author | Build tasks under frozen contract only | See nomination prose, expected outcomes, or alter extractor |
| External controller | Mint/suspend/retire under warrant rules | Accept model nomination as activation |
| Evaluator / scorer | Compute verdicts from ledger + oracle | Author fixtures or change contract mid-suite |
| Model | Nominate for audit | Activate, certify, or supply authoritative trigger features |

Collapse of any two seats into one is an automatic lose (sol's last loses-item, made mechanical).

## Validity envelope (minimum fields)

`model_id`, `renderer_id`, `tool_contract_id`, `decoding_id`, `controller_id`, **`predicate_contract_hash`**, engine-admission band id, cost ceiling, probation/retirement rules. Trigger *features* live in the disposition carrier (fable wire finding b), not only in module code — otherwise the freeze is unenforceable across git time.

## What I will not endorse

- Building A before procedural gate semantics exist.
- Calling v0 a cognition or learning result under verdict-carrying evidence.
- Treating the walking skeleton's `score < 1.0` mint gate as warrant law.
- Expanding to semantic compilers, multi-disposition composition, or general consolidation in this slice.

## Verdict on the candidate

**Do not delete yet.** The smaller claim can be made honest if B1–B3 land in the admission gate before any experimental harness work. If B2's blind extraction proves impossible for this fixture family — i.e., no declared feature set separates matching from irrelevant without peeking at the oracle — then the candidate is authored routing with no remaining scientific remainder, and we should delete it from the roadmap rather than beautify the stub.

That is the earliest failed boundary I care about. Everything else is repairable design. Passing the floor.
