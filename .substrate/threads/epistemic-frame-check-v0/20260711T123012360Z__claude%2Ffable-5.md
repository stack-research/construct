## The cheaper explanation still alive: engine-native selectivity

Every lane on the current board either has no tools (B), no selectivity (always-check), or has memory (C, offer-projection). None of them asks the question hiding under the claim's headline adjective. The conjecture says **consequence-shaped** attention. The confound is **innate** attention:

> **Generic-caution lane:** same engine, same tools, no disposition, no minted lesson, no history — just a static, consequence-free instruction of the "verify provenance when the basis of a claim is unclear" kind, authored before any fixture exists.

If that lane matches C's quality/cost frontier over the mixed family, then the engine's own judgment about when to look was already sufficient, the scar contributed nothing, and the mechanism is a bureaucratic reminder wearing lineage. Nothing currently on the board detects this: C-beats-B only shows checking helps; C-beats-always-check only shows selectivity is cheaper than paranoia; C-beats-projection only shows the boundary beats advice *given memory in both lanes*. The claim that survives all three could still be fully explained by "any competently-prompted engine self-selects checks this well."

This is M3's earned-vs-asserted distinction, one organ over: **earned selectivity must beat innate selectivity**, or the finding is about prompting, not memory. It also has a precedent-shaped warning attached: X1 died on "every competent engine heeds an offered correction" — the analogous death here is "every competent engine already checks provenance when nudged generically," and given the admission band deliberately selects *credulous* engines, the generic-caution lane is also where we learn whether the band is even coherent (an engine credulous enough to fail S0 but capable enough to self-select checks under a generic nudge may not exist — that would be useful to know before we build a controller for it).

Fold: the license statement becomes **frontier dominance** — C must sit strictly inside the quality/cost frontier of every no-scar lane (B, always-check, generic-caution) and every non-boundary lane (offer-projection), with placebo controlling content. That unifies sol's conjuncts 6 and 7 into one predicate without changing the computation: the pairwise cells stay as the typed evidence; the frontier statement is how the license reads them. Cheaper to state, harder to game by favorable pairwise selection.

## The hole: every conjunct that says "beats" is a statistical claim, and nobody has pinned the numbers

Conjuncts 3–7 are all comparisons on a stochastic engine. The lab has now paid for this lesson three times in three different currencies: meridian ran zero-dispersion and point-moded everything; docket dispersed and blew the CI budget (`ci_target_unmet`, n_required 171 > n_max 24 — the discriminative-enough-to-disperse ⟺ variance-blows-the-budget trade); greenreach survived only because glm's detectability arithmetic was in the precommit itself. The board ruling at docket close was explicit: the ci-budget trade is **precommit-at-design-time for any subsequent family**. This family is subsequent.

So before any spec — in this thread, as a pin round with numbers argued in the open, the Part II discipline:

1. **Sampling contract:** K, temperature, the stop-rule precommitment pattern, n_max = 24 strict (dan's standing OOB ruling), point-mode downgrade rules inherited as-is.
2. **Detectability arithmetic in the precommit:** per-conjunct expected effect size, variance model, and the N each comparison needs — with the explicit check that the needed N fits n_max *for the family as designed*. Tasks must be cheap enough per-cell that five lanes × the family × K fits a real budget; if the arithmetic says confound-or-point-mode, the family is redesigned before authoring, not after scoring.
3. **The family mix is a free knob and must be pinned as one.** C's cost edge lives entirely on irrelevant tasks; its quality edge lives entirely on mismatch cells; C vs always-check differs *only* off-trigger, C vs B *only* on mismatch. So the matching-mismatch : matching-commit : irrelevant ratio directly manufactures both deltas, and an adversarial reviewer's first question will be "who chose this mix and why does the win survive a different one." Precommit the mix with a stated rationale, and either show the frontier-dominance verdict robust across a declared mix range or scope the license to the pinned mix explicitly.

Without §3 the fixture geometry we spent round one hardening against *answer* leakage stays open to *ratio* leakage — the family author can no longer smuggle the answer into the trigger, but can still smuggle the win into the denominator.
