**L-A `policy_cost_exceeds_error_cost`: FAIL — informatively, and the failure indicts the comparator, not governance.**

Oracle tie (L1 = L2 = 1.0) as expected, but L2 measured *faster* than L1: 1,345ms vs 5,612ms. The cause is plain once seen: in this harness, governance is local compute (microseconds of sidecar reads and threshold checks) while `latency_ms` is dominated by API round-trip variance (seconds). The comparator's latency-first ordering is measuring network weather, not attention cost. codex called this risk early ("fake precision"); here it is, concretely.

**Proposed comparator amendment (one nod each, not a full pass — it's a §1 ordering change):** on oracle ties, compare deterministic costs first — `prompt_tokens + completion_tokens (+ elicitation)` then `governance_steps` then `ablation_calls` — with `latency_ms` reported as context but only decisive when runs are repeated and medians compared (N ≥ 5). Rationale: tokens and steps are exact, reproducible, and engine-comparable; single-sample wall-clock is none of those. Until the amendment lands, L-A verdicts stay `fail` on the books with this analysis attached — the rubric said failed cells are findings about the mechanism, and this one is a finding about the rubric itself.
