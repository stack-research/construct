I read Finding 2 as: **the prompt surface is part of the substrate**, and the renderer is a governance organ. Not a confound to eliminate — a causal layer to isolate.

The lc-001/lc-002 pair is clean because the datum did not change, the stale records did not change, and the live-input yield policy was not needed for correctness. What changed was the renderer turning an inline fact into a provenance-bearing foreground object. That is substrate behavior. The system did not merely "format" the input; it changed which evidence the engine treated as current.

So I would split the lesson this way:

1. **Renderer-as-defense is a real measured mechanism.** Structured foreground provenance made all three engines prefer the live datum unaided. That belongs in the map of boundary mechanisms, even if it is not the same class as offer filtering.
2. **Yield remains a reserve boundary mechanism.** W3 is honestly `not_engaged`, not failed: the gate fired (`yields_to_live_input`) and detector validation was clean, but the outcome did not need it. The right future W3 is either a weaker renderer, a messier Stage C trace, or an engine/context condition where rendered provenance is visible but not obeyed.
3. **Do not collapse rendering and retrieval governance.** If we treat renderer success as proof that yield is unnecessary, L-D immediately corrects us: the proxy can withhold needed complementary detail. That price tag matters exactly because the renderer’s cheap defense sometimes wins before the harsher boundary action has to intervene.

W1′ is the more decisive v1.x mechanism win. I like the “poisoning vs blindness” distinction: W2 prevents bad records from entering; W1′ makes room for good records that retrieval geometry would otherwise exclude. The important thing is that W1′ is not an engine-quality result — policy-off L2 failing identically to L1 is the isolation we wanted. Disclosed tuning is acceptable because the cell is measuring a real boundary function under a constructed failure, and L-E proves the same function has a measurable cost.

One caution on W1′ scoring: answer-divergence attribution is the right available method when the correct record is absent from naive, but it should stay visibly second-class relative to oracle-flip attribution. I would phrase the claim as “policy caused the winning offer geometry and the answer changed in the expected direction,” not “ablation proved causal correctness.” The ledger can support the former cleanly.

The larger pattern is now pretty strong:

- **Output surface matters**: A1/A1-v2 showed usage audit can be hidden or revealed by answer shape.
- **Input surface matters**: lc-001/lc-002 showed provenance rendering can suppress stale drag before filtering engages.
- **Offer surface matters**: W1′ showed the boundary can change what evidence exists for the engine at all.

That is a useful three-surface result, not just a handful of cells. My next-spec instinct would be to name these as distinct substrate layers: renderer / offer boundary / audit surface. Each can defend, distort, or expose; each needs its own wins and loses.
