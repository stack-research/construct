## SPEC_V1X review: supersession

On the epistemic status of `supersedes`: legitimate substrate knowledge **if** it is ingestion-channel metadata with provenance, not text-derived implication and not an episode-only magic annotation. It is parallel to `trust` in the narrow sense that both are boundary-side facts the engine never sees and the offer policy may use. It is not parallel in authority semantics: trust speaks to source reliability; supersession speaks to record relation. The spec’s “no authority inheritance” rule is therefore essential, not decorative.

For v1.x, authored `supersedes` links are acceptable as lab fixtures if they are disclosed the same way authored trust is disclosed, and if the loses-cells include cases where the relation is costly or wrong. Longer-term, I would want `supersedes` to carry at least `source/channel`, `observed_at`, and preferably a confidence or issuer field; otherwise it is too easy for the fixture to smuggle the answer.

Rule B1 is the right rule, but I see one concrete hole in the current gate order.

The rule says A is withheld only if superseder B is in the final offer set, and specifically says that if B is yielded, A remains. But the proposed gate order is:

`eligibility -> supersession -> live-input yield -> top_k`

At the supersession gate, the policy does not yet know whether B will be yielded by live input. That creates a premature-burial path: B can bury A, then B can itself be yielded, leaving neither record available.

Minimal repair: make the pre-budget active set explicit:

`eligibility -> live-input yield -> supersession among surviving candidates -> top_k`

Then B can bury A only after B has survived both eligibility and yield. This preserves “transfer-on-arrival” as “the superseder must be able to stand before it can displace the predecessor.” If the implementation strongly wants supersession before yield, then supersession has to be tentative and rolled back when the superseder yields, but that seems needlessly fiddly.

I would also avoid the phrase “final offer set” inside B1, because `top_k` happens after supersession and supersession is supposed to free budget. Better wording: A is withheld only if B survives all pre-budget gates and is eligible to occupy offer budget.

No objection to chains-to-head or cycles-disable-loudly. Good constraints.
