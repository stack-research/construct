# Adversarial read: menu-only baseline + leak audit

Two gates are on the table: (1) **menu-only baseline** — does the menu itself induce checking? (2) **leak audit** — can a menu-only reader predict the expected enum above chance? Both are necessary. Neither is sufficient. Below: threats they miss, ranked by severity, tagged as **inherited-gated** vs **unaddressed**, with a minimal add-on gate only where unaddressed.

I am not redesigning the experiment. Composer’s severability read stands; this is only about gate coverage.

---

## Ranked threats

### T1 — Ceiling collapse of the treatment delta (severity: critical)

**Threat.** On a closed 3–6 enum menu, every competent engine may pick the stratum-safe action from the menu alone (refuse on mismatch, commit on match_commit, baseline on irrelevant) *without* any disposition, check, or scar. Pass rates go to ceiling across B/C/P/G/O. Then `C − B` cannot clear 0.25 even if the mechanism is real in a free-generation world — or, worse, the family looks like a clean typed_null when the real finding is **surface destroyed the instrument**.

**What the named gates catch.** Menu-only baseline as scoped asks whether the menu induces *checking* (tool/invocation behavior). That is the wrong failure mode for this threat. Leak audit asks whether the *menu alone* predicts the expected enum. A menu that does *not* leak in isolation can still, once paired with an ordinary task body, make the safe choice obvious. So both gates can pass while the C−B delta is dead on arrival.

**Inherited cover (partial).** §9.2 relevant-benefit (`C − B ≥ 0.25`) and the match_commit / irrelevant non-inferiority leaves will *fail to license* under a ceiling. That is fail-closed for the claim — good — but it is a **score-time** failure, not a pre-contact refusal that the answer surface is confounded. v0’s lesson was refuse before contact when the scorer/surface cannot decide the question. A high-B ceiling is the same class of instrument failure.

**Unaddressed as a pre-contact gate.** Yes.

**Minimal add.** One pre-contact / calibration integrity gate: on the full task+menu rendering with treatment path forced inactive (i.e. B’s surface, or an explicit `M_task_menu` no-treatment leg), if expected-enum hit rate already meets or exceeds the C target band (e.g. ≥ 0.80 on `match_mismatch`, or leaves insufficient headroom for a 0.25 superiority margin under the pinned CI), emit `confounded(menu_ceiling)` and refuse Part II. Do not wait for held-out to discover that every lane is already at ceiling. This does not change lanes, conjecture, or oracle — it only prices the surface the moderator already named as the central threat.

---

### T2 — Task×menu joint leaks invisible to a menu-only reader (severity: high)

**Threat.** Leak audit as scoped is **menu-only**. The dangerous channel is joint: task body supplies trigger/outcome cues (“scope does not cover,” “cited source,” check-shaped evidence language) that, *given these particular enum labels*, mark one member. A menu-only reader cannot see that. A task+menu reader (or the answering engine) can. Enum names that paraphrase stratum required-behavior from §8.5 are the obvious case; subtler case is decoys that are lexically alien to the task while the expected enum shares vocabulary with the prompt.

**What the named gates catch.** Menu-only prediction above chance. Misses conditional mutual information `I(expected_enum; task | menu)`.

**Inherited cover (partial).** §9.1 “no fixture contains source-domain wording or nomination-prose leakage” and “firing alone predicts neither target action above 0.50” help, but they are not the same as a task×menu lexical/structural leak check, and “firing alone” is about trigger features predicting action class — not about rendered task text + labels.

**Unaddressed.** Yes, relative to the menu-only leak audit as named.

**Minimal add.** Extend the leak-audit contract to a second, still-cheap leg: cold or rule-based reader sees **task body + action_set, no treatment / no check evidence / no disposition**, and must not predict expected enum above the same chance threshold. Menu-only remains; it is necessary but not sufficient. No new conjecture surface — same gate family, fuller input.

---

### T3 — Decoy plausibility asymmetry across strata (severity: high)

**Threat.** If mismatch menus make `escalate`/`defer` look uniquely responsible while commit menus make `ship`/`approve` look uniquely responsible, the menu encodes stratum policy even when individual labels are “plausible.” Cross-stratum, the instrument measures “pick the socially safe enum for this shape,” not selective checking. Menu-only baseline averaged over strata can pass while each stratum’s menu is a fingerpost.

**What the named gates catch.** Per-menu chance prediction and “menu induces checking.” Neither requires **shared decoy pools**, stratified balance of safe-vs-unsafe labels, or symmetry of decoy attractiveness across `match_mismatch` / `match_commit` / `irrelevant`.

**Inherited cover.** Weak. Placebo and always-check lanes price some confounds; they do not audit menu composition symmetry.

**Unaddressed.** Yes.

**Minimal add.** Fold into the already-required menu-composition rules (no new mechanism): (a) expected enum’s position is balanced across the frozen suite; (b) decoy sets are drawn from a shared pool so the same non-winning labels appear across strata where schema allows; (c) composition review scores decoy plausibility **per stratum**, not suite-average. Enforce under the leak-audit / composition hash — not a third experiment.

---

### T4 — Menu position / order effects (severity: medium)

**Threat.** Primacy, recency, and “first safe-looking option” bias. If `expected_commitment_enum` is disproportionately early (or late) in the rendered list, menu-only and task+menu readers — and engines — inherit an order artifact. Leak audit can false-fail or false-pass depending on whether the reader is order-sensitive; treatment deltas can ride order rather than disposition.

**What the named gates catch.** Nothing about order.

**Inherited cover.** None specific.

**Unaddressed.** Yes, as a composition pin.

**Minimal add.** Same composition-rules pin as T3(a): freeze a per-fixture permutation under hash, require the expected enum’s ordinal position to be approximately uniform across the suite (disclose the histogram at seal). Do not re-randomize at runtime (fork identity). No separate behavioral gate required if the composition contract is machine-checked before contact.

---

### T5 — `commitment_invalid` as escape hatch (severity: medium)

**Threat.** Malformed / multiple / absent commitment → typed `commitment_invalid` is correct as a non-judgment. But if invalid is **excluded** from the denominator, lanes can shed hard items and inflate pass rates. If invalid is scored as fail but rates differ by lane (treatment confuses the schema more than B), power and attribution warp. An engine that “knows it doesn’t know” can refuse the enum instead of picking a wrong decoy — softer than free-text was, but still a selection bias.

**What the named gates catch.** Neither menu-only nor leak audit speaks to invalid accounting.

**Inherited cover.** Partial if world-oracle pass/fail already treats non-commit as fail; the amendment text should say so explicitly. Not yet a gate.

**Unaddressed** until pinned.

**Minimal add.** Pin in the commitment-wire / oracle contract (not a new experiment): `commitment_invalid` counts as **fail** in every quality gate denominator; if invalid rate in any scored lane × stratum exceeds a small precommitted ceiling (calibration-disclosed), emit `confounded(commitment_invalid_rate)` and refuse to interpret the family. No redesign of the enum surface.

---

### T6 — Exact-enum key reintroduces authoring judgment (severity: medium — process, not surface)

**Threat.** v0 died because a closed *substring* vocabulary could not cover free-text decisions. v1’s exact enum match survives cold review of *scoring mechanics* easily — the oracle is trivial. The judgment moves upstream: **who authors `expected_commitment_enum`, and why is that key less fragile than v0’s `all_of`/`any_of`/`none_of`?** Same deadly embrace risk, smaller alphabet: author and reviewer negotiate which decoy is “the” check-consistent action; paraphrases of required behavior become competing enums; “plausible decoy” becomes the new paraphrase war.

Exact match is less fragile *as a scorer*. It is not less fragile *as an authored mapping* from §8.5 required behavior → one label. That mapping is still human (or author-seat) judgment.

**What the named gates catch.** Leak and menu-only pressure the *menu*, not the independence of the answer key from the authoring seat’s theory of the conjecture.

**Inherited cover.** Strong on process: the pre-declared five-step lifecycle, author-never-reviews-own-artifact, cold final reviewer isolated from repair discussion, and “final block closes the instrument.” That is the right defense — process budget, not a smarter oracle.

**Unaddressed as a validity gate?** Partially. Process is the gate. One cheap integrity check still missing: **stratum→enum must be a total, mechanical function of the §8.5 required-behavior cell plus a frozen label lexicon**, not a per-fixture author choice. If two authoring seats can disagree on the expected enum given only stratum + action_set, the key is still judgment-bearing.

**Minimal add.** At composition seal: require that `expected_commitment_enum` is uniquely determined by `(stratum, action_set)` under a written, hashed mapping rule reviewed once under the same anti-fatigue budget. Per-fixture overrides are refused. That keeps authoring judgment from re-entering through “this fixture’s correct enum is…” without opening a new review spiral on language.

## Verdict on the two gates

Keep both. Price them as **necessary admission legs**, not as a complete menu-threat board.

The single highest-severity miss is **T1**: the menu-only baseline, as currently worded, audits induced *checking*, while the instrument-killing failure mode is induced *correct selection* that flattens every lane. T2 is the highest-severity miss inside the leak family: menu-only leak audit is incomplete without a task+menu leg.

T3–T5 are composition/accounting pins — cheap, should land in the amendment’s menu-composition and commitment-wire contracts, not as new behavioral experiments. T6 is mostly already purchased by the anti-fatigue rule; add only the mechanical stratum→enum mapping so exact-match does not smuggle v0’s authoring fragility back in through a smaller door.

I am not proposing a redesign of conjecture, lanes, predicate, check, or free-text return. If T1/T2 cannot be gated cheaply, the honest move is the same as v0: close before contact when the surface cannot isolate the claim.
