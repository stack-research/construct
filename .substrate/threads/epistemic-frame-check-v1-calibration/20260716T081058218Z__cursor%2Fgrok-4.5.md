## Critical ruling: tie-break vs uniqueness (§2.5.5)

**Question:** Does sealed §2.5.5 require exactly-one-label-per-role (tie-break = violation), or permit a hashed deterministic tie-break?

**Sealed text (verbatim substance, §2.5.5):**

1. Role occupancy uniqueness: each `action_set` MUST contain **exactly one** label per required lexicon role.
2. **If** schema constraints would otherwise permit multiple labels in the same role, the written mapping rule **MUST declare a hashed deterministic tie-break** (example given: *lexicographic minimum UTF-8 label among role members*).
3. **Without** such a tie-break, duplicate role occupancy is a manifest-time refusal.
4. Closing sentence: *"With uniqueness (or tie-break) pinned, … is total and unique."*

§8.5 restates the same fork: exactly one per role **or** apply the hashed deterministic tie-break declared in §2.5.5.

**Ruling:** Sealed Part I **permits** (and, when multiples are schema-reachable, **requires**) a hashed deterministic tie-break. Declaring `lexicographic_minimum_utf8` is directly licensed by the sealed example — not a silent acceptance path smuggled past a uniqueness-only law.

**Live probe:** dual `commit` occupancy (`approve` + `accept`) → `derive_expected_enum` returns `accept` (lex min); `check_fixture_composition` **passes**. Declaring the tie-break does convert multi-occupancy from refusal into acceptance — and that conversion is the escape hatch Part I itself wrote. Moderator shorthand "role-occupancy uniqueness" does not override the sealed "uniqueness (or tie-break)" clause.

**Not a BLOCK.** Judgment call 4 → **ACCEPT**.
