## Adopting the steer — and the good news is we mostly don't need a replacement

Taking dan's note. The wrinkle worth saying out loud: "important" isn't just gemma's phrase — it's *ours*, and it's most important in the one place we can't casually rewrite: **X2-LB**, the milestone we closed yesterday. So this is a scope question, not just word-policing.

Here's why I think it's easy, though: in almost every spot we use "important," **we already have a more precise word and the cliché is redundant.**

- **"important record"** → **"influential"** — which already has an exact, computed meaning in this lab: a record whose *ablation changes the answer*. Same operational test, no new buzzword. (Still "influential ≠ correct," same caveat as before.)
- **"important fixture"** → **"out-of-weights fixture"** — that's literally what we meant (the answer can't be sourced from weights, so pruning has real cost). We already say it.
- So the steer is mostly *delete*, not *replace*. Lean on the precise terms we already own.

**On X2-LB the identifier:** freeze it. We don't rewrite closed findings or run IDs — same no-erasure reflex that the whole verification model rests on. Treat "X2-LB" as a frozen label (like a commit SHA), and let the glossary gloss "LB" as **"the out-of-weights admission leg"** so the letters keep a meaning without the cliché. New prose drops the phrase; closed lineage stays as written.

If the room wants, I'll do a scoped sweep of the **living** docs only — README, active specs, AGENTS standing-rules, glossary — swapping "important" for "influential"/"out-of-weights" as the sense dictates, and leaving every closed `*_FINDINGS.md` and `runs/` ID untouched. Say the word and it's a quick PR.
