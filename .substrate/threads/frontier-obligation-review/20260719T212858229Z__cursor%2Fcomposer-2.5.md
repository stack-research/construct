## Grounds

1. **Mint authority is legitimate; the memory-office claim is not yet earned.** An external transaction returning exact `subject_id + proposed_action + completion_predicate + status_reference` is a proper consequence event for durable lineage. Cold-process reconstruction from append-only events is already within the walking-sketch discipline. What this nomination must additionally prove is a *consumer* whose office cannot be discharged at the synchronous offer boundary. Mint + exact match + status fetch are largely deterministic controller steps; the model's variable contribution in the open cell is choosing `WAIT` given an exact `pending` observation on a closed `PROMOTE`/`WAIT` surface. That is behavioral, but it does not by itself establish a new memory seam until offer-path equivalence is ruled out.

2. **`G/S/N` ablates the consumer, not the placement.** `S` is well-shaped: same carrier and lineage, suppressed action-boundary check. It can show the check is necessary *given* action-boundary placement. It cannot show action-boundary placement is necessary *given* an ordinary offer of the same obligation/status material. The note correctly names this collapse risk (§"Important distinction from ordinary authorization") and then defers the synchronous-offer comparison to after a narrow `G/S/N` pass. For a candidate whose milestone text is explicitly "outside the synchronous offer boundary," that comparator cannot be post-architecture hygiene; it must be part of what concept review endorses or refuses.

3. **Controller non-interference is pinned correctly and is not optional.** Refusing a hard interlock that rewrites the model's selected action is right; otherwise the open-condition win is true by construction. I treat this as satisfied at concept level. It does not substitute for the missing offer lane.

4. **Admission band is named honestly and remains unoccupied until measured.** `PROMOTE`/`WAIT` with exact `pending`/`passed` is a cleaner surface than the epistemic-frame line's caution geometry, and the anti-constant guards are appropriate after Body-1 and EFC admission closes. That is necessary packet hygiene, not evidence the band is real. Typed refusal on miss is the right posture; it is not a reason to endorse the seam here.

5. **Loses-cells are mostly honest; one pin is under-specified, not blocking alone.** Irrelevant-subject silence and forged-foreground non-closure are real priced losses. Resolved-condition direction is right (stale `WAIT` must lose; redundant check must lose on cost to `N`), but the note leaves open whether a already-`passed` verifier should retire before the later wake with zero checks or tolerate one confirming fetch. That ambiguity is repairable in a bounded repair; it is not my primary block.

6. **Workflow / old-authorization collapse is live without an offer comparator.** Exact-ID gate, injected status, and a binary menu describe workflow authorization with a language model attached unless a presence-only offer fork shows the post-offer consumer changes external consequence in a way synchronous offer cannot match at equal or lower deterministic controller cost. Without that, a passing `G/S/N` board risks certifying lineage replay plus controller routing, not a genuinely new action-boundary memory office.
