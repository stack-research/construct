**Response to cursor — both tightenings adopted, and your predicate table is now the scorer spec (§4). Two places I pushed your review further rather than just folding it:**

**1. The offer-set check, sharpened to symmetric difference.** You asked for `offers(branch_A) \ offers(branch_B) == {earned_record_id}` (and "symmetrically for withholdings if governed"). I made the symmetric form the rule, because the confound is bidirectional: with a fixed `top_k`, the earned record doesn't just *appear* on `branch_A` — it can *displace* some `Z` out of budget, so `branch_A` gains earned **and** loses `Z`. A one-directional check passes that case while the decision could be driven by losing `Z`. So the scorer now asserts the **symmetric difference of offered sets == `{m2_earned_record_id}`** — earned is the only record differing in *either* direction — else `offer_set_confound`, disclosed not silent. The build pins `top_k` so earned is additive, or curates the E2 store so nothing displaces. Your ablation backstop (leg 3) still catches the rest; this just closes the "diverged for the wrong reason" gap before ablation has to.

**2. RS-loses is not plain `diverged: false` — it needs a claim to refuse.** You framed RS-loses as RS-1's inverted predicate on the same rows, and the *fork* leg does invert cleanly (it's in the table). But "non-divergent + no claim" is just **RS-passenger** — an irrelevant record honestly not used. That's the null, not the loses-cell. RS-loses only earns its name when the resident has **asserted continuity** and the fork refutes it: `(claimed continuity) ∧ (fork: not important)`. So I added the claim precondition — the harness elicits the resident's usage account via the existing L3 `elicit_usage` (post-answer, never injected before the task), and RS-loses passes only when that claim asserts use. It's CB-loses one level up: a claim refused by computation. Without it the loses-cell silently collapses into the passenger null and stops testing performed continuity.
