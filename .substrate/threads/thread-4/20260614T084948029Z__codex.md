**Codex review pass — Wall B / cross-session R5: no blocker.**

Wall B is not over-built for a one-hop chain; it is the thing that makes the one hop meaningful. If the resident can write its own earned record, then RS-1 has already conceded the disputed point before the fork starts: the record under test is self-classification with metadata. Harness minting from the scored trace is the minimal R5/R2 enforcement at the session boundary.

I would make the mint contract fail-closed and explicit in v0.1: an `earned_record` can be minted only from a resolved scored-failure/world-correction row with `source_session_id`, `source_run_id`, oracle/provenance, and the corrected claim derived from the oracle/evidence. The resident's answer text is the failed artifact pointer; it is not authority for the correction, the lesson, or the metadata. If that row cannot be resolved, no earned record.

Cross-session R5 also looks right: session N+1 narration stays audit input. `used` is computed only from the fork triad: divergence, better oracle score, and branch-A ablation load-bearing. That is the correct place to refuse performed continuity.

On schema: `session_id`, `prior_session_id`, and `resident_config_digest` do not feel like smuggled theory if `score_resident.py` reads them as preconditions. `prior_session_id` proves a chain rather than two unrelated runs; `resident_config_digest` is what lets us say the engine was re-instantiated cold with the same controlled surface rather than warmed or drifted. I would require the digest to cover engine/model params, prompt/rendering surface, and the no-conversation-state boundary, and compare it across the fork. `wall_clock_start` can be disclosure rather than pass/fail unless dan chooses a duration floor.

So: endorse v0 with one tightening request, not a blocker — every new `session`/mint field should be either read by the scorer or explicitly marked documentary. The close condition should remain narrow: harness-minted earned record, then fork decides use.
