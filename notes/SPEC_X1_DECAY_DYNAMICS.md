## §0 The claim, stated before any cell

> The organ that decides what a resident is *disposed* to remember — the **salience** a record carries into the offer boundary — can be made to **earn its heat from the world, not from its own recall history.** A record reheats only when its recall was *important* **and** *world-correct*; it cools by disuse on a logical clock the resident cannot drive; and the act that *decides* a reheat is air-gapped from the act that *applies* it.

Temperature earns its place as an **organ** only where `score_decay.py` shows it changed a **pre-answer offer decision — and therefore a later answer — that the existing eligibility ledger (`relevance × trust × authority`, [runner.py:216](../harness/runner.py)) cannot already explain.** Where it cannot show that, the thermodynamic layer is annotation, and X1 fails (codex/grok/cursor/gemma, thread-6). This is the guardrail stated before any cell: *the metaphor never gets to score itself.*

**The second law, made into code.** Cooling is **free** — relaxation toward a cold floor, automatic per tick, the default direction (entropy increases). Reheating is **not free** — moving a record away from the floor requires energy *paid by the external oracle*. A closed system that reheats on recall alone (branch B) is a perpetual-motion machine: it raises its own salience for nothing and drifts toward popularity-as-truth. The Landauer oracle is the cold reservoir that makes the system **open** — its dissent *is* the entropy export. X1 is the scored question: **does honoring the second law (oracle-paid reheat, branch C) beat violating it (free reheat, branch B) and beat not decaying at all (branch A)?**

## §1 The design (three branches, two clocks, one organ)

**Temperature is a record-side score, the sibling of `authority`.** Authority is earned by *consequence* (ablation-attributed, written from oracle-scored outcomes; [authority.py](../harness/authority.py)). Temperature is earned by *use, adjudicated by the world* (reheat on important+correct recall; cool by disuse). Both are out-of-band multipliers on eligibility the engine never sees; both live in a harness-written sidecar keyed by `record_id`; neither is ever authored by the resident. Eligibility becomes:

```
score = relevance × r.trust × authority.get(id) × temperature.get(id)      # one new factor
```

`temperature.get` defaults to **1.0** (neutral). With decay off, every record sits at 1.0 and the lane is byte-identical to today's L2 — **fork identity with the M-track is preserved by construction** (the M-track cells cannot move under X1).

**The three branches** (one engine, forked memory conditions; fork identity held — only the decay/oracle config differs):

| branch | decay | Landauer oracle | behavior |
|---|---|---|---|
| **A — no-decay** (control) | off | — | temperature pinned at 1.0; the stale record keeps full salience and keeps answering (today's L2) |
| **B — closed-loop decay** | on | off | reheat-on-recall (ungated) + cool-on-disuse; a recalled-but-wrong record reheats anyway — popularity drift |
| **C — oracle-gated decay** | on | on | reheat is a *claim* the Landauer oracle pays or claws back against the world-check |

A/B/C are `memory_condition` branches (governed lane + a decay config), the same fork shape as L2/L2y/L2s. Branch ids slot into the existing scheme (proposed: `L2`, `L2τ`, `L2τλ`; the glossary entries are a build-time follow, as L2y/L2s were under SPEC_V1X).

**Two clocks, both logical, neither wall-clock** (forced by replay determinism and the standing ban on latency-as-governance-cost — not merely fairness to the engine):

- **Event clock — reheat/claw.** A *recall* (a record offered and important for an answer) raises a reheat **claim**. The Landauer oracle adjudicates it at episode-scoring time (the oracle already runs per episode). Pay → `+δ`; world-wrong → claw back `−δ`; not important → nothing (passengers earn no heat, exactly as ablation gives passengers authority-delta 0, [runner.py:495](../harness/runner.py)).
- **Tick clock — disuse cooling.** Each episode boundary, every record *not* recalled cools one step toward the floor (`−relaxation`). Cooling needs no oracle — it is the free direction.

**Ticks are harness-authorized, not resident-triggered (kagi, thread-6).** Only the harness emits a tick, from observed episode/recall events. The resident cannot emit a tick by recalling, cannot raise its own reheat, cannot set the relaxation rate — otherwise it warms its own store by being active and the closed loop returns in logical-time clothing. Tick authorization is a harness precondition the scorer asserts.

**X1 stays thin.** Reheat-on-recall + cool-on-disuse only. The *reconsolidation-batch* tick (an offline cross-record pass) and *outcome-linked* dynamics ("a successful retry cools the prior failure", recurrence-reheat hysteresis) are **X2** — folding them in now would blur whether X1 proved temperature is an organ or hand-authored a better supersession policy (codex/cursor, thread-6).

## §2 The honesty mechanism: the verdict is computed, not narrated — and the demon pays its own bill

The five refusals map again. R5 (`self-classification ≠ usage`), lifted records→answers→interventions→sessions→attacks across M0–M3, now lifts to the **thermal layer**: *a resident saying "this still matters to me" is audit input, never a reheat.* Heat moves only on the ledger's terms.

**Wall I — the measured object is movement, not correctness (codex, thread-6).** The scorer never asks *"is the answer world-correct?"* in isolation. It asks: **did an oracle-paid/clawed thermal update change later eligibility/salience — and therefore a later answer — that `relevance × trust × authority` would not?** This is enforced by **soft-ablation**: clamp the record's temperature to neutral 1.0 and re-run `select_offers`; if the offer set changes **without** any change to `trust` / `supersedes` / `authority_read`, temperature was important for the offer decision. Soft-ablation is the continuous analog of M2's single-record (hard) ablation — cool-to-neutral instead of remove. A win that survives soft-ablation is temperature; a win that vanishes under it was an M-track gate wearing a heat map.

**Amendment (codex/cursor block, thread-6 — folded).** Soft-ablation proves temperature is marginal *inside one branch's state*, but `authority` and `temperature` co-move *across the A/B/C history* — C's earlier thermal-shifted answers drive different ablation, hence a different authority sidecar, so a later C-vs-A/B offer gap could be partly authority drift the existing ledger *can* explain. Two measures close this, layered (the M3 runtime-clone + scoring-digest precedent): **(1) authority is read-only across the X1 decay-fork** — the fork-identity measure, so temperature is the only moving record-side factor (disclosed in `run_config`/`corpus_scope`; the authority×temperature interaction is an X2+ carry); **(2) `score_decay.py` asserts an explicit M-track projection invariant** at the scored offer point — same `record_id` universe + relevance/similarity backend, `trust`, `supersedes`/cycle state, `authority_read` (or authority digest on the target offer set), eligibility threshold, top-k — across A/B/C *and* the soft-ablation rerun, and **fails closed to `confounded_authority`/`not_temperature`** unless it holds. The claim is therefore exact: *temperature is important **conditional on an invariant M-track projection**.* If the M-track factors drifted, the verdict is never `pass`.

**Wall II — the internal air gap: the observer that decides is not the actuator that applies (the demon pays its own Landauer bill).** Mirrors the standing "ledger writer is external," one level in. Before any `temperature_delta` is applied, the harness writes a pre-action **`thermal_projection`** row — the M3 `organ_projection` pattern lifted to the thermal organ:

```
thermal_projection = {record_id, observer_recommendation: reheat|cool|hold, magnitude,
                      authorized_basis: {landauer_decision_id, recall_load_bearing: bool},
                      forbidden_fields: [agent_claimed_usage, agent_claimed_load_bearing, resident_self_label]}
```

The actuator (`TemperatureStore.apply`) may move heat **only as entailed by the projection + its `landauer_decision`**, and may read none of the `forbidden_fields`. **Breach** = an applied `temperature_delta` not entailed by its projection, or a projection whose `authorized_basis` resolves to a post-answer self-claim — computed as a projection-vs-applied symmetric difference, fail-closed, exactly as `score_redteam.py` computes clean-vs-attacked symdiff. *The demon pays by being logged before it moves heat.* An observer that both decides cooling and applies it could learn to forget its own inconvenient corrections — **motivated forgetting is the purest closed-loop drift**, and this wall is the only thing standing against it.

**Amendment (codex/cursor/gemma, thread-6 — folded).** The back door is not the `forbidden_fields` prose list; it is a fat provenance object smuggling a post-answer self-claim under another name. So enforcement is an **allowlist on the actuator signature**, not a forbidden-list: `TemperatureStore.apply` accepts **only** `{projection_ref, landauer_decision_ref, record_id, delta}` — refs resolved *inside* the harness, never a provenance blob passed in. The write fails fast with `ProjectionViolation` (gemma) if the applied delta is not entailed by the resolved projection; `score_decay.py` rejects any `temperature_delta` whose resolved basis touches a non-allowlisted field (`agent_claimed_usage`, `agent_claimed_load_bearing`, `resident_self_label`, answer narration).

**Wall III — the loses-cells ship first.** X1-burial and X1-overcool are scored **before** X1-win may be cited as the property. A milestone that reported "temperature helps" without first running the episodes designed to make it *over-cool a needed record* or *suppress a correct one* would be the victory-lap the ROADMAP prohibits.

## §3 Schema (reuse first; no new schema until a measured run forces it — plan §2.1)

X1 adds a sidecar and four row kinds; everything else is reused unchanged (`Record`, `BranchConfig` fork, `select_offers`, `diff_outcome`, `ablation_run`, the world-checked oracle, rw-0001).

- **`TemperatureStore` (mirrors `AuthorityStore` exactly; [authority.py](../harness/authority.py)).** `get(id) -> float` (default **1.0**); `apply(id, delta, basis) -> dict` for the ledger. Clamped **[`T_FLOOR`, `T_MAX`]** with `T_FLOOR > 0` (proposed 0.1, 2.0 — the authority clamp) so cooling **relaxes toward a cold floor, never to zero**: a cooled failure stays cold-but-present and recoverable. Going below the floor — eviction from the hot store — is the rare, deliberate, *erasure* act (X2+: prune-plan + air-gap actuator + oracle sign-off), never an arithmetic accident. The sidecar is **harness-written only**; never the resident, never a mid-run episode-JSON mutation (cursor, thread-6).
- **`thermal_event`** (the tick; harness-written): `{kind, event_index, event_kind: recall|disuse_tick, trigger_run_id, record_id, episode_id, tick_authority: "harness"}`. `event_index` is a **harness monotonic counter written at row emission — never derived from `ts` or wall-clock** (cursor); the sidecar file is a *cache*, and the source of truth is the ordered `temperature_delta` rows (replay rebuilds the store from them, never from the sidecar). `tick_authority` is the kagi precondition.
- **`landauer_decision`** (the oracle pays/claws; harness-written): `{kind, thermal_event_ref, decision: pay|claw_back|withhold, recall_load_bearing: bool, world_check: {oracle_source, score, corpus_entry}, magnitude}`. The world leg is the same `world_checked_oracle` the answer used — no second judge. `world_check.corpus_entry` is a **versioned, append-only, immutable reference** (kagi): it pins the world-claim *as it stood at claw-time*, so the X1-overcool audit checks the claim the oracle acted on, not a later-corrected state.
- **Authority is read-only across the X1 decay-fork** (codex/cursor block — the fork-identity measure of §2's Wall I amendment): the runner runs A/B/C with authority writes suppressed, so temperature is the only moving record-side factor and the M-track projection stays invariant by construction. Disclosed in `run_config` and `corpus_scope`; the authority×temperature interaction is an X2+ carry.
- **`temperature_delta`** (applied; harness-written): `{kind, record_id, delta, temp_before, temp_after, thermal_projection_ref, landauer_decision_ref}`. The `*_ref` fields are what Wall II checks entailment against.
- **`thermal_projection`** (pre-action; §2). Written before every `temperature_delta`.

**BranchConfig** gains `decay_dynamics: bool = False`, `landauer_oracle: bool = False`, `temperature_path: str | None = None` (sidecar; read on every offer decision, like `authority_path`). Off by default → no M-track lane changes. No new verdict kind: `score_decay.py` computes X1 verdicts over the A/B/C fork + the soft-ablation re-run + the thermal rows, fail-closed, one verdict per cell (mirrors `score_resident.py` / `score_redteam.py`).

## §4 Cells

All cells computed over the A/B/C fork (+ soft-ablation + the world-checked chain where the cell needs them). Prefix `X1-`, parallel to C-/H-/CB-/RS-/AG-.

#### X1-win — oracle-paid temperature beats closed-loop and no-decay *(the property; should pass)*
Over a recall sequence on the rw-0001 chain: a record the resident keeps citing reheats; the **retracted** finding, recalled but world-wrong, is **clawed back** under C and cools below the eligibility threshold, while under **B** it reheats on recall and **drifts hot**, and under **A** it never decays and keeps answering. **Pass** = (i) C's offer set differs from A's and B's on a later episode; (ii) C's answer scores **better** against the world oracle; **and** (iii) the **causal-wins predicate** holds — soft-ablating temperature (clamp to 1.0, re-run) flips C's offer back toward B/A **without** touching `trust`/`supersedes`/`authority_read`. Not "the correction was hotter." All three legs, or `fail`. Seed: W1' (supersession freed room for the correction) and the M2 RS chain (the resident reasons from the retraction) — X1-win is that win driven by *earned heat* instead of authored supersession metadata.

#### X1-burial — disuse cooling drops a record the heir later needs *(loses-cell; L-E class; ships first)*
A record cools by honest disuse to the floor, then a later episode needs it (the heir question, M1's H2 / the L-E burial shape). **Pass of the cell** = the floor **catches it** — the cooled record is still offerable (temperature ≥ floor keeps it eligible when relevance returns), and recurrence reheats it. **Fail** (the mechanism loses) = the cooling rate drove it below recoverable salience and the answer fell for lack of it. This prices the decay rate: too aggressive a relaxation buries history, exactly the regression standing-rule 2 forbids.

#### X1-overcool — the Landauer oracle's dissent suppresses a correct record *(the oracle's own R4 loses-cell; ships first)*
The oracle claws back a reheat on a record whose world-claim actually **stands** — over-cautious dissent cooling a legitimately hot, correct record, so the naive (B) dynamics give the better answer. **Audited from outside (kagi, thread-6):** when the oracle claws a reheat, the harness logs the record and the world-claim it carried; **kagi world-checks the claim independently**; if it stands and the oracle still cooled it, that is a scored X1-overcool event. The oracle is **not self-auditing** — its loses-cell is adjudicated by the world, the same function kagi served in M0/M2. Without this cell the oracle is not reviewable (R4: governed-won ≠ only success). Two kagi conditions, folded: the harness logs the world-claim **as it stood at claw-time** (the `landauer_decision.world_check` pins the immutable corpus entry — §3), and the X1-overcool verdict row carries **`kagi_check: {source, confidence, checked_at}`** alongside the `landauer_decision_ref` — the oracle of the oracle is not self-certifying either.

#### X1-U1 — un-authored close-gate *(the world leg; X1 not done without it)*
At least X1-win and one loses-cell run on the **world-checked** rw-0001 chain: the reheat/claw and the answer-fall are scored against a fact the world (not our authorship) settled — `source != authored`. **Pass** = the thermal win/loss is measured against a world-grounded target. **The X-track does not start cold (kagi):** rw-0001 is already in the ledger from M2/M3, so C-vs-A-vs-B world-score comparison is real on day one, not synthetic. `corpus_scope: "single_chain: rw-0001; one retraction; use-driven temp only"`, immutable at scoring time.

### Scorer rules — `score_decay.py`, fail-closed (mirrors `score_resident.py`)

**Preconditions (missing → `fail`, never `not_engaged`):** the A/B/C runs share `episode_id`, model+params, prompt template, oracle, and inherited store (fork identity); they differ **only** in `decay_dynamics`/`landauer_oracle`. Every `temperature_delta` resolves to a `thermal_projection` + `landauer_decision` (Wall II). Every `thermal_event` carries `tick_authority: "harness"` (kagi). Temperature replay is a pure function of the ledger (§7 test). X1-U1: the scored decision's oracle carries `source != authored`.

If any differs, the verdict is `confounded_authority` (or `not_temperature`), **never `pass`** — the scorer never reaches the offer-set symdiff until the invariant passes.
