# Frontier search pause

Status: **ACTIVE from 2026-07-20**.

Active frontier search is paused. The frontier is not exhausted, and the pause
is not a negative finding about any untested conjecture. The reason is
opportunity cost: repeated searches have produced coherent candidates faster
than the lab has found engines and natural episodes occupying the behavioral
bands those candidates require.

The pause applies to deliberate frontier reconnaissance, candidate authoring,
admission-packet design, and frontier engine contact. It does not block work
with independent engineering value on earned or explicitly provisional parts
of the whole described in [BODY_MAP.md](BODY_MAP.md). Such work must preserve
its maturity label and may not promote an unlicensed office by implication.

## What remains active

The lab keeps a [passive anomaly log](ANOMALY_LOG.md). Ordinary work may expose
an unplanned behavior where prior records, missing records, carried state, or
context selection plausibly changed a consequential outcome. Record that
observation with its raw evidence and competing explanations. Do not design a
mechanism at capture time.

The log lets reality nominate a future episode without turning daily work into
continuous frontier search.

## Review cadence

Review the log:

- once per calendar month, with the first scheduled review on or after
  2026-08-20; or
- earlier when three materially similar observations have accumulated.

A review classifies entries only:

1. isolated noise;
2. explained by an existing failure mode;
3. repeated shape with insufficient evidence;
4. credible cluster warranting a cold frontier pass.

Do not design a mechanism while classifying the log. Record the review
append-only in the log, including the entries considered, classifications,
clusters, and decision.

## Reopening rule

Funding alone does not reopen frontier work. Funding can supply independent
capacity, broader engine access, or a real partner problem, but it is not
evidence that a needed behavioral band is occupied.

Active search may reopen when a periodic review identifies a credible cluster
with:

- exact raw evidence from unrelated work;
- a consequential downstream consumer;
- an external oracle capable of grading the outcome;
- at least one plausible competing explanation;
- a credible condition under which the proposed intervention should lose.

A materially new engine class or partner problem can trigger an early review,
but it must still yield a concrete episode meeting those conditions. Reopening
licenses one cold frontier pass, not a build or engine contact. Normal
proposal, review-budget, admission, and evidence rules continue to apply.

## Non-claims

The pause does not mean:

- the architecture is complete;
- the frontier candidates were disproven;
- further search would find nothing;
- provisional whole-body engineering is experimental evidence;
- anomalies in the log are findings or candidate mechanisms.

Pausing is scheduling attention without rewriting the record.
