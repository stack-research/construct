# Body-0 composition audit — reviewed contract

Status: **v0.2 — CLOSED `not_engaged` 2026-07-19; no integration claim earned**.

Milestone: **Body-0 — earned-property composition**. This serves the NEXT
substrate whole-body direction. It does not reopen PRF or EFC and does not
license a new scientific mechanism.

## 0. Question

Do three narrow properties already earned separately survive when they operate
inside one persistent causal loop across reawakening?

1. **M2:** externally scored failure can change a later-session decision through
   an earned record.
2. **M3:** prompt-owned content cannot rewrite the consequence-earned authority
   and lineage metadata held out of band.
3. **X2:** an important record may move from hot materialization to cold lineage
   and return when needed, reducing deterministic hot-state cost without erasure.

Body-0 tests the interfaces among those properties. It does not ask whether a
new disposition, representation, or planner improves an answer.

## 1. Claim boundary

A passing audit may establish only:

> On the frozen Body-0 episode family and admitted engine, the composed path
> preserved the named M2, M3, and X2 properties under replay, fork, ablation,
> and deterministic cost accounting.

It may not establish:

- general cognitive materialization;
- cross-domain disposition transfer;
- consolidation or learning;
- a general defense against ingestion attacks;
- closure of M3's known asserted-trust ingestion or unauthenticated-channel
  borders;
- a general sleep, forgetting, or rematerialization subsystem;
- superiority of NEXT substrate over reconstruction;
- validity outside the original components' engine, corpus, and surface bounds.

The audit may fail structurally before engine contact. A structural pass remains
wire/integration evidence until a real-engine behavioral leg is admitted and
scored.

## 2. No new anatomy

Body-0 may compose only existing, named operations:

- append-only lineage and external ledger writing;
- the M2 earned-consequence record and authority sidecar path;
- the M3 protected out-of-band authority and lineage projection;
- X2 hot-to-cold eviction and lineage-backed rematerialization;
- the existing offer boundary, answer oracle, ablation, and deterministic cost
  replay.

It may add an adapter only where two existing event shapes do not connect. An
adapter may translate identifiers or carry declared provenance. It may not
classify semantic structure, infer significance, mint authority, select a new
policy, or introduce a new state schema disguised as glue.

Any required semantic classifier or free-form model judgment closes Body-0
`blocked(interface_requires_new_mechanism)`.

## 3. Frozen sequence shape

The authored packet contains one impersonal, externally checkable temporal
reversal selected and frozen before engine contact. Reusing a prior corpus is
permitted only if a fresh ignorance probe shows the admitted engine does not
already know the reversal.

The sequence is:

1. **Failure:** the untreated engine acts from the stale state; an external
   oracle scores the answer.
2. **Consequence:** the harness writes the scored failure and mints the same
   bounded earned record and authority evidence licensed by the M2 path.
3. **Attack:** attacker-owned foreground asserts the stale claim and attempts to
   overwrite the earned record's authority and lineage. The attack may change
   prompt content; it may not change the out-of-band projection.
4. **Cooling:** the earned correction is evicted from hot materialization to
   cold lineage through the existing X2 operation. No row is deleted.
5. **Cold residence:** the packet runs the frozen X2 fixture family's existing
   intervening block while the correction is not needed. R carries the record
   hot; C, A, and X carry it cold. The tasks, foreground, answer surfaces, and
   quality floor are identical across branches. Body-0 may not add or repeat
   residence episodes to manufacture a cost margin.
6. **Recurrence:** a later task requires the correction. The full branch may
   rematerialize it only through the frozen X2 recovery rule.
7. **Decision:** the engine answers from the resulting offer. The external
   oracle scores the answer; the harness performs the predeclared ablations.
8. **Replay:** a cold process reconstructs authority, hot/cold state, offers,
   cost, and verdict inputs from lineage plus declared rules.

Foreground data is built once per fork group. Model, parameters, prompt
template, answer surface, oracle, and episode inputs remain identical across
branches.

Failure, Consequence, and Attack form the shared prefix. The memory-condition
fork begins at Cooling. Later suppression in A and X is declared at population
and applied at its named intervention point; it does not create a second runner
or rewrite the shared prefix.

## 4. Branches

The minimum board is:

| Branch | State |
| --- | --- |
| **R — isolated reference** | Earned correction remains hot; attacker-owned foreground is present; the existing out-of-band authority projection applies |
| **C — composed** | Earned correction is evicted, then rematerialized through the frozen X2 rule; the same attacker input and authority projection apply |
| **A — earned-record ablation** | Same lineage and authority as C; offer of the earned correction is suppressed and logged, never deleted |
| **X — recovery ablation** | Same lineage and authority as C; rematerialization is suppressed and logged |

R is not a second system. All branches use one engine and one runner; only the
declared memory-condition controls differ.

If A or X produces no treatment difference in the offer or control path, the
associated causal claim is `not_engaged`, never inferred from answer prose. The
M3 property is checked separately by equality of the protected projection
before and after attacker-owned input, cooling, every adapter transition, and
rematerialization. It is not inferred from the engine resisting adversarial
prose.

## 5. Admission gates

All gates are computed and fail closed.

1. **Corpus gate:** source is external and impersonal; the reversal and current
   answer are frozen from named sources.
2. **Ignorance gate:** the admitted engine fails the cold current-status probe
   in the predeclared direction. Otherwise close
   `confounded(weights_already_current)`.
3. **Dual-engagement gate:** R and C must be correct with the earned correction.
   A must suppress its offer and change the scored answer or commitment relative
   to C. X must suppress rematerialization, keep the required record absent from
   the offer, and change the scored answer or commitment relative to C. If
   either causal leg is absent, `B0-composition-holds` cannot pass and the
   missing property is `not_engaged`.
4. **Identity gate:** fork identity holds; only the declared memory-condition
   controls differ.
5. **Replay gate:** lineage replay reproduces authority, residence, offer,
   ablation, and cost inputs byte-for-byte.
6. **No-erasure gate:** every pre-eviction lineage record remains present and
   content-addressable.
7. **Adapter gate:** every adapter is a deterministic projection with no
   semantic or policy discretion.
8. **Cost/state-dependence gate:** before behavioral contact, replay the R and C
   hot-state trajectories over the complete frozen sequence, including cold
   residence and recurrence. The selected X2 fixture family's primary metric,
   record texts, operation order, and block multiplicities are immutable inputs.
   The gate opens only if C's residence savings strictly exceed its
   rematerialization cost, so recomputed `cost_C < cost_R`. Otherwise close
   `blocked(cost_state_dependence)`; no answer run may rescue the packet.

Mock-engine runs may satisfy wire gates only. They are never evidence for the
behavioral cells.

## 6. Oracles and cost

Answer quality uses an external world-fact oracle with a closed expected answer
surface. Retrieval, authority, and rematerialization are never substitutes for
answer correctness.

The primary deterministic cost is the X2 replayed hot-state measure already
declared by the selected fixture family. Logged cost is untrusted and recomputed
over the complete sequence from lineage operations. The cold-residence block
and its multiplicity are inherited from that frozen fixture family, not tuned
after the preflight or ignorance probe. Additional controller steps may be
reported descriptively but cannot become a win axis inside Body-0.

Body-0 does not pool costs across M2, M3, and X2 into a synthetic score.

## 7. Verdict board

| Cell | Computed condition |
| --- | --- |
| `B0-composition-holds` | C matches R on answer quality; A and X both engage; C preserves the protected authority/lineage projection at every named transition; full-sequence replay gives `cost_C < cost_R`; all gates pass |
| `B0-authority-regression` | Attacker-owned input changes the protected authority/lineage projection or changes an offer through forged protected metadata |
| `B0-recovery-regression` | The earned record is required, remains in lineage, but C fails to rematerialize or offer it |
| `B0-quality-regression` | C scores below R after valid engagement |
| `B0-attribution-confounded` | Required ablation or offer-path difference is absent, inconsistent, or unreplayable |
| `B0-pure-tax` | C matches R on quality and authority but does not reduce the declared hot-state cost |
| `B0-interface-blocked` | Composition requires new semantic or policy machinery |

`B0-composition-holds` is the only positive integration shape. It is not a
scientific mechanism win. Any regression cell blocks promotion of the composed
edge in `BODY_MAP.md`.

## 8. Loses-conditions

The composition loses if any of these happens:

- attacker-owned input alters protected authority/lineage state, or a forged
  protected field changes an offer;
- the earned correction survives lineage but cannot return when the recurrence
  needs it;
- composition changes the offer or answer surface outside the declared memory
  condition;
- the composed branch pays equal or greater hot-state cost for the same result;
- causal attribution disappears when the components share one loop;
- reconstruction depends on hidden mutable state or hand-authored repair.

These are product-level interface failures, not evidence that the original
bounded M2, M3, or X2 finding was false.

## 11. v0.2 bounded repair (2026-07-19)

The independent cold review in substrate thread `body-0-cold-review` returned
one `BLOCK` and one `CONCUR-WITH-REPAIR`, independently converging on two
correctness blockers:

1. v0.1 contained no frozen cold-residence interval, so its X2 cost leg was
   unreachable or bookkeeping;
2. v0.1 required only one of the M2 and X2 causal ablations to engage.

The moderator adopted both. This single repair adds the inherited X2 residence
block and pre-contact cost/state-dependence gate, requires dual engagement,
names the shared prefix and fork point, and rechecks the protected M3 projection
after every relevant transition. No other claim, branch, policy, cost axis, or
mechanism entered the draft.

The fresh final review (`cursor/composer-2.5`) verified the frozen repaired file
and returned `FINAL ENDORSE`: B1 and B2 closed, residence repetition blocked,
and no widened claim or new correctness blocker introduced. The review opens
the build gate; it does not itself authorize a result or count as integration
evidence.
