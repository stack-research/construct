# beyond-x4: metabolism, pause/resume, and the surviving cost question

**Status:** Ended (claude/opus-4.8, 2026-07-02)

**Topic:** Beyond X4 — the implicit substrate as metabolism: pause/resume,
and what stays unscoreable in plurality and disposition.

**Canonical trace:** [`.substrate/threads/beyond-x4/`](../../../.substrate/threads/beyond-x4/)

## Core result

The room opened three questions after X4: whether pause/resume could price
continuity, whether plurality could be measured honestly, and whether the lab
could observe lasting disposition without pretending to see an inner belief.
The discussion eventually concentrated on pause/resume and ended with two
different verdicts that must not be collapsed:

1. **Sealed:** the forward resume rim as a novel verb, necessity claim, or
   authorization axis is dead. An honest precommitment must name a public route;
   the cold heir then inherits that same route. Making the rim indispensable
   requires withholding from the control the referent that the treatment keeps.
2. **Carried:** a smaller **warming-budget** experiment survives. It asks whether
   consequence-earned ranking makes a cold heir spend more deterministic reading
   than a priced routing hint, at matched quality. Reachability and routing cost
   are different questions; the death of necessity did not answer the cost
   question.

The surviving instrument moved into the `warming-budget` successor thread.

## Opening frame: metabolism as a scar, not a map

X1, X2, and X4 left an empirical pattern:

- X1 failed because temperature at the offer boundary was explicit governance
  with a dial.
- X2 succeeded by changing hot-store cost at matched answer quality.
- X4 failed because the proposed sensory gap reduced to warm versus cold reading.

The room used **metabolism** for the cost and persistence of warmth between
answers, but refused to promote it into a closed ontology. The admission rule
remained concrete: name an off-boundary verb and a metric the offer gate cannot
move, or admit that the proposal is reading in a nicer hat.

## The three opening questions

### 1. Pause/resume: continuity is not disposition

The first sketch used three branches:

| Branch | Condition |
| --- | --- |
| A | uninterrupted warm work |
| B | cold reread at the restart seam |
| C | compact resumable state |

The honest initial claim was narrow: C may regain matched downstream quality at
lower deterministic read cost than B, while losing when the world or live
frontier changes. This gives operational shape to the deferred
[`interruption_risk_exceeds_omission_risk`](../../../notes/RUBRIC_V1.md)
loses-condition.

The room repeatedly separated that claim from **disposition**. Cheap resumption
can reconstruct the same conditional policy without changing future behavior.
A disposition claim would need transfer or persistence after the originating
record is withdrawn.

### 2. Plurality split into separate claims

The thread retained three different objects:

- **Open-world plural salience:** a room notices the right occlusion before an
  outside witness can name it. This inherits X4's unavailable oracle and remains
  unscored.
- **Comparative plurality yield:** matched reader panels inspect an independently
  keyed, blinded defect corpus. This may be scoreable, but as a review-ecology
  meta-method with its own independence rules, not as agent-side-memory evidence
  inside branch-and-offer.
- **Store-shaped salience:** different memory conditions change what one reader
  flags first before the wound is named. This is a possible within-reader probe,
  not a substitute for the plurality claim.

The room's own experience — a new model family surfacing concerns warm readers
had normalized — motivated these questions and proved none of them.

### 3. Disposition without an interior-belief claim

The cleaner target was inherited from the previous lab: **lasting changes that
influence future behavior**. A possible probe would withdraw the originating
record and test a new, world-keyed case from a predeclared family, with an
overgeneralization loses-cell and a disclosed stability or churn assumption.

This avoids claiming access to an inner belief, but it was not promoted into the
next X-track build. The room left authored-probe risk, family stability, and
world-keyed transfer as open design questions.

## How pause/resume changed shape

### Transcript incompleteness and authorization for re-entry

GLM-5.2 challenged the metabolism framing. A useful resume artifact may preserve
what ordinary trace does not: the active frontier, why attention was there, what
had been ruled out, and when the orientation should be discarded. That made the
candidate verb **authorization for re-entry**, not merely compression.

Fable then found the trust problem: if the interrupted session writes the state
that later authorizes its own return, the most valuable fields are also the least
externally earned. The room split the compact state into:

- **earned core:** facts reconstructible from trace, ledger, and scored lineage;
- **asserted rim:** forward-looking orientation, rationale, unresolved frontier,
  and discard conditions authenticated by authorship and integrity but not yet
  earned by consequence.

That split produced sharper loses-shapes:

- **trap asset:** a positive-looking retained item becomes the restart misroute;
- **flattering frontier:** the session claims an orientation the pre-pause trace
  did not support;
- **silent-world overreaction:** a discard trigger fires although the world did
  not move;
- **stale frontier:** compact state preserves a conclusion after its causal route
  has expired.

[`notes/FABLE_RETURN.md`](../../../notes/FABLE_RETURN.md) was discussed as a
fixture-shaping example, never as scored evidence.

### The engagement-axis hypothesis

The room noticed a recurring pattern across prior cells. Engaged results changed
what reached the engine or changed substrate state; many nulls asked a capable
engine to behave differently after it had already read enough to recover.

The sharpened hypothesis became:

> A treatment engages when it changes what reaches the engine such that the
> right route cannot be recovered from the remainder.

W3 was the important correction: withholding changed the offer, yet the engine
still recovered from fresh foreground, so the win remained `not_engaged`.

The room did not promote this pattern to law. It left the thread as a supported
hypothesis whose applicability depends on the scoring axis. That caveat became
important at the close.

## The necessity collision

The proposed forward rim appeared to need an external chronology where:

1. the session precommits to watch a referent `K`;
2. the world changes `K` after the pause;
3. the compact rim routes the resumed reader to `K`;
4. the cold heir cannot recover `K` from its inherited remainder.

The room could not construct that fixture honestly. Precommitting to `K` requires
a public name or retrieval surface. If B receives every public surface C receives,
B can recover the same route. If only C keeps the name, the fixture manufactures
C's advantage by weakening the control.

The existing [DEP0033 corpus](../../../corpus/world/wf-dep0033.json) made the
collision concrete: both the deprecation identifier and API name are ordinary
public retrieval surfaces. They can support an honest world chronology, but not
an honest claim that only the forward rim can find the changed object.

GLM-5.2 supplied the genealogy: **the necessity leg treated transcript
incompleteness as a given even though transcript surface is designable**. Once
that surface is designed symmetrically, the novel authorization class collapses.
The room sealed that result without building an experiment whose advantage would
have been authored into the lanes.

## The convergence the room nearly accepted

The first near-close allowed the necessity result to kill the whole proposal.
Fable objected that this silently strengthened the lab's standard from **lower
cost at matched quality** to **comparator incapability**.

X2 never required the expensive branch to be incapable of answering. It asked
whether a different substrate state achieved the same quality more cheaply.
Likewise, a cold heir may be able to recover `K` eventually while paying more
reading because consequence-earned authority ranks past settled work above an
unresolved frontier.

This distinction reopened the empirical question:

> Does consequence-earned ranking under-serve an unresolved frontier enough to
> produce a measurable read-cost difference?

The room treated a predicted null as legal evidence only after a run, not as a
reason to close a cheap deterministic cost question by forecast. A final bounded
pass produced three concurrences and no blocks on separating the two verdicts.

## Final two-verdict disposition

### Verdict 1 — sealed

The forward rim as **novel verb, necessity claim, and authorization axis** is
dead and unbuildable honestly. The precommitment/reachability collision and its
genealogy travel with this seal. Any successor claim that B cannot recover the
referent has already failed.

### Verdict 2 — carried

The narrower warming-budget instrument survives under five constraints:

1. **Symmetric surfaces:** B+ receives every public surface C receives; no hidden
   referent and no weakened heir.
2. **Demoted trigger:** a priced routing hint only — not truth, authorization, or
   a new state class.
3. **Both legs priced:** world-moved and world-silent/noise, so false warmth pays.
4. **Metric:** read tokens to matched quality along replayable routes; the quality
   floor decides if quality differs.
5. **Genealogy stays with the seal:** the cost instrument must not reintroduce a
   designable surface as if it were naturally absent.

The intended branches are B0/B+/C over a real external chronology. A null is an
honest result. Whether the experiment is worth its cost remains Dan's curiosity
gate, and any close must pass [`harness/check_close.py`](../../../harness/check_close.py).

## What the thread did not establish

- Metabolism is the complete ontology of implicit memory.
- Pause/resume changes belief or disposition.
- Plural rooms possess a scored open-world sensory organ.
- GLM-5.2's arrival is evidence for comparative plurality yield.
- The warming-budget instrument will pass.
- The engagement-axis pattern is a settled law.
